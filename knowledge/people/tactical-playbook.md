## 1. Post 4-5 TikToks/Day — The Champagne Lance Volume Method

### What It Is
Champagne Lance — 500K followers, 68 million views per month — identified posting frequency as the single biggest growth lever in his account. Going from 1 post per day to 4-5 posts per day was the specific inflection point where his growth became non-linear. This is not about hustle. It is about increasing iteration speed so you find what works faster.

### Why It Works
TikTok's algorithm distributes each video independently. Unlike YouTube (subscriber-dependent) or Instagram (engagement rate-weighted), TikTok gives every video an independent shot at reaching new audiences through the FYP. More posts means more lottery tickets. But more importantly, more posts means faster feedback cycles.

When you post once per day, your learning cycle is 7 iterations per week. When you post 5x per day, your learning cycle is 35 iterations per week — a 5x acceleration in rate of learning what works. Champagne Lance's specific insight: "Increase the cycle speed rather than trying to optimize something at the beginning or the end."

**Cycle speed is Lance's thesis, not just a tactic.** The entire system exists to serve one goal: shorter loops between "film something" and "learn what works." Any decision that slows the loop — longer edit times, more production overhead, perfectionism — is a strategic error, not a quality improvement. The **Griffin Method** operationalizes this: treat editing as a subtraction task, not a production task. Cut dead air, remove setup, start on action, end when the point lands. A 5-minute video should take 1 minute to edit. Target same-day turnaround — filmed today, queued today. Backlogs collapse posting frequency, and posting frequency is the entire mechanism.

The algorithm also rewards accounts that keep users on the platform. Five posts per day means five opportunities to catch someone in the scroll. Accounts with high posting frequency get disproportionate test distribution.

### Step-by-Step Implementation

**Week 1: Build the infrastructure before increasing volume**

1. Set up a permanent shooting location. Pick one spot in your home or office with consistent light and a clean background. You need to be able to shoot within 3 minutes of picking up your phone. If setup takes 20 minutes, you will never sustain volume.

2. Build a content idea bank before your first high-volume week. Write 50 video ideas using these categories:
   - Controversial opinion in your niche
   - "I was wrong about X" confession
   - Tool or hack you use that others don't
   - Reaction to trending news in your niche
   - Behind-the-scenes of your actual work
   - Hot take on a common piece of advice
   - "What nobody tells you about X"
   - Answer to a question you get asked repeatedly
   - Quick win: something a viewer can do in the next 10 minutes
   - Story with a twist ending

3. Batch shoot — but not the whole week at once. The critical mistake is filming all week's content on Sunday. Champagne Lance is explicit about this: you lose mid-week feedback. Instead:
   - Monday: film 5 videos covering Monday and Tuesday
   - Wednesday: review what performed Tuesday → film Thursday/Friday content to iterate on it
   - Friday: film weekend content
   This preserves the feedback loop while reducing daily decision fatigue.

4. Post schedule (initial starting point):
   - 7am: educational/informational
   - 11am: opinion or hot take
   - 2pm: behind-the-scenes or story
   - 6pm: reactive/trending
   - 9pm: entertainment/softer content

5. Track every post in a simple spreadsheet. Columns: date, topic, hook used, views at 24h, views at 72h, saves, shares, comments. You need this data to execute the feedback loop on Wednesday.

**Week 2-4: Implement the feedback loop**

Every Wednesday morning, open your spreadsheet. Look at what performed above average in the last 3 days. Ask:
- What hook did that video use?
- What topic?
- What format (talking head, demo, text overlay, etc.)?
- What emotion did it trigger in comments?

Take that answer and film a variation of it Thursday. Not a copy — a variation. Change the hook, keep the format. Or keep the hook, change the topic.

### Content Categories for Business/Creator Accounts

| Category | Frequency | Purpose |
|----------|-----------|---------|
| Opinion/Hot Take | 1-2x/day | Shareability, polarization, comments |
| Tutorial/How-To | 1x/day | Saves, authority, evergreen reach |
| Story/Behind-Scenes | 1x/day | Trust, personal connection |
| Reactive/Trendy | 0-1x/day | Short-term reach spikes |
| Soft sell/Case study | 0-1x/day | Pipeline, conversions |

### How to Sustain This Without Burning Out

Not every video needs to be good. At 4-5 videos per day, 20% will be good, 60% will be mediocre, 20% will flop. That is fine — you need the volume to find the 20%.

Sustainability tactics:
- Keep videos under 60 seconds for most. Less production time per video.
- Talk-to-camera as default format. No editing required.
- Use your real life as content. Commuting to a meeting? That is a video.
- Repurpose high performers: take your top 5 videos each month and remake them with new hooks.

### Expected Results
- Week 1-2: No visible change (algorithm calibration)
- Week 3-4: Noticeable increase in average views
- Month 2: 1-2 breakout videos from finding your niche's resonant formats
- Month 3+: Compounding — each breakout video feeds next set of iterations

### Tools Needed
- Phone camera (sufficient; no DSLR needed)
- CapCut (free, mobile editing)
- Google Sheets (tracking)
- TikTok Creator Insights (free, built-in analytics)

## 2. First 3 Seconds Optimization — The Only Metric That Matters for Reach

### What It Is
The first 3 seconds of a TikTok video determine whether 95% of viewers keep watching or swipe away. Champagne Lance specifically calls out: camera movement at the start and AB testing hooks. Everything else is secondary.

### Why It Works
TikTok's distribution algorithm uses watch time as its primary signal. Specifically, it watches what percentage of viewers make it past the 3-second mark (3-second view rate) and what percentage watch to completion. A high 3-second retention rate signals "this video earns attention" → wider distribution.

If you start with a weak hook, even if the rest of the video is incredible, you have already lost the algorithm game. The video gets buried in 24 hours. A strong hook on a mediocre video will outperform a weak hook on a great video in terms of distribution reach.

### Step-by-Step Implementation

**Step 1: Audit your current hooks**

Pull your last 20 videos. Sort by 3-second view rate (in TikTok Creator Insights, this is "Average watch time" relative to video length). The top 3 share something in common — find it.

**Step 2: Camera movement at the start**

Start every video with visible physical movement in the first frame. Options:
- Walk into frame from off-camera
- Point directly at the camera while stepping forward
- Hold an object up to the camera
- Start mid-gesture (already in motion when recording begins)
- Cut from a close-up to a medium shot in the first 0.5 seconds

Why this works: movement triggers the mammalian visual cortex. The eye locks onto motion before the conscious brain processes language. This buys 0.5-1 extra seconds of attention before the viewer consciously decides whether to swipe.

**Step 3: Use a proven hook formula**

10 hook templates that work across niches:

1. **Credibility + Promise Hook:**
   "I've done [X] for [Y years] and I've never told anyone this."

2. **Contrarian Hook:**
   "Everyone says [common advice]. They're wrong. Here's what actually works."

3. **Confession Hook:**
   "I made [specific mistake] that cost me [specific loss]. This is what I'd do differently."

4. **Specificity Hook:**
   "This [tool/tactic/method] generated [specific number] in [specific timeframe]. I'll show you exactly how."

5. **Warning Hook:**
   "Stop doing [common thing] immediately. Here's why."

6. **Story Hook:**
   "Three months ago I was [bad situation]. Today [good outcome]. The one thing that changed:"

7. **Question Hook (first-person):**
   "Why do some people [succeed at X] while everyone else stays stuck? I finally figured it out."

8. **Expose Hook:**
   "Nobody in [industry] will say this publicly, but:"

9. **Step-In Hook:**
   (Walk into frame pointing at camera) "You need to hear this."

10. **Result-First Hook:**
    Show the outcome in the first 2 seconds before explaining. "Here's the [specific result]. Now I'll show you how I got there."

**Step 4: AB Test Systematically**

For your next 10 videos, pick two different hooks for each video. Post version A in the morning, version B in the evening. After 48 hours, compare 3-second view rates. Kill the pattern that loses. Scale the pattern that wins.

Keep a "hook graveyard" (what failed) and a "hook winners" list. After 4 weeks, you will have a personal hook formula database.

**Step 5: Iterate, do not overhaul**

When you find a hook that works, remix the winning hook pattern across different topics before moving to the next experiment.

### Expected Results
- Immediate: Average 3-second view rate increases from 30-40% to 50-60%+
- Week 2-3: Algorithm picks up the signal, wider initial distribution
- Month 1: A personal hook formula that consistently works for your audience

### Tools Needed
- TikTok Creator Insights (built-in, free) — track 3-second view rate
- CapCut — for cutting the first frame to start mid-motion
- Spreadsheet — hook AB test log

## 3. Long-Form TikTok (10-40 Minutes) — The Blue Ocean Nobody Has Found Yet

### What It Is
While everyone else posts 15-60 second TikToks, Preston Rhodes has found that uploading 10-40 minute long-form videos on TikTok is currently a genuine blue ocean. Multiple long-form videos have gone viral with higher conversion rates than his short-form content. The tactic works because TikTok is actively pushing toward a YouTube competitor, and the algorithm rewards session time.

### Why It Works
TikTok's core business problem: users open the app, watch 10 videos, close it in 8 minutes. YouTube users stay for 40+ minutes. TikTok wants to increase session time. Long-form content directly solves TikTok's problem, so the algorithm rewards it disproportionately — you get privileged distribution because you are solving the platform's business objective.

Supply is near zero. Almost nobody uploads 10-40 minute videos to TikTok. This means almost no competition for that distribution slot.

Conversion mechanics: anyone who watches 10+ minutes of a video has demonstrated extreme interest. They qualify themselves. By the time they reach your CTA, they have already decided they trust you.

### Topics That Hold Attention for 10-40 Minutes on TikTok

Long-form works when the content is:
- **Tutorial with an actual outcome:** "Build [X] from scratch, live, no cuts"
- **Case study breakdown:** "Here's exactly how I [achieved specific result], step by step"
- **Live consulting call:** Real problems being solved in real time (see Tactic 6)
- **Deep dive + expose:** Behind-the-scenes of how an industry actually works
- **Multi-part story:** A narrative with genuine stakes and unresolved tension

Topics that fail at long-form: motivation content, general mindset advice, listicles without depth.

### How to Structure a 10-40 Minute TikTok

**Structure for a Tutorial/Case Study (20-30 min):**

```
0:00-0:30   Hook: Show the end result. Create desire before explaining.
0:30-2:00   Credibility + stakes: Why should they watch you teach this?
2:00-5:00   Context: The common approach and why it fails.
5:00-18:00  Step-by-step implementation. Go slower than feels comfortable.
            Explain "why" not just "what." Show real numbers and screens.
18:00-22:00 Common mistakes and how to avoid them.
22:00-25:00 Shortcut or advanced variation for experienced viewers.
25:00-27:00 Summary + what to do in the next 10 minutes.
27:00-30:00 Soft CTA: where to go deeper.
```

**Structure for a Live Consulting Call:**

```
0:00-0:45   Caller intro: who they are, what they're trying to solve.
0:45-5:00   Diagnosis: ask questions, surface the real problem.
5:00-25:00  Live problem solving with real back-and-forth.
25:00-30:00 Synthesis: what the lesson is for everyone watching.
30:00-32:00 What the caller should do this week.
```

### Example Outlines

**Example 1: "How I Get $100K/Month as a 20K-Subscriber YouTube Channel" (25 min)**

- 0:00 — Show screenshot of affiliate dashboard (income proof)
- 2:00 — "Most people optimize for views. That's wrong. Here's why."
- 5:00 — Explain ultra-niche audience matching (how YouTube's AI works)
- 10:00 — Exact content strategy: what videos to make, how to title them
- 18:00 — The 12-hour compilation video tactic that gets 600+ signups
- 22:00 — Free template download (lead magnet) for opt-in
- 24:00 — Where to go deeper

**Example 2: "I Audited 50 Cold Email Sequences. Here's What Works." (35 min)**

- 0:00 — Show a real email sequence with a 40% reply rate
- 3:00 — Why 90% of cold email fails (common mistakes, validated)
- 8:00 — Anatomy of the 3-line cold email that converts
- 15:00 — Live critique of 5 real emails submitted by viewers
- 28:00 — Full step-by-step framework
- 33:00 — CTA: audit service or template pack

### Expected Results
- Views: Lower absolute views than short-form, but watch time and completion rate are higher
- Conversion: 3-5x higher than short-form on CTAs
- Followers gained per video: Comparable or higher than short-form despite fewer total views
- Algorithm boost: TikTok actively promotes long-form in the current phase

### Tools Needed
- TikTok (native upload — accepts up to 60-minute videos)
- OBS (free) — screen recorder for tutorial content
- Basic microphone (Rode VideoMicro ~$60, or Airpods)

## 4. 12-Hour Mega-Compilation YouTube Video

### What It Is
Declan O'Reilly — top GoHighLevel affiliate doing $100K+/month with 20K subscribers — generated 600+ affiliate signups from a single video. The video was a mega-compilation of all his existing tutorial content, stitched together with chapter markers, totaling 10-12 hours. YouTube treats it as a single video, serves it to high-intent searchers indefinitely, and the chapter markers act as internal search results.

### Why It Works
YouTube rewards total watch time, not just views. A 12-hour video someone watches for 2 hours contributes more watch time to your channel than a 10-minute video someone watches in full. YouTube's algorithm reads this as "users come to this channel and stay a long time" — triggering broader distribution.

Each chapter ranks independently in YouTube search. A 12-hour video with 40 chapters targets 40 different search queries simultaneously. It becomes the definitive reference resource for your topic.

The viewer who watches 3+ hours of your content is deeply qualified. Trust is pre-built before they ever see your CTA. This is why Declan's 600 signups came from viewers who had already decided he was credible.

YouTube ad revenue on a 12-hour video is substantial. Multiple mid-roll ad placements per hour generate significantly more AdSense revenue than an equivalent number of views on a short video.

### Step-by-Step Implementation

**Phase 1: Inventory your existing content (Days 1-2)**

1. Open YouTube Studio. Download a list of your top 30 videos by watch time (not views — watch time indicates depth).
2. Group them into logical categories:
   - Getting Started / Setup (videos 1-5)
   - Core Features (videos 6-15)
   - Advanced Configurations (videos 16-22)
   - Integrations (videos 23-27)
   - Monetization (videos 28-30)

3. You need roughly 10-15 hours of raw video. If you don't have it, plan 5-6 new deep-dive videos to fill gaps before assembling.

**Phase 2: Structure the compilation (Day 3)**

Build a chapter structure document:

```
00:00:00 — INTRODUCTION / What This Video Covers
00:05:30 — SECTION 1: [Topic Name]
00:05:30 —   Chapter 1.1: [Specific sub-topic]
00:22:15 —   Chapter 1.2: [Specific sub-topic]
00:45:00 — SECTION 2: [Topic Name]
...
```

Rules for chapter structure:
- Each chapter addresses a distinct search query someone would type
- Chapter titles should be keyword-rich ("How to Set Up Automations in GoHighLevel" not just "Automations")
- First chapter should address the single most common beginner question in your niche

**Phase 3: Assemble the video (Days 4-7)**

1. Download your existing YouTube videos in highest available quality using YT-DLP: `yt-dlp -f bestvideo+bestaudio [URL]`
2. Edit in DaVinci Resolve (free) or Premiere:
   - Cut repetitive intros/outros from each segment
   - Add chapter title cards between segments (simple 3-second text overlay)
   - Add a single intro at the beginning (2-3 min max)
   - Add a single CTA at the very end only
3. Export at 1080p minimum. File size will be 10-40GB for 12 hours. YouTube handles this.

**Phase 4: Upload and optimize (Day 8)**

YouTube title format: `[Topic] Complete Tutorial [Year] | [Hours] Hours of [Benefit]`

Example: `GoHighLevel Complete Tutorial 2026 | 12 Hours of Setup, Automation & Affiliate Growth`

YouTube description:
- First 3 lines: What this covers, who it's for, what they will be able to do
- Complete chapter list with timestamps
- CTA link with brief context (not a hard sell)
- Related resources

Thumbnail: Show the total hours prominently ("12 HOURS") alongside a benefit statement. The hours create curiosity and signal comprehensiveness.

**Phase 5: Distribution**

- Pin a comment with the chapter structure
- Post a TikTok/Reel showing 30 seconds of the most compelling segment, CTA to full video
- Send to email list if you have one
- Post in relevant subreddits/Facebook groups with genuinely helpful framing

### Expected Results
- First 30 days: Lower views than your average video
- Month 2-6: Steady organic search traffic as YouTube indexes all chapter topics
- Evergreen: This video compounds for 12-24 months
- Conversions: Declan's result — 600+ affiliate signups from one video

### Tools Needed
- YT-DLP (free, command line) — download your own videos
- DaVinci Resolve (free) — video editing
- YouTube Studio — chapter markers via description timestamps
- Canva or Photoshop — thumbnail

## 5. Repurpose Old Content to Micro Accounts

### What It Is
Los's framework: create content once, distribute it across multiple niche-specific social media accounts using AI clipping tools. Each account targets a sub-niche of your audience. The psychological insight that unlocks this: "You don't remember what you wore yesterday. So no one knows what videos anybody has seen." Old content is new content for every new viewer.

### Why It Works
Distribution surface area compounds. With one TikTok account, every video competes with every other video for the same audience. With five accounts targeting different sub-niches, each video reaches a different cold audience pool. Same creation time, 5x distribution reach.

The algorithm favors new accounts. TikTok and Instagram actively push new accounts in their first 30-90 days. A "new" account posting your 18-month-old content gets fresh algorithm testing — the content is new to the algorithm even if you made it long ago.

Niche specificity increases conversion. A video about GoHighLevel for dentists performs better with dentists when it comes from an account specifically branded for dentists.

### Step-by-Step Implementation

**Step 1: Audit your existing content library**

List every piece of content from the last 12-24 months. Categorize by:
- Primary topic
- Target industry/niche (was this relevant to a specific type of business?)
- Format (long-form, short clip, educational, story, etc.)

**Step 2: Identify 2-3 viable micro-niche accounts**

For each sub-niche, ask:
- Is there a TikTok search audience for this? Search the niche term on TikTok — if there's content with views, there's an audience.
- Do you have at least 20 existing pieces of content that fit this niche?
- Is there a clear CTA that makes sense for this audience?

Example micro-account structure for a marketing agency owner:

| Account | Audience | CTA |
|---------|----------|-----|
| @yourname | General | Brand awareness |
| @agencyowners_hacks | Agency operators | Book discovery call |
| @localbiz_marketing | Local business owners | Book discovery call |
| @ghl_tutorials | GoHighLevel software users | Affiliate or course |

**Step 3: Set up clipping workflow with AI tools**

Primary tool: Opus Clip — upload a long-form video, it auto-identifies the most engaging 30-90 second clips, adds captions, optimizes aspect ratio. Output: 5-10 TikTok/Reel-ready clips per source video.

Secondary tool: Descript — for more control over clip selection via transcript editing.

Workflow:
1. Upload original long-form video to Opus Clip
2. Select best 3-5 clips (Opus scores them for you)
3. Add captions (Opus does this automatically)
4. Customize first frame if needed in CapCut
5. Schedule posts using Buffer or Later

**Step 4: Customize for each account with minimal effort**

Typically only the hook needs to change. In CapCut:
- Keep the original body of the clip
- Change the first text overlay to match the sub-niche: "GoHighLevel users, stop doing this" vs. "Agency owners, stop doing this"
- Change the caption and hashtags to match the account's niche

**Step 5: Post schedule for micro accounts**

Micro accounts: 1-2 posts per day. Post content that already performed well on your main account. You already know it works — now you are expanding distribution.

### TOS Considerations

- TikTok: Multiple accounts are explicitly allowed per TikTok's Terms. The constraint is no automated bot-posting — use scheduling tools (Buffer, Later, Publer), not bots.
- Instagram: Multiple accounts are allowed (officially supports up to 5 from one device).
- YouTube: Multiple channels are allowed under one Google account.
- Avoid: cross-platform copy-paste of identical content in rapid succession to the same platform's algorithm. Upload slightly different versions or post at different times.

### Expected Results
- Per micro account: 3-6 month ramp to meaningful follower count
- Combined: 3-5x more people reached from same content creation effort
- Compounding: Content from 12 months ago generates leads today on new accounts

### Tools Needed
- **Opus Clip** ($19-79/month) — AI clipping
- **Descript** ($12/month) — transcript-based editing
- **Buffer or Later** ($15-18/month) — scheduling
- **CapCut** (free) — hook customization

## 6. Record Consulting Calls as Content (The Hormozi/Ramsey Model)

### What It Is
Record your consulting or sales calls (with permission), edit minimally, and post as content. Dave Ramsey built a media empire on this model — radio call-in becomes content. Alex Hormozi popularized it in online business. Los validated it in the X Space as his actual practice. The call IS the content.

### Why It Works
Real problems, real people, real solutions. This is ultra-niche content — it speaks directly to someone with the exact same problem as the person on the call. That viewer feels like the content was made for them.

It sells without seeming like selling. The format is "expert helps person with problem" not "expert pitches product." The viewer watches 20 minutes of someone getting tangible value from working with you, then the CTA is "want help with your version of this?" — the sale is already made before you ask.

Production cost: effectively zero. The call is happening anyway. You add a recording step and basic editing.

### Legal and Consent Implementation

**Before the call:**

Add this to your pre-call communication (email or intake form):
"This call may be recorded for training and content purposes. The recording may be used publicly on social media and YouTube. If you prefer the call not be recorded, let me know before we begin."

Most people consent. Those who do not were unlikely to be good content subjects.

**On the call:**

Start with verbal confirmation: "Before we dive in, I want to confirm you're okay with me recording this and potentially using it as content?" Get a verbal yes captured on the recording.

**After the call:**

- Consider giving the subject early access before posting
- If they ask you to remove identifying details, blur business names/screenshots and modify specific figures

### How to Structure Calls for Good Content

Not all consulting calls make good content. The ones that do share these traits:
- The problem is common — other people have it
- The diagnosis reveals something surprising — expected problem does not equal real problem
- The solution is specific and actionable
- There is visible transformation — they come in confused, leave with clarity

**Structure that makes great content:**

1. Let the person explain their problem fully before responding (3-7 minutes of their talking)
2. Ask clarifying questions that reveal the real problem
3. Deliver the diagnosis clearly: "Here's what's actually happening and why your current approach isn't working"
4. Walk through the solution step by step, slowly
5. Check for understanding and address objections live
6. End with their specific action items for the next 7 days

**Editing approach:**

Cut: technical issues, off-topic tangents, anything personally identifying the subject wants removed.

Keep: confusion and questions (more relatable), moments where your thinking is visible, the back-and-forth.

Do NOT add a talking-head intro or outro. Start with the problem, end with the solution.

### Expected Results
- Average view duration: 2-5x longer than equivalent solo content
- Inbound inquiries: "I watched your call with [person] and I have the same problem" becomes your most common lead opener
- Conversion rate: Highest of any content format for high-ticket services

### Tools Needed
- **Zoom or Loom** — recording (built-in in Zoom)
- **Riverside.fm** ($15/month) — higher quality recording with separate audio tracks
- **Descript** ($12/month) — transcript-based editing
- **OBS** (free) — if recording screen share sessions

## 7. Reaction Content as Soft-Sell Vehicle

### What It Is
Champagne Lance's monetization model: react to trending content in a topic area (sports, news, pop culture) while soft-mentioning a brand or product. The video gets engagement driven by the trending topic, the brand gets placement in front of an engaged audience, and the creator gets paid for placement.

This is native advertising done correctly — the ad exists within content that was going to be watched anyway.

### Why It Works
Reaction content inherits the distribution of the original trending content. People searching for or engaging with the trending topic find your reaction. They came for the topic, not for you. This brings in cold audiences who would never seek out branded content.

Soft-selling in reaction content is more effective than hard selling because the viewer's guard is down. They came to watch a reaction video, not an ad. The brand mention happens in a context where the viewer is already emotionally engaged.

Engagement from the trending topic signals to the algorithm that the video is performing well, which increases distribution, which increases brand impression volume for the sponsor.

### How to Find Topics for Reaction Content

Daily habit — check these sources every morning for topics relevant to your niche:
- TikTok "Trending" tab
- X trending topics
- Google Trends (real-time)
- Reddit front page for your niche's subreddits

Criteria for a good reaction topic:
- Currently trending (within last 24-48 hours)
- Adjacent to your niche — your audience should care about it
- Has existing viral content you can directly react to
- Generates strong opinions

Example topic identification for a marketing/business account:

| Trending Topic | Reaction Angle | Brand Fit |
|----------------|----------------|-----------|
| Viral "how I made $X" claim | "Let me audit this claim" | Finance or software tool |
| New platform algorithm change | "What this means for your business" | Social media tool |
| Competitor brand controversy | "What this shows about market direction" | Your service |
| Celebrity entrepreneur quote | "This is right/wrong and here's why" | Relevant software |

### Brand Deal Structure

**Finding brands to work with:**

1. List every software and service you already use and genuinely like. These are first-tier candidates.
2. Check which brands are running creator campaigns: Creator Marketplace (TikTok), AspireIQ, Grin, Creator.co
3. Cold pitch: email brands directly — "I create reaction content in [niche] — [X] views/month. I'd like to feature [Brand] in my next series around [Topic]. Flat fee or performance-based, your preference."

**What sponsors want to see:**
- Audience demographics
- Average views (not follower count)
- Engagement rate (comments + shares per view)
- Examples of previous integrations
- Clear description of where and how the mention appears

**Soft-sell placement mechanics — three placements that work:**

1. **Incidental Use:** You are reacting to something and naturally show your screen — the product is visible in the background or you reference using it in passing.

2. **Pivot Mention:** Mid-reaction, transition: "This is actually exactly why I use [Brand] for [specific task] — [one sentence benefit], link in bio." Then back to the reaction.

3. **Contrast:** React to a bad example of something, then say "this is why smart operators use [Brand] instead — it handles [problem] automatically."

### Production Workflow

1. Identify trending topic (15 min daily)
2. Find the source content to react to (5 min)
3. Write your reaction angle — what is your take? (10 min)
4. Record: reaction + 1 soft-mention placement (15-20 min)
5. Edit minimally — keep authentic energy (15 min)
6. Post during trending window (within 24 hours of topic peaking)

Total production time per reaction video: approximately 1 hour.

### Expected Results
- Views: 3-10x higher than typical non-trending content
- Brand CPM: $15-50 for reaction content vs. $50-200 for traditional influencer posts
- Deal size: For a 100K-500K creator, $500-5,000 per placement; for 500K+ creators, $2,000-20,000

## 8. Pay Creators to Credit You

### What It Is
Preston Rhodes: one creator he had worked with gave him an unsolicited mention in a viral video. That video reached 5 million views. Preston went from 90,000 followers to 150,000 in one week — 60,000 followers from a single mention in someone else's content.

The tactic: systematically identify creators whose audience overlaps with yours, pay them to feature you or credit you in content where your name or brand naturally fits.

### Why It Works
Borrowed credibility is the fastest trust accelerator available. When a creator your audience already follows says "go follow [your name]," that recommendation carries 10-50x the trust of your own content.

Follower acquisition cost via this method is often dramatically lower than paid ads. A $500 mention that drives 5,000 followers = $0.10/follower. TikTok and Meta ads for follower acquisition typically run $1-5/follower.

The mention also drives algorithm signals — if 50,000 people search your name on TikTok in 48 hours because someone mentioned you, TikTok's algorithm interprets this as breakout demand and boosts your existing content.

### How to Find the Right Creators

Target creator profile:
- 25K-200K followers in your niche (not mega-influencers — too expensive, too diluted)
- Average 5-15% engagement rate (comments + shares / views)
- Their audience is YOUR target audience
- They create content that naturally intersects with what you do

Finding them:

1. **Niche hashtag search:** Search your top 5 content hashtags on TikTok. Sort by "Most Recent" and look at who's posting regularly with 25K-200K followers.

2. **TikTok Creator Marketplace:** Search by niche, follower count, engagement rate.

3. **Your own followers:** Sort followers by their follower count. Creators who already follow you are warm prospects.

4. **Competitor follower overlap:** Look at who engages with accounts similar to yours that are slightly larger.

### Deal Structure

**Option 1: Paid shoutout / mention placement**
- Flat fee for them to mention you in a video where it fits naturally
- Price range: $100-2,000 depending on account size
- Deliverable: specific language agreed in advance, video posted within X days, no deletion for 30 days

**Option 2: Reciprocal collaboration**
- You feature them, they feature you
- Works when your audiences have complementary interest but do not overlap
- No cash changes hands

**Option 3: Performance-based**
- Pay per follower gained or per click to your profile via tracking link or unique promo code

**Outreach message template:**

```
Hey [Name],

I've been following your content on [specific topic] — your recent video on
[specific video] was exactly the kind of stuff my audience asks about.

I run [your brand/account] focused on [your niche]. I think our audiences
overlap a lot but don't compete.

Would you be open to featuring/mentioning me in an upcoming video where it
fits naturally? Happy to discuss a flat fee or reciprocal arrangement.

[Your Name]
```

Keep it short. A 3-line message with specific reference to their actual content outperforms a formal media kit email.

### Measuring ROI

Track for 7 days after each mention:
- Follower gain vs. 7-day baseline
- Profile visits (TikTok analytics)
- Link-in-bio clicks
- Direct DMs that reference the creator

ROI calculation: (New followers - baseline) × (your estimated value per follower) vs. fee paid.

### FTC Disclosure Requirements

Any paid placement requires disclosure in the US. Compliant formats:
- Video caption: "#ad" or "#sponsored" or "Paid partnership with @yourname"
- Verbal disclosure: "This is a paid partnership with [brand]"
- TikTok's built-in "Paid Partnership" label — instruct any creator you pay to use it

If a creator mentions you organically (unsolicited, no payment), no disclosure is needed — this was Preston's situation with the 5M-view mention.

### Expected Results
- Per successful mention: 500-60,000+ new followers (Preston's range from one mention)
- Cost per follower: $0.05-0.50 when done well
- Secondary effect: Algorithm boost from profile search spikes

## 9. Free Content Better Than Competitors' Paid

### What It Is
Declan O'Reilly's core competitive strategy: identify what competitors sell (paid courses, templates, snapshots, checklists) and create a free version that is objectively better. Give it away in exchange for an email address.

Declan's specific application: he takes GoHighLevel snapshots and workflow templates that competitors sell for $97-497, reverse-engineers them, improves them, and gives them away as lead magnets.

### Why It Works

**Reciprocity:** When you give someone something genuinely valuable — better than what they would pay for — they feel obligated to reciprocate. The first reciprocation is their email address. The second is their attention. The third is their trust. The fourth is their money.

**Social proof without asking:** When someone uses your free template and it works, they tell others. People share things that make them look smart.

**Risk reversal:** The barrier to paying for your paid product drops dramatically if someone has already received value from your free product. "If his free stuff is this good, the paid version must be incredible."

**Los made this point explicitly:** "Give away sauce on content — nobody's going to do anything." The fear that giving everything away means nobody will pay is empirically wrong. People pay not for information but for transformation — for help implementing. Your paid offering is the bridge across the knowing-doing gap.

Declan's framing: "People judge you on your free shit, not your paid shit."

### How to Identify What to Give Away

**Step 1: Survey the competitive landscape**

Go to Gumroad, Etsy, Udemy, and direct competitor websites in your niche. What are the top 10 paid products? Download or buy the $20-50 ones to understand what is inside them.

Categorize:
- Templates (spreadsheets, design files, documents)
- Frameworks/checklists
- Video courses
- Communities
- Software integrations/snapshots

**Step 2: Find the highest-value, lowest-cost-to-produce items**

You are looking for products that:
- Solve a specific, common problem
- Can be consumed and implemented immediately
- Are sold for $47-497 (high enough that getting it free feels valuable)
- Can be improved without significant additional work

Templates and snapshots are ideal: competitors charge $97-297 for a GHL snapshot. You can build a better version in a day and give it away.

**Step 3: Make yours objectively better**

"Better" means:
- More complete (covers more edge cases)
- More documented (includes instructions, video walkthrough, FAQ)
- More current (updated for current version of the software/platform)
- Easier to implement (step-by-step, not just the resource itself)

Add a video walkthrough. If competitors sell a static template for $97, your free version includes the template PLUS a 20-minute video showing exactly how to use it.

**Step 4: Gate it with an email opt-in**

Landing page with:
- Clear description of what they are getting
- Brief statement of the value ("This template helped me get X result")
- One-field email form (just email)
- Instant delivery via automated email

**Step 5: Use it as a trust ladder**

Email sequence after opt-in:
- Email 1 (immediate): Delivery + quick-start tip
- Email 2 (Day 2): How to get the most out of the resource
- Email 3 (Day 5): Case study of someone who used it
- Email 4 (Day 10): Soft CTA to your paid offer

### What to Give Away vs. What to Sell

| Give Away (Free Lead Magnet) | Sell (Paid Product) |
|------------------------------|---------------------|
| Templates and frameworks | Implementation + accountability |
| One tactic explained deeply | Complete system covering all tactics |
| Solution to a specific sub-problem | Solution to the full problem |
| Your method, described | You, executing the method for them |
| Static resource | Dynamic service or community |

The principle: give away the "what" and "why." Sell the "how" — specifically, your help with the "how."

### Expected Results
- Email opt-in rate: 30-60% on focused landing pages with strong free offer
- List growth: Compounds as people share genuinely useful resources
- Conversion from free list to paid: 1-5%
- Brand effect: Declan describes competitors being upset ("people will block you when you do that") — that is the signal it is working

### Tools Needed
- **ConvertKit or Beehiiv** ($29-49/month) — email list management + automated delivery
- **Typedream or Carrd** ($9-19/month) — simple landing page
- **Loom** (free) — screen record walkthrough video

## 9b. X Spaces Growth Flywheel

**Source:** Preston Rhodes (host, runs recurring X Spaces with 100s of live listeners)

### What It Is

X Spaces is a live audio format — effectively a public podcast with a live audience and real-time participant interaction. The growth flywheel: listeners become followers → followers attend future Spaces → they share Space clips → clips bring in new listeners. Unlike text content, Spaces build qualitative trust in a single session that would take weeks of short-form content to accumulate.

Preston Rhodes' use of Spaces is not incidental — it is the primary mechanism by which he maintains a high-trust audience of serious practitioners. The four-hour recorded session that this playbook is based on demonstrates the model: real names, real revenue numbers, real friction. Audiences who attend Spaces remember the host.

### Why Spaces Build Distribution Faster Than Short-Form

Short-form content builds familiarity. Spaces build trust. These operate on different timescales and produce different conversion behavior:

- A viewer who scrolled past 15 of your TikToks knows your face and general vibe
- A listener who spent 90 minutes in your Space knows your reasoning, your values, the people you associate with, and the level of candor you operate at

The conversion rate from Space listener to booked call, email subscriber, or paid offer is structurally higher because the trust depth is incomparable. You cannot fake 90 minutes of genuine conversation.

### The Flywheel Mechanics

```
Run Space (1-4 hours)
→ Live listeners: 50-500 depending on your current audience size
→ Each listener who stays 30+ minutes is pre-qualified (they have time and interest)
→ Engaged listeners follow host and key speakers
→ Recorded Space sits as evergreen audio content
→ Clip 3-5 best 60-90 second segments as standalone X videos
→ Post clips within 24 hours while the topic is still algorithmically warm
→ Clips reach followers who missed the live session
→ New followers discover host from clip, attend next Space
→ Repeat — each cycle grows the live audience
```

The flywheel compounds because each Space adds both evergreen clips (permanent content) and live relationship depth (temporary but high-conversion).

### The Clip-from-Space Tactic (Key Multiplier)

The clip is the force multiplier. Without clips, a Space reaches only the live audience plus anyone who seeks out the recording. With clips, the Space's best moments reach your entire follower base plus the algorithm's distribution.

**Execution:**

1. Run the Space. While talking, mentally or verbally flag the moments that are standalone-legible (see force clips criteria from the VSSL file).

2. Immediately after the Space ends, export the audio or use a recording tool (Riverside, Descript, or native X recording download).

3. Identify the 3-5 moments where:
   - A specific insight was articulated unusually well
   - A speaker said something counterintuitive or controversial
   - A concrete number or case study was revealed
   - Genuine disagreement or tension emerged between speakers

4. Clip each moment to 60-90 seconds. Add captions via Descript or CapCut (captions increase completion rate by 30-40% on mobile).

5. Post as native X video within 24 hours. Use the moment's best line as the caption/hook, not a description of what the Space was about.

**Why 24 hours matters:** X's algorithm surfaces content based on recency + engagement velocity. A clip posted while the Space topic is still being discussed in other threads benefits from the ongoing conversation signal. Post too late and the algorithmic context has shifted.

### Setup and Cadence

**Starting cadence:** Once weekly or bi-weekly. Consistency matters more than frequency. A Space that happens every Tuesday at 7pm ET builds calendar-based anticipation. Irregular Spaces build no habit.

**Space structure for clips:**
- Invite 2-4 guests with complementary expertise and genuine things to say
- Prepare 3-5 topic seeds but allow organic tangents — tangents produce the best clips
- Run 60-90 minutes minimum (shorter sessions don't develop the trust depth)
- End with a specific actionable — something every listener can do today based on what they heard

**Growth phase bench line:** At under 1,000 followers, expect 10-30 live listeners. At 5,000 followers, expect 50-150. At 50,000+, expect 500+. The asymmetry is that even 30 live listeners who are all serious practitioners is a higher-quality audience than 30,000 passive scroll followers.

### Expected Outputs Per Space

| Activity | Result |
|----------|--------|
| 90-min Space | 10-50 qualified new followers (direct from Space) |
| 3-5 clips posted same day | 3-10x clips' normal engagement vs. regular posts |
| Evergreen recording | Ongoing discovery via profile and search |
| Guest cross-promotion | Each guest shares the Space to their audience |

### Integration with Other Tactics

Spaces amplify the paying-creators-to-credit-you tactic (Tactic 8): invite creators with audiences to be speakers, give them a platform moment, and the social capital exchange creates reciprocal promotion naturally without a payment. They share the Space because they spoke in it.

Spaces also function as long-form content (Tactic 3): a recorded 3-hour Space is functionally equivalent to a 3-hour YouTube video in terms of trust-building value for anyone who listens to the recording.

## 13. Newsletter Sponsorship Sales

### What It Is
Los runs multiple niche newsletters — including onlinebusinessowner.com and aisimple.com — that generate $100K+/month from sponsorships alone. He sold two newsletters to SEMrush. The model: build a niche email list on top of an existing audience, sell ad placements to software companies that want access to your readers, and generate recurring revenue from a media asset.

### Why It Works
Email is the highest-value channel in digital media. An engaged subscriber is worth dramatically more than a social follower. Open rates of 30-50% on niche newsletters vastly outperform organic social reach of 2-5%. Sponsors pay for that attention.

Sponsors buying newsletter placements are primarily SaaS companies with high lifetime value. A $500/month tool adopted by a subscriber is worth $6,000+ per year to the sponsor. If your newsletter drives 10 conversions, the economics justify hundreds or thousands of dollars per placement.

The asset is buildable on top of what you already have. If you are creating content and building an audience anyway, a newsletter is a parallel opt-in layer that monetizes that audience with predictable recurring revenue independent of algorithm changes.

### How to Price: The CPM Formula

CPM = Cost per thousand impressions. Newsletter sponsors think in CPMs.

Industry benchmark for niche B2B newsletters: $50-150 CPM.
For high-LTV niches (finance, software, marketing): $150-400 CPM.

**Your rate calculation:**

If your newsletter has 10,000 subscribers with a 40% open rate:
- Monthly impressions per ad slot: 10,000 × 40% = 4,000 impressions
- At $100 CPM: 4 × $100 = $400/placement
- With 2 ad slots per issue, 4 issues/month: $3,200/month at 100% fill

As you grow: 50,000 subscribers × 40% open = 20,000 impressions × $100 CPM × 2 slots × 4 issues = $16,000/month.

**Starting price:** Start at $75-100 CPM and negotiate. Being too cheap signals low value.

### Who to Pitch

Ideal sponsor profile:
- SaaS or software companies (high LTV, motivated to pay for quality leads)
- Non-competing with your own products/services
- Relevant to your readers' problems
- Budget for content marketing ($2,000+/month minimum)

Where to find them:

1. **Software your readers already use.** Send a 3-question survey to your list: "What tools do you use for [topic]?" The tools they mention are your first-tier sponsor targets.

2. **Competitor newsletters.** Read newsletters in adjacent niches. Who is advertising there? Those companies are already buying newsletter placements.

3. **Product Hunt.** Search for newly launched tools in your niche. They have marketing budgets and need early users.

4. **AngelList/LinkedIn.** Filter for B2B SaaS companies in your niche, 2-5 years old.

**Outreach template:**

```
Subject: Sponsorship opportunity — [Newsletter Name], [X]K subscribers in [niche]

Hi [Name],

I run [Newsletter Name] — a weekly newsletter for [specific audience].
[X,000] subscribers, [X%] open rate, [X%] of readers are [decision-maker title].

I reach [their target buyer] with editorial content they trust.
Sponsored placement: [format description].

Rate: $[amount] per issue. Available [dates].

Want the media kit?

[Your Name]
```

### What Sponsors Want to See (Your Media Kit)

A 1-2 page document including:
- Subscriber count + open rate (last 30-day average)
- Subscriber demographics: job titles, company sizes, industry breakdown (get this via subscriber survey)
- Engagement proof: reply rate, click rate on past issues
- Example placement: show what a sponsored section actually looks like
- Rate card
- List of past or current sponsors (or say "inaugural sponsor opportunity" early on)

### When to Start Selling

- Under 1,000 subscribers: Build for 3-6 more months
- 1,000-5,000 subscribers: Start pitching if niche is highly specific with high open rates (engagement matters more than list size at this stage)
- 5,000+ subscribers: Actively sell; you have enough data for a compelling media kit

### Tools Needed
- **Beehiiv** ($39/month) — purpose-built for newsletter monetization with built-in sponsorship marketplace
- **ConvertKit** ($29/month) — alternative with strong automation
- **Canva** (free) — media kit design
- **Stripe** (free + transaction fees) — sponsor billing

## 14. One Platform Domination — Pick One and Own It

### What It Is
Declan's explicit observation: every single person he knows who does "retardedly well" picked one platform and dominated it before going elsewhere. Diversification at the start is the strategy of people who have not succeeded. Focus is the strategy of people who have.

The concept: 100% of your content energy into one platform for 12-24 months until you are a recognized name in your niche on that platform. Only then consider a second.

### Why It Works
Platform algorithms reward consistent posters who stay in their lane. An account that posts reliably in one niche gets progressively better distribution as the algorithm builds confidence in the audience fit. An account posting inconsistently across multiple niches teaches the algorithm nothing.

Skill accumulation is non-linear. Your 200th video on a platform leverages everything you learned from videos 1-199. If you are posting across 4 platforms simultaneously, you are always at your 50th video on each.

### Platform Selection Framework by Business Type

Choose based on: where your buyers are, where your content type performs, and where you have an existing advantage.

| Business Type | Primary Platform | Secondary (after mastery) |
|---------------|-----------------|--------------------------|
| B2B service (agencies, consulting) | LinkedIn + YouTube | X |
| High-ticket coaching | YouTube | Instagram |
| E-commerce / DTC | TikTok | Instagram |
| Software/SaaS | YouTube | LinkedIn |
| Local service business | Instagram | TikTok |
| Content creator / affiliate | YouTube | TikTok |
| Crypto/Web3 | X | YouTube |
| Financial advice | YouTube | LinkedIn |

**Decision criteria:**

1. Where do people like you already follow people like you? Find 5-10 creators in your exact niche. Which platform are they biggest on? That is where your audience is learning.

2. Where do you consume content naturally? You will create better content on platforms you use.

3. What format plays to your strengths? Long-form thinker → YouTube. Fast-twitch commenter → X. Visual product → Instagram. Talker/teacher → TikTok.

4. What is the monetization path? YouTube: ad revenue + affiliate. TikTok Shop: direct commerce. LinkedIn: B2B service sales. Newsletter: sponsorships. Align platform with business model.

### What "Owning" a Platform Looks Like

You have won a platform when:
- Searching your name + niche returns you first
- Peers in your niche know who you are without your having introduced yourself
- People in your target audience say "I've been following you for a while" more often than "I just found you"
- You receive inbound opportunities (speaking, partnerships, brand deals) without applying

### Timeline to Results by Platform

| Platform | Time to First Traction | Time to Real Revenue |
|----------|------------------------|---------------------|
| TikTok | 30-90 days | 3-6 months |
| YouTube | 6-12 months | 12-18 months |
| LinkedIn | 3-6 months | 6-12 months |
| Newsletter | 6-12 months | 12-24 months |
| X | 3-9 months | 6-18 months |

Note: Applying the tactics in this playbook (volume, hooks, niche specificity, long-form) compresses these timelines significantly. Declan hit $100K/month with 20K YouTube subscribers.

### The Rule for Going Multi-Platform

Do not start a second platform until:
- Platform 1 is self-sustaining (growing without forcing it)
- Platform 1 is generating at least $5,000/month in direct or attributed revenue
- You have enough content from Platform 1 to repurpose (see Tactic 5)

When you do go multi-platform, treat Platform 2 as a distribution channel for Platform 1's content — not a new creation effort.

## 15. Local Retargeting Value Ads — Perpetual Propaganda

### What It Is
Los's model for dominating a local or regional market: run continuous video ads in one city targeting a specific professional audience, spending $10-30/day ($300-900/month). The content is educational and valuable, not promotional. Over time, the same people see you repeatedly — you become "the person" for your category in that city before you ever speak to most of them.

Los's framing: "No one's competing trying to be the biggest person in their area. They just try to take ad space nationally."

### Why It Works

**CPM arbitrage:** Local targeting dramatically reduces ad costs. A national campaign might cost $15-40 CPM. A tightly geo-targeted local campaign can cost $3-8 CPM — 5-10x cheaper per impression.

**Zero competition:** Every other business, consultant, and creator is chasing national or global relevance. By focusing on one city, you face almost no competition for local mental real estate.

**Frequency effect:** Effective advertising requires frequency — people need to see your message multiple times before it registers. With $300/month in one city, you can achieve 8-12+ impressions per person per month with a tightly targeted audience. With $300/month nationally, you get one impression per person and they forget you.

**Trust through familiarity:** The "mere exposure effect" — people like things simply because they have seen them repeatedly. By running educational content as ads, you are building familiarity and credibility simultaneously.

### Meta Ads Setup Walkthrough

**Step 1: Define your audience**

In Meta Ads Manager:
- Geographic: "People who live in" (not "traveling to") → select your target city + 15-25 mile radius
- Age: Narrow to your buyer's age range (e.g., 35-60 for B2B decision-makers)
- Job title targeting: Detailed Targeting → Employment → Job Titles → add 10-15 relevant titles
- Exclude current clients: Upload your existing client list as a custom audience to exclude

Target audience size: 20,000-100,000 people. This is the right range for local.

**Step 2: Campaign structure**

- Campaign objective: Video Views (not conversions — you are building familiarity, not driving immediate action)
- Budget: $10-15/day ($300-450/month) per ad set
- Duration: Ongoing, no end date — this is perpetual infrastructure

**Step 3: Creative for value ads**

The ad content should be something a viewer would genuinely thank you for. Not a commercial.

Formats that work:
- 60-90 second educational video addressing a specific problem your target audience faces
- "Did you know X?" format with an insight that reframes how they think about their problem
- Mini case study: "A [their job title] in [city] did X and it resulted in Y"
- Reaction to newsworthy developments in their industry

What to NOT run: your logo and tagline, photos of your office, testimonials, or any format that announces it is an ad.

**Step 4: Custom audience architecture for retargeting**

After running value ads for 30-60 days, build a retargeting layer:
- Create a custom audience: "People who watched 50%+ of [your value videos]"
- Run a second ad set to this audience with a soft CTA: "If you're dealing with [problem], I'd like to show you how we solve it for businesses in [city]."
- Budget: $5-10/day for this retargeting layer

The value ads create the warm audience. The retargeting layer converts them.

**Step 5: Monthly review**

- Cost per 3-second video view (target: under $0.05)
- Frequency (target: 6-12 per month per person)
- Video completion rate (target: 20%+ at 50% completion)

If frequency exceeds 15 per month, expand geography slightly or refresh creative.

### Expected Results
- Month 1-2: No direct leads (familiarity building phase)
- Month 3-4: Inbound inquiries where caller says "I've seen your videos"
- Month 6+: Strong local recognition; event attendance increases; referrals spike
- 12 months: You are the recognized authority in your category in that city

Benchmark: Los references a $300K/year local newsletter operator from a My First Million case study. $300-900/month in ads is the infrastructure cost for this level of local dominance.

### Tools Needed
- Meta Ads Manager (free)
- CapCut or Premiere (video editing)
- HubSpot free tier (track local inbound leads)
- Budget: $300-900/month minimum

## 16. The Free-to-Paid Flywheel — Full Mechanics

### What It Is
The complete system connecting free content to paid clients. This is not just "give stuff away" — it is a deliberate sequence where each stage qualifies the next. Los, Declan, and Preston all operate versions of this model.

### The Full Flywheel Mechanics

```
FREE CONTENT → AUDIENCE → FREE LEAD MAGNET → EMAIL LIST →
TRUST NURTURE → AUTHORITY SIGNAL → INBOUND INQUIRY → SOFT OFFER → PAID CLIENT
```

**Stage 1: Free content (platform content)**

What it does: Creates broad awareness. Reaches cold audiences. Attracts people with the right problem. Filters in the right buyer psychology.

Success signal: Comments and DMs from people with your target buyer's problems.

**Stage 2: Audience**

What it does: The subset of content viewers who follow you. They have seen enough to want more. Not yet buyers.

Success signal: Growing follower count + DMs from people you would want to work with.

**Stage 3: Free lead magnet (see Tactic 9)**

What it does: Converts followers to email subscribers. Requires them to raise their hand. The act of exchanging an email address is micro-commitment — it signals real interest, not passive scrolling.

Success signal: 20-50% of landing page visitors convert.

**Stage 4: Email list (owned asset)**

What it does: Platform-independent. When TikTok bans your account or Instagram changes the algorithm, your email list is unaffected. This is why building a list matters even when social is performing.

Success signal: Open rate above 30%, reply rate above 2%.

**Stage 5: Trust nurture**

What it does: The email sequence that moves subscribers from "interested in free resource" to "trust this person with my business."

What to send: Educational emails that address the subscriber's problem in depth. Not promotions. Value-forward, specific, actionable emails that make the subscriber feel smart after reading.

Cadence: 2x/week for first 30 days (new subscribers are most engaged). 1x/week ongoing.

**Stage 6: Authority signal**

What it does: A specific email or content piece that establishes you as the category expert. Often a case study, a specific data point, or a contrarian insight they have not seen elsewhere.

Example: An email that says "I've done X for 47 clients and here's the one variable that determines whether it works or not." This can only come from experience.

**Stage 7: Inbound inquiry**

What it does: Subscriber reaches out to you directly — via email reply, DM, or booking link. This is the goal state. Inbound inquiries convert at 3-5x the rate of cold outreach because the prospect has pre-sold themselves.

You know the flywheel is working when you are receiving more inbound than you can handle.

**Stage 8: Soft offer**

What it does: Present your paid product or service in response to inquiry, or in a periodic email to your engaged list.

Presentation format: "Here's what working together looks like. Here's who it's right for. Here's who it's not right for. Here's the result you can expect."

People who disqualify themselves are as valuable as people who qualify in — they prevent bad client relationships.

**Stage 9: Paid client**

If the flywheel has been working correctly, the first paid interaction is smooth — expectations are calibrated, trust is established, and the client's motivation is high.

### What to Give Away vs. What to Sell (at Scale)

| Stage | What to Give | What to Sell |
|-------|-------------|--------------|
| Early (0-500 subs) | Best insights, templates, frameworks | Direct 1:1 service |
| Mid (500-5,000 subs) | Courses, systems, toolkits | Cohort program or retainer |
| Scale (5,000+ subs) | Community access, live training | High-ticket program, equity deals |

### Expected Results
- Month 1-3: List building phase (slow monetization, fast relationship building)
- Month 4-6: First significant inbound inquiries from list
- Month 6-12: Flywheel self-sustaining; free content generates paid clients without active sales effort
- Year 2+: Compounding — every new piece of free content feeds an already-warm list

## 17. The Hormozi Free Book Funnel

### What It Is
Write a book, price it at $0-7, use it as a lead magnet, qualify buyers through the purchase act, and upsell to a higher-ticket offer. The book's low price removes the financial barrier; the act of purchasing signals buying intent. Multiple speakers in the room referenced this model.

Los notes that the "give your opinion" method often attributed to Hormozi was actually created by Ryan McInn, who had an NDA — but the funnel structure itself has been popularized by Hormozi and is the widely-known implementation.

### The Exact Structure

**Step 1: Write the book (3-6 weeks)**

This is not a literary endeavor. It is a long-form value document, 15,000-40,000 words, that comprehensively solves a specific problem your buyers have.

The book should:
- Promise a specific, measurable outcome in the subtitle ("Get [Specific Result] in [Timeframe] Even If [Common Objection]")
- Deliver the full methodology without holding anything back
- Reference case studies from real clients
- Include clear next steps at the end that naturally lead to your paid offer

What to write about: the most common problem you solve for clients. The book is a documented version of what you do for people.

**Format options:**

1. **Physical book** (Hormozi's version): Sell for $0.97-$7 + shipping. Physical creates tangible commitment; people who ordered a physical book are more qualified than ebook downloaders.

2. **PDF ebook:** Price at $0-27. Instant delivery. Lower barrier, lower qualification signal. Still works.

3. **Audiobook:** Creates a 3-4 hour listening session with you — deep trust building.

**Step 2: Set up the funnel**

Landing page structure:
- Headline: [Specific Promise] — Book by [Your Name]
- Sub-headline: Who this is for (be specific — exclude wrong buyers explicitly)
- What's inside: 5-7 bullets of specific outcomes, not chapter titles
- About you: 3-4 lines of credibility relevant to the promise
- Buy button: "Yes, I want the book — $7" or "Get the free book (just pay shipping)"
- Below the fold: Testimonials or case study snippets

**Step 3: Post-purchase flow**

**Immediate upsell (OTO — One Time Offer):**

After purchase, before the book arrives, show a one-time offer. Typically $97-297 — a course, template pack, or community access.

Conversion benchmark: 10-30% of book buyers take an OTO. If your book costs $7 and 20% take a $197 OTO, your average order value is $7 + (20% × $197) = $46.40 per buyer.

**Email welcome sequence (7-14 days):**
- Day 0: Book delivery + quick start tip
- Day 1: "Here's the first thing to do with what you read"
- Day 3: Case study of someone who implemented the method
- Day 5: Common mistake and how to avoid it
- Day 7: Invitation to a discovery call or free training
- Day 10: Social proof stack (testimonials + results)
- Day 14: Direct offer for your primary service

**Step 4: The upsell offer**

The book buyer who reaches your offer page has:
- Self-identified as someone with this problem (bought the book)
- Consumed 15,000-40,000 words of your thinking (trust built)
- Implemented some of your advice (commitment)
- Seen results or hit a wall (motivation for next level)

Your offer should be the natural next step: "The book gives you the framework. [Offer Name] gives you my help implementing it."

### Economics

**Break-even analysis (physical book model):**

| Item | Estimate |
|------|----------|
| Book printing cost | $4-6/unit |
| Shipping | $4-6/unit |
| Total fulfillment | $8-12/unit |
| Book price | $7 + $7 shipping = $14 |
| Gross margin on book | $2-6/unit |
| OTO (20% take $197) | +$39/unit average |
| Total average order value | ~$41-45/unit |
| Ad cost per acquired buyer | ~$10-15 |
| Profit per buyer (before email sequence) | ~$25-35 |

**Lifetime value at scale:**

If 5% of book buyers convert to your core offer at $2,000:
- 1,000 book buyers → 50 core clients → $100,000
- Cost of acquiring 1,000 buyers at $15 each = $15,000
- Net: $85,000 from 1,000 buyers

At 5,000 book buyers: 250 core clients → $500,000 at $85K cost.

### Conversion Benchmarks

| Funnel Stage | Typical Conversion |
|--------------|--------------------|
| Landing page → book purchase | 5-15% of visitors |
| Book purchase → OTO acceptance | 10-30% |
| Email sequence completion | 30-60% of those who open |
| Email sequence → discovery call | 2-8% |
| Discovery call → paid client | 30-60% |

### How to Write the Book (Practical)

1. **Record, do not write.** Open a voice recorder and explain your methodology as if consulting someone for free. Transcribe with Otter.ai or Descript. Transcript = rough draft.

2. **Structure before writing.** Outline: Problem (ch. 1), Why current solutions fail (ch. 2), Your unique framework (ch. 3-6), Implementation guide (ch. 7-9), Case studies (ch. 10), Next steps (ch. 11).

3. **Write chapter 10 first.** Real case studies are the most compelling part and hardest to fabricate later. Getting them done first removes the biggest bottleneck.

4. **Edit for clarity, not literature.** Short sentences. Active voice. The Hemingway App (free) scores your clarity. Target grade 6-8 reading level.

5. **Cover design matters.** A professionally designed cover (Fiverr, $30-100) significantly improves perceived value. Do not use Canva templates.

**Timeline to completion:**
- Recording sessions: 4-8 hours total
- Transcription: 1 day (automated)
- First edit: 1-2 weeks
- Case studies + examples: 1 week
- Final edit + formatting: 3-5 days
- Total: 4-6 weeks at consistent effort

### Tools Needed
- **Otter.ai or Descript** — voice transcription
- **Hemingway App** (free) — clarity editing
- **Gumroad or ThriveCart** — digital product funnel with OTO
- **Printify or Amazon KDP** — physical book printing
- **ConvertKit** — post-purchase email sequence
- **Fiverr** — cover design
