# Levels of Energy: Copywriting & Audience Targeting Framework

*Based on Frederick Dodson's "Levels of Energy" (2010) and David Hawkins' "Power vs. Force" (1995) / "The Map of Consciousness Explained" (2020)*

---

## Why This Framework Exists

Most audience targeting asks: *Who is this person?* Age, income, job title, pain points.

Dodson's framework asks something different: *What state is this person in?* Their emotional frequency determines what messages they can receive, what promises resonate, and what CTAs they'll act on. Two people with identical demographics — same income, same problem, same industry — will respond to completely different copy if they're operating at different energy levels.

This is psychographic targeting at a deeper layer than VALS or Big 5 personality typing. It's not about values or personality traits. It's about the current operating state of their nervous system.

---

## The Origin: Hawkins + Dodson

### David Hawkins' Map of Consciousness

Psychiatrist David Hawkins published *Power vs. Force* in 1995 after 20+ years of research (250,000+ calibrations using kinesiology/muscle testing). He mapped human consciousness on a logarithmic scale of 1–1000.

The scale is logarithmic, not linear. Level 300 is not 1.5x level 200 — it's 300 to the 10th power. A few points of movement represents enormous inner change. This also means relatively few people live at the higher levels.

**The critical threshold: Level 200.**

Below 200 = Force states. The person consumes more than they contribute. Their worldview is adversarial, zero-sum. Life is something happening *to* them.

Above 200 = Power states. The person generates more than they take. Their worldview is collaborative. Life is something they shape.

### Frederick Dodson's Expansion

Dodson (author of 35+ books, reality creation coach) took Hawkins' framework and made it applied and granular. In *Levels of Energy* (2010), he mapped specific behavioral signatures, money beliefs, communication styles, and practical techniques for each level band.

His core insight: **"You experience the world not as it is, but as you are."**

The level a person operates at determines which reality they can perceive. This isn't metaphysics — it's practically measurable in what advertising they respond to, what promises they believe, what risks they'll take.

### Level-by-Level Detail (Marketing-Relevant Bands)

#### Level 20–50: Shame / Guilt / Apathy
**Internal state:** Self-directed hatred. Worthlessness. Hopelessness. Feels like the world would be better without them.
**World view:** Condemning. Tragic.
**Marketing relevance:** Not a viable customer. Cannot receive offers. Deep intervention needed, not sales.

---

#### Level 75–100: Grief / Fear
**Internal state:** Persistent sadness, loss. World perceived as dangerous. Paranoia, anxiety, inferiority.
**Behavior:** Avoidance. Does nothing without external push. Paralysis.
**Money beliefs:** Money is scarce and dangerous. Every financial decision triggers fear of catastrophe.
**Marketing relevance:** Fear-based copy technically reaches them but produces paralysis or buyer's remorse, not action. Dangerous to target here.

---

#### Level 120–125: Desire / Craving
**Internal state:** Craving, compulsion, unfulfilled need. Addictive loop — get, don't feel satisfied, want more.
**World view:** Disappointing. The acquisition never delivers what was promised.
**Behavior:** Impulsive buying. Seeks the rush of the purchase, not the result. Addicted to sweets, substances, quick hits, the feeling of "about to get."
**Money beliefs:** Money is the solution to all problems. If I just had more, everything would be fine.
**Marketing relevance:** Responds intensely to dopamine-triggers. Lottery language. "What if..." fantasies. Gets drawn in by the possibility of the thing, not the thing itself. High buy rate, high refund rate, low transformation rate.

> *Dodson's note: "The 125 person gets a rush through destructive means." The addiction is to the craving state, not the outcome.*

---

#### Level 150–160: Anger
**Internal state:** Resentment, frustration, hostility. Life is antagonistic. Others are obstacles or threats.
**World view:** Adversarial. Hateful. Everyone is either with you or against you.
**Behavior:** Combative. Blames others for their situation. Energy to act, but misdirected. Can be motivated by revenge or spite.
**Money beliefs:** The system is rigged against me. The rich are thieves. I'm being cheated.
**Marketing relevance:** Responds to "us vs. them" framing. Anti-establishment angles. "The thing THEY don't want you to know." Rebel positioning. Can convert if you give them a villain to rage against and a weapon to fight with.

---

#### Level 175–190: Pride / Antagonism
**Internal state:** Inflated sense of self. Superiority. Scorn toward others. Status-seeking.
**World view:** Demanding. Indifferent God. "I deserve more."
**Behavior:** Status signaling. Seeks validation and recognition. Defensive when challenged. Holds on to past successes. Fights "the system" to prove a point.
**Money beliefs:** Money = status and superiority over others. Wealth proves I'm better.
**Marketing relevance:** Responds to exclusivity, status signals, "only for the top 1%", expert positioning, anything that makes them feel like they're in the inner circle. Deeply brand-conscious. Will pay a premium for identity-aligned products.

> *Watch out: Appeals to pride can work, but the customer is loyal to their self-image, not your brand. One slight to their ego ends the relationship.*

---

#### Level 200: Contentment / Courage (The Threshold)
**Internal state:** Baseline functionality. Routine. Not joyful, not miserable. The first "power" level.
**World view:** Feasible. Permitting. Life is manageable.
**Behavior:** Can take action but needs external nudge. Requires proof. "I'll believe it when I see it." Risk-averse. Preoccupied with safety, food, basic security.
**Money beliefs:** Money comes from hard work. Must trade time for income.
**Marketing relevance:** Responds to case studies and testimonials. Social proof heavy. Needs to see that others like them have succeeded. Step-by-step promises. Simple, achievable outcomes. NOT big dreams — they don't believe in those yet.

---

#### Level 250: Willingness / Neutrality
**Internal state:** Grounded. Orderly. "Live and let live." Functional and stable.
**World view:** Satisfactory. Trusting. Things work out.
**Behavior:** Can achieve through deliberate action. Values networks and relationships. Still limited imagination — struggles to think outside proven paths.
**Money beliefs:** "You must work hard to receive money." Believes in effort-reward correlation but hasn't broken through to leverage yet.
**Marketing relevance:** Responds to step-by-step systems, credentials, track records, peer networks. "Join 10,000 people who..." Logical structure. Proof-points. Values orderliness — messy or chaotic brands lose them.

> *Dodson's prescribed technique for the 200s: **Scripting** — write detailed "movie scripts" of the desired life. This trains imagination, focus, and courage — three things the 200s lack.*

---

#### Level 275–300: Courage / Early Kindness
**Internal state:** Action-oriented. Eagerness. Relaxation replacing tension. Early genuine positivity.
**World view:** Challenging but interesting. Growth is possible.
**Behavior:** Pursues goals. Begins extending kindness beyond self-interest. Not yet fully others-focused but moving that direction.
**Money beliefs:** Starting to believe in leverage. Open to new income models.
**Marketing relevance:** Responds to "challenge accepted" framing. Goal-oriented copy. Progress narratives. "Here's what's possible in 90 days." Values honesty and directness. Early adopter energy — willing to bet on themselves.

---

#### Level 310–320: True Willingness / Inner Positivity
**Internal state:** True inner positivity begins. Not forced smile, not effortful "positive thinking." Genuine positive intent toward self, others, world.
**World view:** Hopeful. Inspiring. Good things happen to people who work for them.
**Behavior:** Self-motivating. Doesn't need external motivation. Open-minded. Extroverted. "I'm OK, you're OK." Good mental and physical shape. Discerns fact from fiction. Lets go of grudges easily.
**Money beliefs:** Money flows to value creators. Beginning to see income as a reflection of impact.
**Marketing relevance:** Responds to community and belonging. "People like you." Win-win framing. Transformation stories. Values authenticity — sniffs out manipulation immediately. Responds well to behind-the-scenes, honest process content.

---

#### Level 350–380: Acceptance / Enthusiasm
**Internal state:** Proactive. Forgiving. Sees possibilities others miss. Mercy and harmony develop.
**World view:** Harmonious. Meaningful.
**Behavior:** Goal-setting becomes natural. Takes genuine responsibility for change. No longer blames external forces.
**Money beliefs:** Abundance is available. Willing to invest in self for long-term returns.
**Marketing relevance:** Responds to possibility and transformation. ROI language — not just "feel better" but "here's what this unlocks." Values integrity of the offer. Won't buy from brands they don't respect.

---

#### Level 400–450: Reason / Intelligence
**Internal state:** Rational, analytical. Clear thinking that transcends emotional reactivity. Sees multiple perspectives.
**World view:** Meaningful. Wise.
**Behavior:** Evidence-based decision making. Abstract thinking. Expert in chosen domains. High professional competence. Intellectually curious. Open to logical persuasion.
**Money beliefs:** Money is a resource to be deployed intelligently. Returns on investment matter.
**Marketing relevance:** Responds to data, research, nuanced arguments. Will read long-form content. Wants to understand the mechanism, not just the outcome. Intellectual respect required — talking down to them kills the sale. Case studies with specifics. Third-party validation.

> *The 400-level buyer's kryptonite: oversimplification. Hype and manipulation repel them. The sale happens through insight, not pressure.*

---

#### Level 450–500: Creativity / Joy / Early Love
**Internal state:** Natural, spontaneous. Childlike (not childish). Everything is interesting and beautiful.
**World view:** Beautiful. Abundant.
**Behavior:** Creates for the joy of creating. Influence extends naturally — people are uplifted by their presence. Has detached from status seeking.
**Money beliefs:** Wealth flows naturally from contribution. Detachment from money paradoxically attracts it.
**Marketing relevance:** Responds to beauty, craft, and genuine impact. Wants to know what the work *means*, not just what it delivers. Mission-led brands. Aesthetic quality signals. "What we're building together." Values the relationship with the brand, not just the transaction.

---

#### Level 500+: Love / Joy / Peace
**Internal state:** Unconditional love. Pervasive happiness. Inner peace not dependent on circumstances.
**World view:** Reverential. Benign. Whole.
**Behavior:** Saint-level. Gandhi, Mother Teresa territory. Roughly 1 in 250 people ever reach sustained Love consciousness.
**Marketing relevance:** Extremely small market. Copy that resonates here is about legacy, service, and meaning — not personal gain. Mission over metrics.

## The Three Big Orientations

Dodson draws a critical line between how different energy bands relate to desire:

| Band | Orientation | Wants |
|------|-------------|-------|
| 0–199 | **Having** | Possessions, external validation |
| 200–450 | **Doing** | Action, achievement, systems |
| 450–600 | **Being** | States, meaning, presence |

This directly maps to copywriting structure:

- **Having-oriented copy** leads with what they'll possess. "Get the car. Get the money. Get the girl."
- **Doing-oriented copy** leads with what they'll accomplish. "Build the system. Hit the number. Reach the goal."
- **Being-oriented copy** leads with who they'll become. "Live with freedom. Create with meaning. Be at peace."

Using the wrong orientation for a given audience creates disconnect at a fundamental level, even if the offer is perfect.

## Why This Goes Beyond ICP

A traditional ICP says: "My buyer is a 35-year-old male, $75k income, works in sales, frustrated with their boss."

That same ICP could describe a person operating at level 150 (angry, blaming their boss, wants to burn it down) or level 320 (genuinely growing, wants to level up, open to systems and frameworks).

These two people need completely different copy, different offers, different CTAs, and different community environments. Selling the same product the same way to both will fail half the time.

**Energy level targeting adds the dimension that demographics miss: what state is the person in RIGHT NOW.**

Two practical consequences:

1. **The same person shifts levels.** Your customer from last year at level 200 might be at level 300 now. Their old hooks don't land. Your copy needs to evolve with them — or they leave.

2. **Your offer might attract different energy levels depending on how you position it.** A productivity tool positioned as "stop wasting time and finally make real money" draws level 120–150 buyers (craving/anger). The same tool positioned as "build the system that lets your work matter more" draws level 300–400 buyers. One cohort will churn. The other will refer.

## Identifying Your Audience's Energy Level

You can't ask directly. But the signals are everywhere.

### Language Signals

| What they say | Likely level |
|---------------|-------------|
| "I just need to make rent this month" | 100–150 |
| "The algorithm is rigged / the game is rigged" | 150–175 |
| "I'm grinding every day but not seeing results" | 175–200 |
| "I need proof this actually works before I invest" | 200–250 |
| "I want a step-by-step system I can follow" | 250–300 |
| "I'm ready to bet on myself" | 275–320 |
| "I want to build something that lasts" | 320–380 |
| "Show me the data / what's the mechanism?" | 400–450 |
| "I want my work to mean something" | 450–500 |

### Behavioral Signals

**Level 100–175 audience behaviors:**
- Consumes free content obsessively but never buys
- Asks for refunds frequently
- Blames products when they don't work
- Follows dozens of gurus simultaneously
- Highly active in comment sections (especially hostile ones)
- Drawn to $7–$27 impulse purchases and lottery-style launches
- Engaged by shock/controversy content

**Level 200–300 audience behaviors:**
- Needs social proof before committing
- Reads reviews thoroughly
- Asks "how long did it take for results?"
- Values testimonials from people who look like them
- Responsive to free trials, guarantees, money-back promises
- Buys step-by-step courses, SOPs, templates

**Level 300–450 audience behaviors:**
- Reads long-form content thoroughly
- Asks substantive questions before buying
- Researches the founder/company
- Wants to understand the philosophy, not just the tactics
- Values reputation and track record
- Will pay a premium for quality they can verify
- Refers others proactively when satisfied

**Level 450+ audience behaviors:**
- Buys based on values alignment
- Cares about the mission behind the company
- Low price sensitivity if the work resonates
- Long retention, high LTV
- Drives word-of-mouth organically without incentives

### Channel Signals

The platforms and content formats people consume reveal their level:

| Platform / Format | Typical energy concentration |
|-------------------|------------------------------|
| Engagement bait / "like if you agree" | 120–175 |
| "I made $X in 30 days" screenshots | 150–200 |
| Step-by-step YouTube tutorials | 200–275 |
| Business podcasts with founder stories | 275–350 |
| Long-form newsletters, deep research | 350–450 |
| Philosophy/systems/second-order thinking | 400–500 |
| Retreat/mindfulness content | 450–600 |

## Copywriting at Each Level: Practical Examples

### Level 120–175: The "Make Money Online" Audience

This is Dodson's most misunderstood band — and the most over-marketed-to. It spans desire (120), anger (150–160), and pride (175–190).

**Psychological state:** Dopamine-driven. Craving the feeling of possibility. Scarcity mindset. Zero-sum thinking. Resentment of those succeeding. Desperate for shortcuts.

**What works:**
- Vivid dream-state painting ("Imagine waking up at 10am with $800 in your account...")
- Social proof from "regular people" (not gurus)
- "They don't want you to know this"
- Speed + ease + simplicity claims
- FOMO triggers (countdown timers, limited spots)
- Rags-to-riches narratives
- Revenge arc ("I was broke and ignored — now...")

**Hook examples:**
> "I made $4,200 last Tuesday with my phone. Here's the exact thing I did."

> "Stop grinding for a boss who doesn't respect you. Here's the exit."

> "Why most people stay broke (and the one shift that changes everything)"

**CTA examples:**
> "Grab your spot before this closes"
> "Get instant access now"
> "Join 3,847 people already doing this"

**What doesn't work:**
- Long rational explanations of mechanism
- Nuanced ROI analysis
- Process-heavy framing ("it takes 6–12 months")
- Community/belonging angles without status attached
- Anything that feels like work before the reward

**The trap:** This audience buys at high rates but refunds at high rates and rarely implements. Their energy state doesn't sustain action. They bought the feeling, not the product. High churn, low referral, high support tickets.

---

### Level 200–275: The Functional Achiever

**Psychological state:** Cautious, needs proof, motivated by fear of wasting money/time. Wants a system. Values orderliness.

**What works:**
- Case studies with specific numbers from relatable people
- Step-by-step frameworks with clear sequences
- Risk removal (guarantee, free trial, low first investment)
- Social proof at scale ("10,000 students")
- Credentials and track record
- Before/after transformation stories
- "Here's exactly what you'll do in week 1, week 2, week 3..."

**Hook examples:**
> "The exact 5-step system I used to add $12k/month to my income (with screenshots)"

> "Copy my process. I've already tested what doesn't work."

> "Join 8,000 people who used this framework to replace their salary"

**CTA examples:**
> "Start with the free training — no credit card"
> "See if this is right for you →"
> "Try it risk-free for 30 days"

**What doesn't work:**
- Abstract "possibility" framing without steps
- High-ticket first touch
- Trust-first without evidence first
- Philosophical or meaning-based language

---

### Level 300–380: The Self-Directed Builder

**Psychological state:** Intrinsically motivated. Growing. Wants to contribute. Operates from genuine positive intent. Sniffs out manipulation.

**What works:**
- Community and belonging ("builders like you")
- Win-win value framing
- Authentic behind-the-scenes content
- Transformation narratives that emphasize growth journey, not just outcome
- Mission and values alignment
- Peer-level conversations (not guru-to-student)
- Honest about limitations and tradeoffs

**Hook examples:**
> "The honest truth about building a personal brand in 2026 — what actually works and what's a waste of time"

> "How we built a $500k/year business without an ads budget — the boring real version"

> "If you're already doing the work, here's what separates the ones who break through"

**CTA examples:**
> "Join the community"
> "Let's figure out your next step together"
> "Apply to work with us"

**What doesn't work:**
- Manipulation or false urgency
- Status-flex bragging without substance
- Overpromised outcomes
- Talking down to them

---

### Level 400–450: The Rational Expert

**Psychological state:** Analytical. Evidence-driven. Respects intellectual rigor. Makes decisions from reason, not emotion. Won't be rushed.

**What works:**
- Data and research citations
- Mechanism explanations ("here's *why* this works")
- Long-form content with depth
- Nuanced takes that respect their intelligence
- Specific, verifiable claims (not vague "transformations")
- Third-party evidence and peer validation
- Analysis of what doesn't work and why

**Hook examples:**
> "We analyzed 1,200 landing pages. Here's what actually moves conversion rate."

> "The research on what separates top 10% earners from everyone else — and it's not what you think"

> "A rational framework for choosing your content strategy in 2026 (with tradeoffs)"

**CTA examples:**
> "Read the full breakdown →"
> "Download the research"
> "Schedule a strategy call to see if this fits your situation"

**What doesn't work:**
- Hype language
- Vague transformation promises
- Social proof without specifics
- Oversimplification ("just do these 3 things!")

---

### Level 450–500: The Purpose-Driven Creator

**Psychological state:** Creating for meaning. Values alignment above all. Buys with their identity, not just their wallet. Wants to be part of something.

**What works:**
- Mission-led positioning
- Craft and quality signals
- "What we're building together" framing
- Legacy and impact language
- Aesthetic and aesthetic excellence
- Who you serve and why it matters
- Long-form narrative about the vision

**Hook examples:**
> "We built this for the people who are done chasing metrics and want to build something real"

> "A platform for creators who care about the work, not just the numbers"

> "Ten years from now, this is what we're aiming to have built — here's why"

**CTA examples:**
> "Join us"
> "Become a founding member"
> "Apply — we're selective about who we work with"

## Match Energy vs. Elevate Energy

This is the strategic core of the framework. Two valid approaches, very different consequences.

### Match: Meet Them Where They Are

Write copy that mirrors the emotional state your audience is currently in. Use their language, their fears, their resentments, their fantasies.

**When to match:**
- Top-of-funnel awareness ads (interrupt the scroll, speak the language)
- First-touch cold traffic
- High-competition markets where attention is the primary battle
- Commodity offers where switching cost is low

**Risks of matching indefinitely:**
- You attract the energy level you speak to — and keep them there
- Level 150 copy builds a level 150 audience. High refunds, high drama, low LTV.
- You end up locked in an arms race of increasingly extreme promises

### Elevate: Pull Them Forward

Write copy that acknowledges their current state and offers a path toward a slightly higher state. This is aspirational without being disconnected.

The key phrase: **slightly higher**. You cannot market level 500 content to a level 150 audience. The gap is too large. They literally cannot perceive what you're describing.

Dodson's principle: "We literally cannot perceive what exists beyond our current level of consciousness." This is why launching a "meaning and mission" brand to a cold "make money online" audience fails — it's not a persuasion problem. It's a perception problem.

**The elevation ladder:**
- Level 150 → Offer level 200 (courage, possibility, one step up from victim mode)
- Level 200 → Offer level 275 (a working system, proof it's possible)
- Level 275 → Offer level 320 (community, genuine growth, "people like you are doing this")
- Level 320 → Offer level 380 (your next stage of capability)
- Level 380 → Offer level 420 (deeper understanding, mastery)

**When to elevate:**
- Positioning premium products and services
- Building long-term brand loyalty
- Retaining and upgrading existing customers
- Building a community with high culture

**Practical execution:**
Open with matching language (meet them in their current state), then introduce the elevated frame (show them the next step).

> "You're grinding every day and not seeing the results you expected — we know that feeling. What most people in your position discover is that it's not the effort that's wrong, it's the operating system. Here's what changes..."

The first sentence: Level 200 language (matching). The bridge: "what most people discover." The second sentence: Level 300 framing (elevating).

## Application by Format

### Ad Copy

**Awareness ads (cold traffic):** Match. Use the language of the level you're targeting. Don't elevate yet — they'll scroll past.

**Retargeting ads (warm traffic):** Slightly elevate. They know you. Use aspiration language + social proof combo.

**Conversion ads (hot traffic):** Risk removal + specificity. They're in "doing" orientation. Remove friction.

### Landing Pages

The above-the-fold section should match the energy level of the traffic source. If your ad spoke level 200 language, your headline needs to match — the energy disconnnect is felt within 2 seconds.

Body copy can graduate up. By the time you're asking for the purchase, you're painting the "doing" or "being" outcome — slightly elevated from where they landed.

**Structural rule:** Match → Bridge → Elevate → CTA

### Email Sequences

Email is the best format for elevation because you have multiple touches over time.

- **Email 1–2:** Match. Validate their current state. Show you understand where they are.
- **Email 3–4:** Bridge. Reframe the problem. Show the gap between where they are and what's possible.
- **Email 5–7:** Elevate. Paint the next level. Make the offer as the vehicle for getting there.
- **Email 8+:** Retention at elevated level. Now you're speaking to who they're becoming, not who they were when they subscribed.

**Subject line examples by level:**

Level 150–175:
> "Why you're not seeing results (and who's actually responsible)"
> "The gurus aren't telling you this"

Level 200–250:
> "The exact system that got me from $0 to $8k/month"
> "Step 1 of 5: here's where most people get stuck"

Level 300–350:
> "An honest look at what actually works"
> "What I got wrong in year one (and what I'd do differently)"

Level 400+:
> "The mechanism behind why high performers plateau — and how to break through"
> "Why the conventional wisdom on X is backwards"

### Content Strategy

Your content should speak to a specific energy band consistently. Trying to serve all levels with the same content creates noise that resonates with none.

If your brand primarily serves level 200–300:
- Case studies with step-by-step breakdowns
- "What I actually did" transparency
- Answering "how long / how much / what exactly"
- Success stories from recognizable peers
- Systems, templates, frameworks

If your brand primarily serves level 350–450:
- Analysis pieces that challenge assumptions
- Second-order thinking content
- Behind-the-scenes on philosophy and decision-making
- Honest failure/lesson content
- Long-form essays, not listicles

## The Level-Energy Brand Audit

**What energy level is your brand currently attracting?**

Run this audit:

1. **Testimonials:** What language do your customers use? Fear/craving language (level 120–175)? Process/results language (200–300)? Growth/transformation language (300+)?

2. **Comments/DMs:** What are the most common questions and complaints? "Does this actually work?" = Level 200. "How do I get this result faster?" = Level 150. "What's the thinking behind X?" = Level 400.

3. **Refund patterns:** High refunds = you're attracting or creating level 120–175 energy states in your buyers. Low refunds, high referrals = 300+.

4. **Your own copy:** What language are you using? Count your FOMO triggers, your scarcity claims, your "I made $X" screenshots. Then count your mechanism explanations, your honest tradeoffs, your mission statements. The ratio tells you who you're calling in.

5. **Platform choice:** TikTok ad spend → primarily 120–250. LinkedIn → 250–400. Substack/podcast → 350–500.

## Common Mistakes

**Mistake 1: Writing for a higher level than your audience occupies.**
You're excited about your 400-level insights, but your audience is at 200. They don't understand the framing, don't trust the messenger, and don't buy. Fix: Lead with level-200 proof, introduce the insight as the explanation.

**Mistake 2: Permanently writing for the lowest level your audience visits.**
If you always speak to the fear and craving, you attract that state and lock them there. Your LTV tanks. Your community becomes toxic. Fix: Use elevation sequencing in email. Move them up as the relationship deepens.

**Mistake 3: Confusing level with income or sophistication.**
A $200k/year executive can be operating at level 175 (pure status/pride). A $30k/year teacher can be at level 380 (genuine contribution orientation). Income is not energy level. Match the state, not the salary.

**Mistake 4: Assuming your audience stays at one level.**
People fluctuate. A customer who bought from you at level 200 two years ago may now be at 320. Your retention emails should reflect their growth, not just recycle the original pitch.

**Mistake 5: Confusing the level of your content with the level of your audience.**
You can write level-400 insights in level-250 language. This is the art form. The idea is sophisticated; the delivery is accessible. The best teachers operate at level 500 and speak to level 200. The framework is in the insight, not the vocabulary.

## Reading Your Audience's Energy Level: Quick Reference

Ask yourself: What is the dominant emotional state of a person when they first encounter my brand?

- **Desperate, losing sleep, afraid:** Level 100–125
- **Pissed off, ready to fight, blaming:** Level 150–175
- **Skeptical, needs proof, cautious:** Level 200–250
- **Ready to take steps, wants a system:** Level 250–300
- **Growing and investing in themselves:** Level 300–380
- **Asking sophisticated questions:** Level 380–450
- **Aligned with a mission:** Level 450+

Look at what content stops their scroll. Look at what questions they ask in sales calls. Look at the language in their reviews and testimonials. The emotional fingerprint is there.

| 100–150 | Fear / Desire | Pain + fantasy | "Are you tired of..." | FOMO + urgency |
| 150–190 | Anger / Pride | Us vs. them | "The system is broken..." | "Join the ones who figured it out" |
| 200–250 | Functional | Proof + steps | "Here's exactly how..." | Risk removal + social proof |
| 275–320 | Growth | Challenge + community | "If you're ready to level up..." | "People like you are building this" |
| 350–400 | Contribution | Honest + nuanced | "The real reason most people..." | "Apply to work together" |
| 400–450 | Reason | Data + mechanism | "The research shows..." | "Let's figure out your situation" |
| 450–500 | Purpose | Mission + meaning | "What we're building..." | "Join us" |

*The goal isn't to stay at whatever level your audience is. The goal is to meet them there, then pull them one level higher. That's how you build an audience that grows with you — and stays.*
