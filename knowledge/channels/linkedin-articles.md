# LinkedIn Articles Channel Guide

> **Use when:** Publishing long-form content on LinkedIn for thought leadership, SEO visibility, and professional authority building.

## Quick Reference

| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| **Length** | 1,200-2,000 words |
| **Headline** | Under 50 characters |
| **Subheadings** | Every 200-300 words |
| **Hashtags** | 3-5 relevant tags |
| **Best days** | Tuesday-Thursday |
| **Best time** | 9-11 AM ET |

---

## Articles vs Posts: When to Use Each

| Format | Best For | Reach Pattern | SEO Value |
|--------|----------|---------------|-----------|
| **Articles** | Thought leadership, complex topics, evergreen content | Indexed by Google, lives on profile permanently | High - ranks in search |
| **Posts** | Quick insights, engagement, community building | Flash engagement (24hrs), algorithm-driven | None |

**Key insight**: Articles receive **7x more views** than standard posts but require higher value density. Short-form content gets you found; long-form content builds your reputation.

## The LinkedIn Article Algorithm (2025-2026)

LinkedIn's algorithm prioritizes:

1. **Expertise signals** - Original insights over rehashed advice
2. **Conversation quality** - Content that sparks meaningful discussion
3. **Contextual relevance** - Industry-specific value
4. **On-platform engagement** - Penalizes external links by ~50%
5. **Dwell time** - How long readers stay on your article

### What Kills Reach

- External links in article body (use first comment instead)
- More than 5 hashtags
- Generic "thought leadership" without substance
- Dense walls of text
- Clickbait headlines that don't deliver

## Article Structure Template

### 1. Headline (Under 50 Characters)

Apply algorithmic authorship principles:

```
Wrong: "If You Want to Succeed in Sales, Read This"
Right: "Close More Deals by Asking This One Question"

Wrong: "Why I Think AI Will Change Marketing"
Right: "AI Changes Marketing: 3 Shifts Happening Now"
```

**Headline formulas that work:**
- [Number] + [Outcome]: "3 Pricing Mistakes Killing Your Deals"
- [Action] + [Benefit]: "Stop Losing Clients to This Common Error"
- [Contrarian take]: "Cold Email Isn't Dead—You're Doing It Wrong"

### 2. Opening Hook (First 200 Characters)

The first 200 characters appear before "See more" and determine whether readers click. The algorithm tracks this engagement signal.

**Hook patterns:**

| Type | Example |
|------|---------|
| **Problem** | "Most B2B sales teams waste 40% of their time on unqualified leads. The fix takes 15 minutes." |
| **Contrarian** | "Everyone says follow up 7 times. The data says that's wrong." |
| **Story** | "Last quarter, I lost our biggest client. What happened next changed how I sell." |
| **Data** | "We analyzed 10,000 cold emails. Only 3% got responses. Here's what they had in common." |

### 3. Body Structure

Use subheadings every 200-300 words. Each section follows this pattern:

```
[Subheading - clear benefit or topic]

[Opening sentence - main point]

[Supporting evidence - data, example, or story]

[Actionable takeaway - what reader does next]
```

### 4. Closing & CTA

End with a clear next step:
- "Share your experience in the comments"
- "DM me for the template"
- "Save this for your next [situation]"

## Algorithmic Authorship Rules for LinkedIn

These rules optimize content for both LinkedIn's algorithm and Google indexing. Apply them to maximize discoverability.

### Sentence Structure

**Rule 1: Conditions AFTER main clause**
```
Wrong: "If you want more engagement, post on Tuesdays."
Right: "Post on Tuesdays if you want more engagement."

Wrong: "Because budgets are tight, focus on organic."
Right: "Focus on organic because budgets are tight."
```

**Rule 2: Start instructions with verbs**
```
Wrong: "Carefully review your analytics."
Right: "Review your analytics carefully."

Wrong: "Quickly respond to comments."
Right: "Respond to comments quickly."
```

**Rule 3: Use short, declarative sentences**
```
Wrong: "When it comes to LinkedIn articles, which have been shown to perform better than posts for long-term visibility, the optimal length appears to be somewhere between 1,200 and 2,000 words."

Right: "LinkedIn articles outperform posts for long-term visibility. The optimal length is 1,200-2,000 words."
```

### List & Formatting Rules

**Rule 4: Use numbered lists for steps/methods, bullets for types**
```
Steps to write a LinkedIn article:
1. Research your topic
2. Outline key points
3. Write the draft
4. Add subheadings
5. Edit for clarity

Types of LinkedIn content:
- Articles (long-form)
- Posts (short-form)
- Newsletters (subscriber-based)
- Documents (carousel format)
```

**Rule 5: Same part of speech in list items**
```
Good (all verbs):
1. Research competitors
2. Identify gaps
3. Write unique angles
4. Publish consistently

Bad (mixed):
1. Research competitors
2. Gap identification
3. Write unique angles
4. Consistency in publishing
```

**Rule 6: Same phrase pattern in list elements**
```
Good (Verb + Object pattern):
1. Research your competitors thoroughly
2. Identify their content gaps
3. Write differentiated angles
4. Publish on optimal days

Bad (inconsistent patterns):
1. Research competitors
2. Gaps should be identified
3. Write unique angles
4. Publishing consistency matters
```

### Entity & Anchor Rules

**Rule 7: Name entities twice before switching to attributes**
```
Wrong: "LinkedIn articles are powerful. They get indexed by Google."

Right: "LinkedIn articles are powerful for thought leadership. LinkedIn articles get indexed by Google. The SEO benefit of LinkedIn articles makes them valuable for long-term discovery."
```

**Rule 8: Use anchor words between sequential sentences**
```
Wrong: "Subheadings improve readability. Use them every 200-300 words."

Right: "Subheadings improve readability. These subheadings should appear every 200-300 words. The frequency of subheadings affects both engagement and SEO."
```

**Rule 9: Use different lemmatized versions of key terms**
```
"Publishing LinkedIn articles requires understanding the algorithm. To publish effectively, analyze what performs well. The publishing frequency should be 1-2 articles per month."
(publishing → publish → publishing)
```

### Evidence & Formatting Rules

**Rule 10: Use numeric values, not vague quantifiers**
```
Wrong: "LinkedIn articles get many more views than posts."
Right: "LinkedIn articles get 7x more views than posts."

Wrong: "Post during peak hours for better engagement."
Right: "Post between 9-11 AM ET on Tuesday-Thursday for 23% higher engagement."
```

**Rule 11: Always use examples after declarations**
```
Wrong: "Use strong hooks in your opening."
Right: "Use strong hooks in your opening. For example: 'We analyzed 10,000 cold emails. Only 3% got responses.'"
```

**Rule 12: Bold the ANSWER, not the query terms**
```
Wrong: "The **optimal length** for LinkedIn articles is 1,200-2,000 words."
Right: "The optimal length for LinkedIn articles is **1,200-2,000 words**."
```

### Internal Linking Rules (For Articles That Link Out)

**Rule 13: Don't link in the first sentence of a paragraph**

The first sentence establishes context. Links disrupt this.

```
Wrong: "[Effective headlines](link) determine whether readers click."

Right: "Headlines determine whether readers click. The psychology of headline writing involves pattern interrupts and curiosity gaps. Learn more about [headline psychology](link) in our guide."
```

**Rule 14: Explain the connection before linking**

Provide context before any link so readers understand why they should click.

**Rule 15: Place external links in first comment, not article body**

LinkedIn penalizes off-platform traffic by ~50%. Always put links in the first comment and mention "Link in comments" at the end of your article.

- [ ] Target keyword in first 200 characters

- [ ] Descriptive subheadings with keyword variants
- [ ] Alt text on images (if applicable)

## Publishing Strategy

### Timing

| Day | Performance |
|-----|-------------|
| **Tuesday** | Highest engagement |
| **Wednesday** | Strong performance |
| **Thursday** | Good engagement |
| **Monday** | Moderate |
| **Friday-Sunday** | Lower engagement |

**Best time**: 9-11 AM ET (when professionals check LinkedIn)

### Frequency

- **1-2 articles per month** for quality
- Consistency matters more than volume
- Each article should justify the reader's time investment

### Promotion Strategy

1. **First hour is critical** - Engage with every comment immediately
2. **Cross-post a teaser** - Share a short post linking to the article
3. **Tag relevant people** - Only if genuinely relevant to them
4. **Share in relevant groups** - Add context, don't just drop links
5. **Repurpose** - Turn key points into separate posts over the following weeks

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

| Mistake | Fix |
|---------|-----|
| Dense paragraphs | Break into 2-4 sentences |
| Generic opening | Lead with data, story, or contrarian take |
| No subheadings | Add every 200-300 words |
| External links in body | Move to first comment |
| Too many hashtags | Limit to 3-5 |
| Vague claims | Add specific numbers and examples |
| AI-sounding phrases | Remove "In conclusion", "It's worth noting" |
| No CTA | End with clear next step |
| Publishing randomly | Schedule for Tuesday-Thursday mornings |
| Ignoring comments | Respond within first hour |
