Your podcast will be published on YouTube, Spotify, Apple Podcasts, and across social media. High-quality audio and video help establish your authority and make your content more engaging for potential clients searching for expertise.

### Location (Background)

Your recording location should feel professional and authentically you.

**Good backgrounds:**
- Your office with bookshelves, law degrees, or legal books visible
- Clean desk with minimal clutter
- Professional artwork or firm branding in the background
- Natural office setting that represents your practice

**Poor backgrounds:**
- Messy or disorganized spaces
- Bright windows directly behind you (creates silhouette effect)
- Distracting movement in the background
- Shared spaces
- Loud areas

> **Note:** It doesn't need to be perfect. It needs to look like you and your practice. We'll review your background together on the kickoff call and make any adjustments needed.

## Microphone Setup

### Step 1: Unbox your Shure MV7

### Step 2: Connect the USB cable
Connect the included USB cable from the microphone to your computer.

### Step 3: Position the microphone
Place the microphone on your desk or attach to a boom arm (optional).

### Step 4: Automatic detection
The microphone will automatically be detected by your computer - no software installation needed.

### Step 5: Final positioning
Position the microphone **6-8 inches from your mouth**, slightly off to the side (not directly in front of your face blocking the camera).

> **Note:** The Shure MV7 has built-in auto-adjustment and echo cancellation. You don't need to change any settings.

---

## Lighting Setup

### Step 1: Position your light
Place your ring light or video light directly in front of you, above or beside your computer screen.

### Step 2: Angle the light
Position it so the light is shining on your face directly or just above your eye line (not behind you or to the side).

### Step 3: Adjust brightness
Adjust brightness to a comfortable level - you should be well-lit but not washed out.

### Step 4: Test your setup
Test by opening your computer's camera app and checking how you look on screen.

> **Pro Tip:** Natural light from a window in front of you is also great. If you have good natural light, you may not need additional lighting - we'll evaluate this together on the equipment check call.

## Quality Checklists

### Audio Quality Check

Before your first recording, confirm:

- [ ] Microphone is plugged into your computer via USB
- [ ] Microphone is positioned 6-8 inches from your mouth
- [ ] You're in a quiet room (close windows, turn off AC/fans if possible during recording)
- [ ] Microphone is selected as your audio input in your computer's settings

### Video Quality Check

Before your first recording, confirm:

- [ ] Your computer's built-in camera is clean (wipe with soft cloth if dusty)
- [ ] Camera is positioned at eye level (use a laptop stand or stack of books if needed)
- [ ] Your face is well-lit from the front (not backlit)
- [ ] Background is clean and professional
- [ ] You can see yourself clearly in your computer's camera preview

## Live Streaming Engagement

> **Use when:** Running live streams on Twitter/X, YouTube, Twitch, or similar platforms.

### The Only Goal: Retention

Your only job during a live stream is to get viewers to stay as long as possible. Viewer retention is the primary signal that determines algorithmic boost for replays and recommendations.

### Viewer Retention Tactics

| Tactic | How to Execute |
|--------|----------------|
| **Acknowledge viewers** | When you see the viewer count go up, acknowledge it: "We got someone new, welcome" |
| **Rapidfire topics** | If engagement drops, immediately pivot to a new topic |
| **Every sentence is a hook** | Talk show hosts script each sentence as standalone hook - study them |
| **Stop-scroll value** | Always be in the middle of something engaging OR have something visually interesting happening |

### Why Retention Matters More Than Content

"People care about live streamers when they're really famous. They don't really care about them before that."

**Before you're famous:** Content must carry you. Viewers stay for:
- Stories they can't stop listening to
- Visual chaos (what's happening?!)
- Controversy/hot takes
- Actionable alpha

**After you're famous:** Viewers stay for YOU. But you have to earn that first.

### Engagement Signals to Watch

| Platform | Can You See Who Joins? | Engagement Indicator |
|----------|------------------------|---------------------|
| Twitter/X Live | No (only count) | Watch the number go up |
| Twitter/X Spaces | Yes (shows names) | Acknowledge by name |
| YouTube Live | Yes (chat shows joins) | Call out usernames |
| Twitch | Yes (chat shows joins) | Call out usernames |

If you can't see who joins (like Twitter Live Video), react to the COUNT: "Oh we just got 3 more people, let me restart this point for you..."

### Stream Structure for Maximum Retention

| Time | Content Type | Priority |
|------|--------------|----------|
| 0:00-2:00 | **Hot open hook** - Most controversial/interesting point first | CRITICAL |
| 2:00-15:00 | Topic 1 with pattern interrupts every 3-5 min | HIGH |
| 15:00-30:00 | Topic 2 - Pivot if engagement drops | HIGH |
| 30:00+ | Q&A or continued topics | MEDIUM |

**Pattern interrupt examples:**
- "Let me show you something wild..."
- "Actually, wait - this is more interesting..."
- "I wasn't going to share this but..."
- Switch to screen share / demo
- Bring up a chart / image

### Timestamping During Streams (Critical for Clips)

Keep a note doc open during the stream. Mark timestamps in real-time:
- `6:00` - Hot take on [topic]
- `12:00` - [Guest name] drops alpha
- `18:00` - Stack demo started
- `25:00` - Controversy about [topic]

**Even rough timestamps like "6 min, 12 min, 18 min" save hours of post-production time.**

### Stream Frequency Optimization

| Frequency | Data Required | Action |
|-----------|---------------|--------|
| Daily (starting out) | Need 1 month data | Required to understand patterns |
| 4-5 days/week | After 1 month | Cut lowest-performing days |
| 3 days/week | After 3 months | Focus on proven time slots |

**Skip Fridays** if doing afternoon streams - markets closed, crypto audience disengaged.
