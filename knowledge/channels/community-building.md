# Community Building Channel Guide

> **Use when:** Building owned communities on Discord, Slack, Facebook Groups, or forums. Also covers community-led growth strategy.

---

## Why Community in 2026

- **Owned audience** — not subject to algorithm changes (unlike social media)
- **Highest retention channel** — community members churn 30-50% less
- **User-generated content** — community creates content for you
- **Product feedback loop** — direct access to user needs and frustrations
- **Network effects** — each new member makes the community more valuable
- **Competitor moat** — hard to replicate (takes time to build)

## Platform Selection

| Platform | Best For | Size Sweet Spot | Effort |
|----------|----------|----------------|--------|
| **Discord** | Tech, gaming, creator communities | 100-10K | Medium |
| **Slack** | B2B, professional communities | 50-5K | Medium |
| **Facebook Groups** | B2C, local, niche interests | 100-50K | Low |
| **Circle** | Course/membership communities | 50-2K | Low |
| **Reddit** | Niche topic authority | N/A (subreddit) | High |
| **Community forums** (Discourse) | Deep technical discussions | 100-10K | High |
| **WhatsApp/Telegram** | Small, high-engagement groups | 20-250 | Low |

### Decision Framework
```
B2B SaaS → Slack (professional context, integrations)
B2C product → Discord or Facebook Group (wider reach)
Course/membership → Circle (built for paid communities)
Technical product → Discord or Discourse (code blocks, threads)
Local business → Facebook Group or WhatsApp
```

## Community Architecture

### Channel/Category Structure (Discord/Slack)

```
CATEGORY: WELCOME
  #welcome          — Auto-greet, rules, intro prompt
  #introductions    — Members introduce themselves (template provided)
  #announcements    — Official updates (read-only)

CATEGORY: DISCUSSION
  #general          — Open conversation
  #wins             — Celebrate member achievements
  #questions        — Help each other (encourage member-to-member answers)
  #feedback         — Product suggestions and bug reports

CATEGORY: RESOURCES
  #learning         — Guides, tutorials, templates
  #tools            — Tool recommendations
  #job-board        — Relevant opportunities

CATEGORY: EVENTS
  #events           — Upcoming calls, webinars, AMAs
  #recordings       — Past event recordings

CATEGORY: ADMIN (visible to mods only)
  #mod-chat         — Moderation discussions
  #flagged          — Content flagged for review
```

### The 10-Channel Rule
Never launch with more than 10 channels. Empty channels feel dead. Start with 5-7, add more when existing channels are consistently active.

## Community Growth Stages

### Stage 1: Seeding (0-100 members)

**Goal:** Create the initial spark of activity

- Personally invite your 20-30 most engaged customers
- Be in the community daily, starting every conversation yourself
- Post 3-5 discussions per day (you'll carry most conversations initially)
- **Do not promote publicly** until there's existing activity to discover
- Use the "campfire" approach: small, intimate, high-quality conversations

**Metrics:** Daily active members (DAM), messages/day

### Stage 2: Growing (100-1,000 members)

**Goal:** Build self-sustaining conversations

- Activate member-to-member interactions (don't answer every question yourself)
- Identify and empower "super members" (5-10 people who are always helpful)
- Add community rituals: weekly threads, monthly challenges, member spotlights
- Start promoting: mention community in email signatures, onboarding, social media
- Create content from community discussions (turns community into content engine)

**Metrics:** Messages per member, % members who post (not just lurk)

### Stage 3: Scaling (1,000+)

**Goal:** Systematize and delegate

- Appoint moderators (volunteer or paid) — 1 mod per 200-500 active members
- Create onboarding flow: welcome DM → intro prompt → first-week checklist
- Automate what you can: welcome messages, role assignment, FAQ bot
- Run regular events: AMAs, workshops, guest speakers
- Community-sourced content: case studies, testimonials, user guides

**Metrics:** Retention (30-day active), engagement rate, NPS of community

## Engagement Tactics

### Daily / Weekly Rituals

| Ritual | Frequency | Example |
|--------|-----------|---------|
| **Welcome new members** | Daily | Automated welcome + personal follow-up DM |
| **Discussion prompt** | Daily | "What's one thing you learned this week?" |
| **Wins thread** | Weekly | "Share your wins from this week — big or small" |
| **AMA** | Biweekly | Founder, customer, or industry expert Q&A |
| **Challenge** | Monthly | "30-day [topic] challenge" with daily prompts |
| **Member spotlight** | Weekly | Interview or feature one community member |
| **Resource roundup** | Weekly | Curated tools, articles, templates |

### The 3 Types of Community Members

```
CREATORS (5-10%): Create original content, start discussions
  → Reward: moderator status, early access, direct line to founders

CONTRIBUTORS (20-30%): Reply, share, help others
  → Reward: recognition, badges, shoutouts

LURKERS (60-70%): Read but don't post
  → Strategy: make it easy to contribute (reactions, polls, quick questions)
  → Don't pressure — lurkers still get value and may become customers
```

### Content from Community (the Content Engine)

Turn community activity into marketing content:
1. **FAQ → Blog posts**: Common questions become SEO articles
2. **Wins → Case studies**: Member success stories become testimonials
3. **Discussions → Social posts**: Interesting threads become LinkedIn/X content
4. **Feedback → Product updates**: Feature requests inform product roadmap
5. **Expert answers → Guides**: Top responses become knowledge base articles

## Community-Led Growth

### The Flywheel

```
COMMUNITY creates CONTENT
  → Content attracts NEW MEMBERS
  → New members create MORE CONTENT
  → More content attracts MORE MEMBERS
  → Members become CUSTOMERS
  → Customers refer FRIENDS
  → Friends join the COMMUNITY
  → Loop accelerates
```

### Community as a Product Feature

| Strategy | Example |
|----------|---------|
| **Community onboarding** | "Join our community of 2,000+ [role]" in product onboarding |
| **In-app community link** | Button in product that opens community |
| **Community-exclusive features** | Beta access, templates, tools only in community |
| **Support via community** | Peer support reduces support costs by 30-50% |
| **Community-gated content** | Best resources require community membership |

## Moderation

### Community Rules Template

```
COMMUNITY RULES
1. Be respectful. Disagree with ideas, not people.
2. No spam or self-promotion (except in designated channels).
3. No hate speech, harassment, or discrimination.
4. Stay on topic in specific channels.
5. Don't share others' private information.
6. Ask before DMing people unsolicited sales pitches.
7. When asking for help, provide context (what you tried, what happened).
```

### Moderation Workflow

```
REPORTED CONTENT
  │
  ├── Clear violation (spam, hate speech)
  │   → Remove immediately + warn user
  │   → 3 warnings = ban
  │
  ├── Grey area (self-promotion, off-topic)
  │   → Move to appropriate channel or DM user privately
  │   → "Hey, this is great but belongs in #resources"
  │
  └── Not a violation
      → No action, optionally respond to reporter
```

## Metrics

| Metric | Formula | Healthy | Concerning |
|--------|---------|---------|-----------|
| DAM (Daily Active Members) | Unique members active/day | 10-20% of total | < 5% |
| Message rate | Messages per day | Growing week-over-week | Declining |
| Creator ratio | Members who posted / total | 5-10% | < 2% |
| Response time | Avg time to first reply on questions | < 4 hours | > 24 hours |
| 30-day retention | Members active after 30 days / joined | 40-60% | < 25% |
| Member-to-member ratio | Conversations between members (not staff) | > 60% | < 30% |
| NPS | "How likely to recommend this community?" | 50+ | < 30 |
