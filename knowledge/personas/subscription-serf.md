# Persona: The Subscription Serf

> **Use when:** Targeting consumers frustrated by dark patterns, or marketing financial tools, comparison services, or consumer advocacy.

## Profile

**Who:** High-income adults who feel nickel-and-dimed
**Age:** 30-55
**Income:** Above median but feels squeezed
**Frustration:** Fighting predatory pricing and friction-designed billing

---

## Core Psychology

Companies have **weaponized friction**: frictionless to pay/subscribe, impossibly high-friction to cancel/refund. This "Roach Motel" design is revenue strategy called "breakage" - revenue from customers who give up trying to claim what they're owed.

In healthcare: "Deny, Delay, Defend" is standard for insurance. Algorithms deny claims in batches, betting patients won't appeal.

---

## Top 10 Pain Points

1. **Deny, Delay, Defend** - Insurance denies coverage for standard procedure via automated system. 10 hours on phone to overturn. Company knows 40% give up and pay.

2. **Out-of-Network Ambush** - In-network hospital, in-network surgeon. Anesthesiologist (met for 30 seconds while sedated) is out-of-network. $4,000 surprise bill.

3. **Gym Cancellation Quest** - Sign up in one click. Cancel requires certified letter via mail OR in-person between 10am-2pm on Tuesday to speak with manager who's never there.

4. **Zombie Subscription** - Canceled free trial. Three months later, charge appears. Company claims didn't "confirm" via email that went to spam. No refund button, only chatbot loop.

5. **Upcoding Surprise** - Routine physical (100% covered). Asked ONE question about sore knee. Billing code changes to "diagnostic." $300 bill.

6. **Dynamic Pricing Gouge** - Checks flight on laptop. Switches to phone. Price up $50. Algorithm tracking desperation and pricing accordingly.

7. **Prior Authorization Purgatory** - Doctor prescribes medication. Insurance demands trying (and failing) on three cheaper drugs first. Must get sicker before allowed to get better.

8. **Resort Fee of Life** - Book hotel at listed price. Checkout adds "Destination Fee," "Service Fee," "Admin Fee" totaling 30% more.

9. **Car Dealership Hostage** - Agrees on car price. Finance office adds "Nitrogen Tires" ($400) and "VIN Etching" ($300) or deal's off. Five hours in, just signs to escape.

10. **Warranty Void Lie** - Sticker says "Warranty Void if Removed." Illegal under Magnuson-Moss Act, but company refuses repair anyway, daring consumer to sue over $200.

---

## "This Feels Rigged Because..."

- **Asymmetric Friction:** One click to pay, ten steps to refund/cancel
- **The Breakage Model:** Your time is worth more than the $50 error. Friction IS the revenue stream.
- **Algorithmic Cruelty:** Prices set by maximum you'll pay before walking away, not by value
- **The Fine Print Trap:** Contract waived right to class-action, forcing arbitration where company wins 98%

|------------|----------|
| "I make too much money to be this stressed about $40, but it's the principle" | "I'd like to understand this charge" |

## Content Hooks

### Headlines
- "The $40 Fee They're Betting You Won't Fight"
- "How Companies Design Cancellation to Be Impossible"
- "What Your Insurance Company Hopes You Never Learn"
- "The 'Breakage' Economy: Why They Want You to Give Up"

### Opening Lines
- "They're not counting on your money. They're counting on your exhaustion."
- "The cancel button exists. It's just hidden behind 47 steps."
- "That surprise bill? The algorithm sent it to thousands of people betting most won't appeal."

### Validation Angle
The **"Tax on Existence."** Appeals to sense of justice. Show the specific mechanism (how cancel button is hidden in code, the exact phrase to stop retention script) gives them a target for anger and a "cheat code."

## Why This Persona Converts

- **Has disposable income** but hates wasting it
- **Will pay for convenience** that actually delivers
- **Values transparency** - anti-dark-pattern positioning works
- **Strong word-of-mouth** - tells everyone when they find good solutions
