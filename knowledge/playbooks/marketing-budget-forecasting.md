# Marketing Budget & Forecasting Playbook

> **Use when:** Setting marketing budgets, forecasting revenue from marketing, justifying spend to leadership, or allocating across channels.

---

## Budget Benchmarks by Stage

| Stage | Revenue | Marketing % of Revenue | Monthly Budget |
|-------|---------|----------------------|---------------|
| Pre-revenue | $0 | N/A | $0-500 (founder time) |
| Early ($0-$100K ARR) | $0-8K MRR | 30-50% of revenue | $500-4K/mo |
| Growth ($100K-$1M ARR) | $8K-83K MRR | 15-30% of revenue | $4K-25K/mo |
| Scale ($1M-$10M ARR) | $83K-833K MRR | 10-20% of revenue | $25K-170K/mo |
| Mature ($10M+ ARR) | $833K+ MRR | 8-15% of revenue | $170K+/mo |

**SaaS-specific:** 2026 median marketing spend for high-growth SaaS is 20-30% of revenue. Enterprise-focused companies spend more on sales (lower marketing %); PLG companies spend more on marketing (higher %).

**DTC/E-commerce:** Typically 15-30% of revenue, weighted heavily toward paid acquisition and email.

## Budget Allocation Framework

### The 70/20/10 Rule

```
70% — PROVEN CHANNELS
  Channels with demonstrated ROI
  Optimize and scale what works
  Predictable spend → predictable pipeline

20% — TESTING
  New channels or tactics being validated
  Enough budget to reach statistical significance
  Promote to "proven" or kill after 60-90 days

10% — EXPERIMENTAL
  High-risk, high-reward bets
  Brand campaigns, PR, events, new platforms
  Accept that most won't pay off directly
```

## Revenue Forecasting

### The Pipeline Math

```
TARGET: $100K new MRR this quarter

WORK BACKWARD:
  Close rate:          25%
  → Need 400K in pipeline

  Opportunity rate:    30% of SQLs → opportunity
  → Need 1,333 SQLs

  SQL rate:            25% of MQLs → SQL
  → Need 5,333 MQLs

  MQL rate:            5% of visitors → MQL
  → Need 106,660 visitors

  Cost per visitor:    $2 blended
  → Need $213,320 in marketing spend

  OR:
  Cost per MQL:        $40
  → Need $213,320 in marketing spend

SANITY CHECK:
  Is $213K spend for $100K MRR reasonable?
  If MRR retains at 90%/year, LTV per customer ≈ $1,200
  At $100K MRR / $500 ARPU = 200 new customers
  CAC = $213K / 200 = $1,065
  LTV:CAC = $1,200 / $1,065 = 1.13:1 ← NOT GOOD

  Fix: improve conversion rates or reduce CAC
  If close rate → 35%: need 285K pipeline → 952 SQLs → 3,810 MQLs → $152K spend
  → CAC = $760, LTV:CAC = 1.58:1 ← better but still needs work
```

## ROI Calculation by Channel

### How to Calculate Marketing ROI

```
SIMPLE ROI:
  ROI = (Revenue from channel - Cost of channel) / Cost of channel × 100

  Example: Meta Ads spent $10K, generated $35K revenue
  ROI = ($35K - $10K) / $10K × 100 = 250% ROI

ROAS (Return on Ad Spend):
  ROAS = Revenue / Ad Spend

  Example: $35K / $10K = 3.5x ROAS

FULLY-LOADED ROI (includes labor + tools):
  Cost = Ad spend + team salaries (prorated) + tools + creative production
  Revenue = All revenue attributed to that channel (including assisted)
```

## Budget Defense Framework

### How to Justify Marketing Budget to Leadership

**Frame 1: Cost of NOT spending**
"If we reduce marketing by $X, we'll lose approximately $Y in pipeline based on our current conversion rates. That's $Z in lost revenue over 12 months."

**Frame 2: Unit economics**
"Every $1 we spend generates $X in pipeline. Our LTV:CAC is Y:1. We're not spending enough — increasing budget by 20% should yield 15% more revenue at the same efficiency."

**Frame 3: Competitive context**
"Competitor X is spending $Y/month on ads alone (visible via ad library). If we don't invest, we cede market share."

**Frame 4: CAC payback**
"Our CAC payback is X months. Every customer pays for themselves in X months, then generates profit for the remaining Y months of their lifetime."
