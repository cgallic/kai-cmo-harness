# SEO Link Building Playbook

> **Use when:** Building backlinks to improve domain authority and search rankings. Covers ethical tactics only — no PBNs, link farms, or schemes.

---

## Link Building Hierarchy (by Quality)

```
HIGHEST VALUE:
  1. Earned editorial links (journalist links to your content naturally)
  2. Data/research citations (original research gets cited)
  3. Resource page links (your tool/guide listed as a resource)
  4. Guest posts on high-DA sites (byline with contextual link)
  5. Broken link building (replace dead links with your content)
  6. HARO/journalist queries (expert quotes with attribution)
  7. Directory links (niche directories, not generic)
  8. Partner/client links (natural mentions in partner content)

LOWEST VALUE (but still useful):
  9. Social profile links
  10. Forum/community links (when genuinely helpful)
```

---

## Tactic 1: Original Research & Data

**Why it works:** Original data is the most linkable asset. Journalists, bloggers, and other sites need sources to cite. Be the source.

**Playbook:**
1. Identify a question your industry asks but nobody has data for
2. Collect the data (survey your users, analyze your product data, scrape public data)
3. Publish a "State of [Industry] Report" with charts, key findings, methodology
4. Pitch the findings to 20-50 journalists who cover your space
5. Create shareable charts/infographics that carry your branding

**Expected links:** 10-100+ depending on the data's novelty and your outreach quality.

**Example:**
"We analyzed 10,000 law firm calls. Here's what we found: 47% go to voicemail after 5pm. The average missed call costs $450 in potential revenue."

### Data-Driven Content Ideas
- Annual industry report ("State of [X] 2026")
- Benchmark study ("Average [metric] across [N] companies")
- Survey results ("We surveyed 500 [role]. Here's what they said.")
- Product usage data ("Our users' most common [behavior]")
- Cost analysis ("The true cost of [common business problem]")

## Tactic 2: Digital PR

**Why it works:** One press mention from a high-DA site can be worth 50 guest posts.

**Playbook:**
1. Create a newsworthy angle (data, trend, prediction, contrarian take)
2. Write a press release or pitch email (see `playbooks/pr-comms-strategy.md`)
3. Pitch to journalists covering your vertical
4. Offer yourself as an expert source for future stories

**Expected links:** 5-30 per campaign, from DA 50-90 sites.

**Key:** The story must be genuinely newsworthy. "Company launches product" gets zero links. "Company's data reveals surprising trend" gets links.

## Tactic 3: Guest Posting

**Why it works:** You provide valuable content, they give you a link and exposure to their audience.

**Outreach Template:**
```
Subject: Guest post idea for [Publication]: [Specific title]

Hi [name],

I noticed [specific recent article they published] and thought your readers
might also find value in [specific topic with a unique angle].

I'd love to contribute a guest post titled: "[Specific title]"

It would cover:
- [Point 1 — unique insight or data]
- [Point 2 — practical takeaway]
- [Point 3 — actionable framework]

About me: [1 sentence credentials]. I've written for [2-3 publications].

Happy to send a draft outline if you're interested.

[Name]
```

**Targeting Criteria:**
- DA 40+ (below this, the link value is marginal)
- Relevant to your niche (topical relevance > raw DA)
- Active blog with regular publishing (not abandoned)
- Allows dofollow links (check existing guest posts)
- Real audience engagement (comments, social shares)

## Tactic 4: Broken Link Building

**Why it works:** You find broken links on relevant sites and offer your content as a replacement. The site owner gets a fixed link, you get a backlink. Win-win.

**Process:**
1. Find resource pages in your niche (`"best [topic] resources" + inurl:resources`)
2. Check for broken links (use Ahrefs Broken Link Checker or Check My Links extension)
3. Create content that matches what the broken link pointed to
4. Email the site owner: "I noticed a broken link on your [page]. I have a resource that might be a good replacement."

**Outreach Template:**
```
Subject: Broken link on your [page name]

Hi [name],

I was reading your [resource page / article] and noticed the link to
[anchor text] is broken (leads to a 404).

I recently published [title] which covers the same topic. Would you
consider linking to it as a replacement?

[URL]

Either way, wanted to give you a heads up about the dead link.

[Name]
```

## Tactic 5: HARO / Connectively / Expert Quotes

**Why it works:** Journalists need expert sources. You provide quotes, they link to you.

**Daily Routine (15 min):**
1. Check HARO emails (3x/day: 5:35 AM, 12:35 PM, 5:35 PM ET)
2. Filter for queries relevant to your expertise
3. Respond within 2 hours (speed wins)
4. Provide a specific, quotable answer (not a generic pitch)
5. Include credentials and link to your site

**Response Template:**
```
Source: [Your name], [Title] at [Company] ([URL])

[2-3 sentence expert response — specific, quotable, with data if possible]

[1 sentence bio with relevant credential]

Happy to elaborate or provide additional data.
```

**Expected links:** 2-5 per month from DA 50-80+ sites.

## Tactic 6: Resource Page Link Building

**How to find targets:**
```
Google searches:
  "[topic] resources"
  "[topic] links"
  "[topic] useful sites"
  "best [topic] tools"
  inurl:resources [topic]
  intitle:"useful links" [topic]
```

**Outreach:** Short, direct, explain why your resource belongs on their page.

## Tactic 7: Linkable Assets

Create assets designed to earn links naturally:

| Asset Type | Link Potential | Effort | Example |
|-----------|---------------|--------|---------|
| Free tool/calculator | Very high | High | "ROI calculator for [service]" |
| Original research | Very high | High | "State of [industry] report" |
| Comprehensive guide | High | Medium | "The complete guide to [topic]" |
| Infographic | Medium | Medium | Data visualization of key findings |
| Template/checklist | Medium | Low | "Free [task] checklist template" |
| Statistics page | High | Medium | "[Topic] statistics 2026" |
| Glossary/definitions | Medium | Low | "[Industry] glossary: 50 terms defined" |

## Link Building Metrics

| Metric | What It Measures | Good | Excellent |
|--------|-----------------|------|-----------|
| Referring domains | Unique sites linking to you | Growing MoM | 50+ new/month |
| Domain Rating/Authority | Overall link strength | DR 30-50 | DR 50+ |
| Link velocity | New links per month | Consistent | Accelerating |
| Link quality | Average DA of linking sites | DA 30+ | DA 50+ |
| Anchor text diversity | % branded vs keyword vs generic | Mixed | Natural distribution |
| Referral traffic | Visits from backlinks | Any | > 5% of total traffic |

---

## What to Avoid

| Tactic | Why It's Risky | Penalty Risk |
|--------|---------------|-------------|
| PBN (Private Blog Networks) | Google detects and penalizes | High |
| Paid links (undisclosed) | Violates Google guidelines | High |
| Link exchanges ("I'll link to you if you link to me") | Detected at scale | Medium |
| Low-quality directories | No value, spam signal | Low-Medium |
| Automated link building tools | Creates obvious patterns | High |
| Comment spam | Zero link value, damages reputation | Low (but useless) |
| Article spinning / syndication networks | Duplicate content, thin links | Medium |

**Rule:** If you wouldn't want a Google reviewer to see your link building spreadsheet, don't do it.

## Monthly Link Building Calendar

| Week | Activity | Target |
|------|----------|--------|
| Week 1 | HARO responses (daily) + 1 guest post pitch | 3-5 HARO responses, 1 pitch |
| Week 2 | Broken link building campaign (10 prospects) | 5-10 outreach emails |
| Week 3 | Create/update one linkable asset | 1 tool, guide, or data page |
| Week 4 | Outreach for linkable asset + resource page pitches | 15-20 outreach emails |

**Expected results after 6 months:**
- 30-50 new referring domains
- DR improvement of 5-15 points
- 2-5 high-DA links (DA 60+) from digital PR
- Steady referral traffic stream from guest posts
