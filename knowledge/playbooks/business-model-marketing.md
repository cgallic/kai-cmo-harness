# Business Model Marketing Playbook

> **Use when:** Developing marketing strategy for B2B SaaS, DTC E-commerce, Fintech, Crypto/Web3, or Marketplace businesses. Contains benchmarks, channel priorities, and tactics specific to each model.

---

## Quick Reference by Model

| Model | North Star | Critical Constraint | Primary Lever |
|-------|------------|---------------------|---------------|
| **B2B SaaS** | Pipeline Velocity, Magic Number | MQL-to-SQL handoff | Demand Gen vs Lead Gen |
| **DTC E-commerce** | Contribution Margin, 60-Day LTV | Rising CAC, Trend Loyalty | Creative Velocity, Retention |
| **Fintech** | CAC vs Compliance Cost, Activation | Trust Deficit, Regulation | Education, Embedded Partnerships |
| **Crypto/Web3** | Active Wallets, Community | Decentralization vs Execution | Community, Token Incentives |
| **Marketplace** | Liquidity (Match Rate), GMV | Cold Start Problem | Subsidize Valuable Side |

## Part 1: B2B SaaS Marketing

### Strategic Foundations

**The Pipeline Reality:**
- Buyers are "dark" - 70%+ research complete before engaging sales
- Marketing-sourced pipeline should be 30-60% of revenue targets
- Funnel is actually a "bowtie" - marketing drives post-sale expansion too

**Key Definitions (SLA Requirements):**

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **MQL** | Meets demographic/firmographic criteria + behavioral intent (demo request, tech doc download) |
| **SQL** | MQL vetted by SDR/scoring, ready for sales conversation |
| **SAL** | SQL accepted by AE to work |
| **PQL** | (PLG) User hitting usage thresholds indicating purchase intent |

**Critical:** Responding within 5 minutes is 21x more likely to qualify than 30 minutes.

### Channel Priorities by Stage

**Seed/Pre-Seed:**
- Primary: Founder-led sales and evangelism
- Channels: Founder essays, specialized Slack groups, manual cold outreach
- Anti-Pattern: Hiring agency for paid ads before PMF

**Series A:**
- Primary: Repeatable demand gen
- Channels: LinkedIn Ads, Google Ads, SEO content library, niche conferences
- Budget: 20-40% of revenue to fuel growth

**Series B+:**
- Primary: Efficiency, multi-channel orchestration, brand
- Channels: Brand "air cover," ABM, review sites (G2, Capterra), partner marketing

### Content Strategy

**Content-to-Pipeline Mapping:**

| Funnel | Content Type |
|--------|--------------|
| **TOFU** | "What is" and "How to" problem-solving |
| **MOFU** | Buying guides, comparison sheets, webinars |
| **BOFU** | ROI calculators, implementation guides, case studies |

**B2B SEO Priority:** Purchase intent > Search volume. "Best enterprise CRM for fintech" (50 searches) beats "what is CRM" (5,000 searches).

**Gated vs Ungated (2025):**
- Ungate: Consumption content (blogs, basic guides)
- Gate: High-value utility (original research, complex tools)
- Hybrid: Ungate content, request email to "save for later" or access dataset

### Demand Gen & ABM

**Demand Gen vs Lead Gen:**
- Lead Gen: Capture contacts (high volume, low quality)
- Demand Gen: Educate market so they come ready to buy (lower volume, higher quality)

**ABM Tiers:**

| Tier | Approach | Example |
|------|----------|---------|
| **1:1** | Highly personalized | Custom landing pages, direct mail |
| **1:Few** | Segmented campaigns | "Fintech companies >500 employees" |
| **1:Many** | Programmatic targeting | Intent data-driven lists |

Companies using ABM are 60% more likely to meet revenue goals.

### Benchmarks (2025)

**Funnel Conversion:**

| Metric | Benchmark | Notes |
|--------|-----------|-------|
| Visitor to Lead | 2-5% | Top performers >10%; Enterprise ~0.7% |
| Lead to MQL | 31-41% | SaaS average ~41% |
| MQL to SQL | 15-21% | **Critical bottleneck.** Best-in-class: 40% |
| SQL to Opportunity | 36-42% | |
| Opportunity to Close | 20-30% | Enterprise ~31% |

**Unit Economics:**

| Metric | Benchmark | Notes |
|--------|-----------|-------|
| CAC Payback | 12-18 months | <12 ideal for SMB |
| LTV:CAC | 3:1 | <3:1 unstable; Top: 5:1 |
| Marketing Budget | 10-20% revenue | VC-backed hyper-growth: up to 47% |

**CAC by Channel:**

| Channel | CAC | Notes |
|---------|-----|-------|
| Referral | ~$150 | Most efficient, limited volume |
| Organic Search | $290-942 | High upfront, high long-term efficiency |
| LinkedIn Ads | ~$982 | High cost, high quality decision-makers |
| Paid Search | $1,200-2,000 | High intent, structurally expensive |
| Outbound Sales | ~$1,980 | Reserve for high ACV Enterprise |

### Anti-Patterns
- Gating everything (reduces reach and retargeting pools)
- Handing MQLs to AEs without SDR qualification
- Focusing on "Leads" instead of "Pipeline Revenue"

## Part 2: DTC E-Commerce Marketing

### Strategic Foundations

**The DTC Equation: Brand + Margin + Data**
- Pure-play arbitrage model is dead
- Need pricing power (Margin) through identity (Brand) and owned relationship (Data)
- Without >50-60% gross margins, paid acquisition math fails

**The LTV Math:**
```
CPA on Meta: $50
First-Order AOV: $60
Gross Margin: 50%
First Order: -$20 (loss)

Solution: 60-day LTV must break even or business fails
```

**Omnichannel Expansion:**
- DTC is a channel, not a business model
- Wholesale/marketplace expansion lowers blended CAC (retail shoppers = $0 CAC)
- Physical shelf presence validates brand online

### Channel Priorities

**Meta (Facebook/Instagram):**
- Primary demand *creation* engine
- Strategy: Broad targeting (Advantage+ Shopping), let creative find audience
- Median iROAS: ~2.31x (high variance based on creative quality)

**Google:**
- Primary demand *capture* engine
- Branded search and Shopping campaigns = mandatory defensive moats

**TikTok:**
- "Shoppertainment" - not social
- Requires native, lo-fi, trend-driven content
- TikTok Shop integrated commerce = massive sales driver

**Email & SMS (The Profit Generators):**
- Only channels you own
- SMS: 98% open rates, time-sensitive drops/flash sales
- Email: Storytelling, education, long-tail retention
- Target: 20-30% of total revenue

### Creative Strategy

**Creative is the New Targeting:**
- With automated bidding, ad creative is the biggest lever

**Testing Framework (Factory Model):**

| Phase | Action | Budget |
|-------|--------|--------|
| Pre-Flight | Test new concepts (hooks, angles) | ~$500 |
| BAU Challenge | Pit winners vs current best | |
| Scale | Move winners to main campaigns | |

- Velocity: 2-4 new variants per cycle
- Diagnose: Thumb Stop Ratio (3s view / Impressions), Hold Rate (100% view / 3s view)

**UGC at Scale:**
- Outperforms polished studio assets for acquisition
- Source via micro-influencer seeding or platforms (Billo, Insense)
- Licensing (whitelisting) allows running ads from creator's handle

### Retention Marketing

**The Math:**
- 5% increase in retention = 25-95% profitability boost
- LTV:CAC below 3:1 is "fundamentally unstable"

**Key Metrics:**

| Metric | Benchmark |
|--------|-----------|
| Repeat Purchase Rate (Beauty) | ~25.9% |
| Repeat Purchase Rate (Apparel) | ~25-26% |
| General E-commerce | 15-30% |

**Tactics:**
- Subscribe & Save: Table stakes for consumables
- Experiential Loyalty: Early access, exclusive community (combat "Trend Loyalty")
- Win-Back Flows: Trigger at 60, 90, 120 days with increasing incentives

### Benchmarks (2025)

| Metric | Benchmark | Context |
|--------|-----------|---------|
| Blended CAC | ~$64 avg | Dependent on AOV |
| LTV:CAC | >3:1 | Golden rule |
| Conversion Rate | 2-5% | Beauty ~6.8%, Electronics ~1.1% |
| Repeat Purchase Rate | 25-30% | Beauty > Fashion > Home |
| Email Revenue % | 20-30% | Healthy owned audience |
| ROAS (Meta) | ~2.31x median | Top performers >3.0x |

## Part 3: Fintech Marketing

### Strategic Foundations

**The Trust Deficit:**
- 20% of users strongly distrust fintechs vs 6% for traditional banks
- Over-invest in social proof, security badging, educational content

**The Compliance Tax:**
- Startups: 15-20% of revenue on compliance
- Mature firms: 8-12% of revenue
- Every piece of content requires legal review

**Strategy:** Build "Compliance-First Marketing Machine"
- Pre-approved claims libraries
- AI tools to pre-screen copy for non-compliant terms ("Guarantee," "Free")

**Education-First Marketing:**
- Financial products are complex
- Demystify APR, compound interest, blockchain mechanics
- Build authority with "Money 101" content

### Channel Strategies

**Content Marketing:**
- Focus on high-volume search queries ("How does APR work?")
- Captures users at start of financial journey

**Referral Programs:**
- Particularly powerful in fintech (PayPal, Robinhood, Cash App)
- Viral model CAC: $5-15 (vs hundreds for paid)

**Partnership Marketing (Embedded Finance):**
- Partner with platforms users already trust (SaaS invoicing → lending product)
- Borrow trust, lower CAC

### Vertical Specifics

| Vertical | Focus |
|----------|-------|
| **Consumer (Neobanks, Payments)** | High volume, low margin. Viral loops, referrals, brand affinity. |
| **B2B (Infrastructure, Embedded)** | High ACV, long cycles. Content, ABM, DevRel. |
| **Lending/Credit** | Highly regulated. Fair lending, transparent disclosure. |

### Compliance Considerations

| Regulation | Key Points |
|------------|------------|
| **UDAAP** | Avoid absolute claims ("Best," "Guaranteed") without substantiation |
| **ECOA** | Marketing cannot discriminate against protected classes; Monitor targeting |
| **Disclosure** | "Clear and conspicuous" required ("Terms apply," "Crypto is volatile") |

### Benchmarks

| Metric | Benchmark | Context |
|--------|-----------|---------|
| CAC (B2B/Enterprise) | ~$1,450 | Long cycles + compliance |
| CAC (Consumer Neobank) | $5-15 | Viral growth models |
| CAC (Traditional Bank) | ~$300+ | Legacy + less virality |
| Retention Rate | ~37% | Lowest of any industry |
| App Abandonment | 73% | Drop-off within first week |

## Part 4: Crypto/Web3 Marketing

### Strategic Foundations

**Community-First:**
- Community IS the marketing team
- Token holders evangelize the protocol
- Balance decentralization philosophy with execution reality

**Token Incentives:**

| Mechanism | Evolution |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Airdrops** | From "spray and pray" to retention-based vesting (linear streaming) |
| **Points Programs** | Pre-airdrop meta. Off-chain points for activity, may convert to tokens. |

**Bear Market Playbook:**
- Hype fails; focus on "Building"
- Educational content, DevRel, partnerships
- Retain "Settlers" (core believers) while "Tourists" leave

### Channel Priorities

| Channel | Purpose |
|---------|---------|
| **Twitter/X** | Public square for announcements, thought leadership |
| **Discord** | HQ for governance, support, community management |
| **Telegram** | Announcement wire, critical for international |
| **Crypto Media** | PR in Decrypt, CoinDesk, The Block for legitimacy |

### Community Building & DevRel

**Discord Architecture:**
- Structured roles and channels for governance/support
- Ambassador programs (token-incentivized super-users)

**Developer Relations:**
- For infrastructure projects, target developers
- Hackathons (ETHDenver), grants programs

### Regulatory Reality

**Howey Test Navigation:**
- Avoid promising "profits" from "efforts of others" (security classification risk)
- Geo-fencing often required (block US IPs from airdrops)

### Benchmarks

| Metric | Focus | Context |
|--------|-------|---------|
| Wallet Growth | # unique active wallets | Truest adoption measure |
| TVL (DeFi) | $ value locked | Marketing efficiency metric |
| Community Size | Discord/Telegram members | Warning: High bot prevalence |
| Token Distribution | Gini coefficient | Healthy = wide, not whale-concentrated |
| CAC (Wallet) | <$50 | Profitable DeFi acquisition |

## Part 5: Marketplace Marketing

### Strategic Foundations

**The Chicken-and-Egg Problem:**
- Need supply AND demand simultaneously

**Resolution Strategy:**

| Stage | Approach |
|-------|----------|
| **New Marketplaces** | Solve for demand first. Fake supply manually (Zappos buying from Amazon). |
| **Incumbents** | Solve for supply. Leverage existing customers to attract sellers. |

**Cold Start Tactics:**
1. Get the hardest side first (usually supply)
2. Geographic constraint (launch in one city)
3. Single-player mode (SaaS tool for one side before opening marketplace)
4. Subsidize the valuable side (Uber paid drivers hourly to sit in empty cars)

### Liquidity: The Only Metric

**Definition:** Probability that a transaction will occur

| Type | Metric |
|------|--------|
| **Buyer Liquidity** | Search-to-fill rate |
| **Seller Liquidity** | Utilization rate (% inventory sold/booked) |

**Benchmark:** Match Rate below 60% indicates churn risk.

### Marketplace Tactics

**Managed vs Open:**
- Early: "Manage" experience (vetted supply) for trust
- Scale: Transition to "open" platforms

**Take Rate Benchmarks:**

| Category | Take Rate |
|----------|-----------|
| Services (Uber/Fiverr) | 20-30% |
| Physical Goods (Etsy/Amazon) | 15-20% |

### Benchmarks

| Metric | Benchmark | Context |
|--------|-----------|---------|
| Liquidity (Match Rate) | >60% | Below = accelerated churn |
| Buyer CAC | ~$30 | Typically lower than seller |
| Seller CAC | ~$150 | Harder to acquire, higher LTV |
| LTV:CAC | >3:1 | Standard |
| GMV Growth | 15-25% YoY | Scale indicator |
| Take Rate | 15-30% | Digital > Service > Physical |

## Part 6: Cross-Cutting Themes

### Team Building by Stage

**Seed (0-20 employees):**
- 1 "Full Stack" Marketer or Founder-led
- Focus: Experimentation, product marketing, content
- Structure: Flat, reports to CEO

**Series A (~50 employees):**
- Director of Marketing + Demand Gen + Product Marketing + Content
- Focus: Building repeatable engine
- Structure: Functional specialization begins

**Series B (~125 employees):**
- VP Marketing + Directors (Brand, Demand, Product) + Specialists
- Focus: Specialization and scale
- Structure: Dedicated creative, ops, analytics teams

### Budget Allocation

| Model | Budget % of Revenue |
|-------|---------------------|
| B2B SaaS | 10-20% (Growth); up to 47% (VC hyper-growth) |
| DTC | 20-30% (ad spend velocity) |
| Fintech | Includes 8-12% compliance/legal allocation |

### Marketing Strategy Checklist

1. **Define Unit Economics:** CAC target and LTV based on benchmarks
2. **Identify the Constraint:** Awareness? Trust? Pipeline Velocity? Liquidity?
3. **Map Customer Journey:** Where do they learn? (Google, TikTok, Peers, Conferences)
4. **Audit Compliance/Risk:** Especially Fintech/Crypto
5. **Set North Star Metric:** SQLs, Liquidity, 60-day LTV, etc.
6. **Staff Accordingly:** Generalist vs Specialist based on stage
