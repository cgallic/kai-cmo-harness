# Account-Based Marketing (ABM) Playbook

> **Use when:** Targeting specific high-value accounts rather than broad audiences. Best for B2B companies selling $10K+/year deals to identifiable companies.

---

## ABM vs Traditional Demand Gen

| | Traditional | ABM |
|---|-----------|-----|
| **Approach** | Cast wide net, qualify down | Start with target list, personalize |
| **Audience** | Personas (job titles) | Named accounts (specific companies) |
| **Content** | One-size-fits-all | Personalized per account/industry |
| **Metric** | Leads generated | Accounts engaged → pipeline created |
| **Best for** | SMB, high-volume sales | Enterprise, high-ACV sales |
| **Team** | Marketing-led | Marketing + Sales aligned |

---

## The 3 ABM Tiers

```
TIER 1: ONE-TO-ONE (5-20 accounts)
  Fully custom campaigns per account
  Named account research, personalized content, custom landing pages
  Sales + Marketing collaborate per account
  Effort: High | ACV: $100K+

TIER 2: ONE-TO-FEW (20-100 accounts)
  Campaigns by industry/segment cluster
  Semi-personalized content (industry-specific, not account-specific)
  Shared campaigns across similar accounts
  Effort: Medium | ACV: $25K-100K

TIER 3: ONE-TO-MANY (100-1000 accounts)
  Programmatic ABM — technology-driven targeting
  Intent data + firmographic targeting at scale
  Same playbook as demand gen but to a defined list
  Effort: Low per account | ACV: $10K-25K
```

## ABM Execution Framework

### Step 1: Build the Target Account List

**Selection criteria:**
- Revenue range (company must be able to afford you)
- Employee count (indicator of operational complexity)
- Industry fit (your product solves a problem in their vertical)
- Technology stack (they use tools that integrate with yours)
- Growth signals (hiring, funding, expansion)
- Geographic fit (if you serve specific regions)

**Sources for target lists:**
| Source | What It Provides | Cost |
|--------|-----------------|------|
| LinkedIn Sales Navigator | Company + contact filtering | $100+/mo |
| ZoomInfo / Apollo.io | Firmographic + contact data | $200+/mo |
| Crunchbase | Funding signals, company info | Free tier |
| BuiltWith | Technology stack detection | $300+/mo |
| G2 / TrustRadius | Competitor's customers (intent data) | $500+/mo |
| Your CRM | Closed-lost accounts worth revisiting | Free |

### Step 2: Research Each Account (Tier 1)

For Tier 1 accounts, build an account dossier:

```
ACCOUNT: {Company Name}
═══════════════════════

COMPANY:
  Industry:    {vertical}
  Revenue:     ${X}M ARR
  Employees:   {N}
  HQ:          {city}
  Funding:     {last round, investor}
  Tech stack:  {relevant tools they use}
  Growth:      {hiring? expanding? new product?}

DECISION MAKERS:
  Economic buyer:  {Name, Title} — signs the check
  Champion:        {Name, Title} — wants the solution internally
  Influencer:      {Name, Title} — technical evaluation
  Blocker:         {Name, Title} — might resist change

PAIN HYPOTHESIS:
  Based on {signal}, we believe they struggle with {problem}
  because {reasoning}. Our solution addresses this by {benefit}.

ENGAGEMENT HISTORY:
  - {Any prior interactions, website visits, content downloads}

PERSONALIZATION HOOKS:
  - {Recent news about the company}
  - {Recent LinkedIn posts from champion}
  - {Conference they spoke at or attended}
  - {Shared connection or mutual customer}
```

### Step 3: Create Personalized Content

**Tier 1 content (per account):**
- Custom landing page: `yoursite.com/for/{company-name}`
- Personalized video: Loom/video addressing their specific pain
- Custom case study: similar company in their industry
- Personalized email sequence: reference their specific situation
- Direct mail: physical package (book, branded item) — stands out in digital noise

**Tier 2 content (per industry):**
- Industry-specific landing page: `yoursite.com/for/{industry}`
- Industry case study: results from companies in their vertical
- Industry webinar: "How {industry} companies solve {problem}"
- Persona-specific content: different messaging for buyer vs user

**Tier 3 content (programmatic):**
- Dynamic website personalization (show industry-relevant content)
- Targeted ad campaigns by account list
- Email sequences with industry-specific proof points

### Step 4: Multi-Channel Orchestration

For each target account, run coordinated touches across channels:

```
WEEK 1: AWARENESS
  LinkedIn ads targeting the company + decision-maker titles
  + Content engagement tracking

WEEK 2: WARM-UP
  LinkedIn connection request from AE to champion
  + Personalized content share (article relevant to their pain)

WEEK 3: DIRECT OUTREACH
  Email 1 from AE: reference their specific situation + relevant case study
  + Continue LinkedIn ad impressions (familiarity)

WEEK 4: VALUE DELIVERY
  Email 2: share a relevant insight or data point (no pitch)
  + LinkedIn comment on champion's post (genuine, not salesy)

WEEK 5: ASK
  Email 3: direct ask for 15-min meeting
  + Voicemail from AE (multi-channel touchpoint)

WEEK 6+: NURTURE OR ESCALATE
  If engaged: book meeting, move to opportunity
  If no response: shift to longer-term nurture cadence
```

### Step 5: Measure Account Engagement

```
ACCOUNT ENGAGEMENT SCORE
═══════════════════════

Company: {Name}
Score: {0-100}

DIGITAL SIGNALS:
  Website visits:        {count} from {N} people    (+5 per visit)
  Content downloads:     {count}                    (+10 each)
  Email opens:           {count}                    (+2 each)
  Email replies:         {count}                    (+20 each)
  Ad clicks:             {count}                    (+5 each)
  Event attendance:      {count}                    (+15 each)

RELATIONSHIP SIGNALS:
  LinkedIn connections:  {count} / {target contacts} (+10 each)
  Meetings held:         {count}                    (+25 each)
  Champion identified:   {yes/no}                   (+20)
  Multiple contacts engaged: {count}                (+15 per contact)

STATUS:
  0-20:   Cold — continue awareness
  21-50:  Warming — increase outreach
  51-80:  Engaged — sales should prioritize
  81+:    Hot — active opportunity
```

## ABM + Sales Alignment

### The SLA (Service Level Agreement)

| Marketing Commits To | Sales Commits To |
|---------------------|-----------------|
| Deliver target account list (quarterly) | Follow up on engaged accounts within 24 hours |
| Create personalized content per tier | Execute multi-channel outreach sequences |
| Run targeted campaigns per account list | Log all account interactions in CRM |
| Share engagement data weekly | Share deal feedback monthly |
| Provide battlecards per competitor | Report win/loss reasons per account |

### Weekly ABM Standup (15 min)

Agenda:
1. Hot accounts (score 50+) — who's engaging, what's next?
2. Campaign performance — impressions, clicks, engagement by tier
3. New intelligence — any target accounts showing buying signals?
4. Blockers — what's preventing progress on key accounts?

## Metrics

| Metric | Formula | Good | Excellent |
|--------|---------|------|-----------|
| Account engagement rate | Engaged accounts / target accounts | 30-50% | 60%+ |
| Account-to-opportunity rate | Opportunities / target accounts | 10-20% | 25%+ |
| Pipeline from ABM | $ pipeline from ABM accounts | 30-50% of total | 60%+ |
| Deal size (ABM vs non-ABM) | Avg deal size comparison | 1.5x non-ABM | 2x+ |
| Sales cycle (ABM) | Days from first touch to close | 20-30% shorter | 40%+ shorter |
| Win rate (ABM) | Closed-won / opportunities | 1.5x non-ABM | 2x+ |
| Cost per account engaged | ABM spend / engaged accounts | $500-2000 | — |
