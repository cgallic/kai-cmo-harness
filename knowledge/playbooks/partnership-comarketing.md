# Partnership & Co-Marketing Playbook

> **Use when:** Building strategic partnerships, running co-marketing campaigns, creating integration partnerships, or leveraging others' audiences for mutual growth.

---

## Partnership Types

| Type | What It Is | Example | Value |
|------|-----------|---------|-------|
| **Integration** | Your product works with theirs | Slack + Zoom | Mutual product value |
| **Co-marketing** | Joint content, campaigns, events | Two SaaS companies co-host a webinar | Shared audience access |
| **Referral** | They send customers to you (and vice versa) | Accountant recommends your SaaS to clients | Warm introductions |
| **Reseller/channel** | They sell your product | MSP sells your tool to their clients | Distribution |
| **Technology** | Deep integration, joint product | Stripe + Shopify | Platform dependency |
| **Content** | Guest posts, co-authored research, podcast swaps | Two newsletters cross-promote | Audience sharing |
| **Affiliate** | Commission-based referrals | See `channels/affiliate-referral.md` | Performance-based |

## Finding the Right Partners

### The Partner Fit Matrix

```
                    HIGH AUDIENCE OVERLAP
                           │
         Competitors ──────┼────── Ideal Partners ★
         (avoid)           │      (complementary product,
                           │       same customer, no competition)
                           │
LOW SYNERGY ───────────────┼──────────────── HIGH SYNERGY
                           │
         Irrelevant ───────┼────── Aspirational
         (no fit)          │      (bigger brand, would elevate you,
                           │       but hard to pitch today)
                           │
                    LOW AUDIENCE OVERLAP
```

### Partner Discovery Checklist

- [ ] List 10 tools your customers already use alongside yours
- [ ] Check your CRM: what other products do your best customers mention?
- [ ] Survey customers: "What other tools are essential to your workflow?"
- [ ] Search "[your category] integrations" to find common pairings
- [ ] Check competitor partner pages: who are they partnered with?
- [ ] Look at industry events: who else sponsors/speaks?
- [ ] Find newsletters/podcasts your ICP follows: potential content partners

## Co-Marketing Campaign Playbook

### Campaign Types (by effort)

| Campaign | Effort | Reach | Lead Quality |
|----------|--------|-------|-------------|
| **Social media cross-post** | Low | Medium | Low |
| **Newsletter swap/mention** | Low | Medium | Medium |
| **Joint blog post** | Medium | Medium | Medium |
| **Co-hosted webinar** | Medium | High | High |
| **Joint research report** | High | Very High | High |
| **Co-branded landing page** | Medium | Medium | High |
| **Joint product launch** | Very High | Very High | Very High |

### Webinar Co-Marketing (Most Common)

**Why it works:** Both partners promote to their audience. You get exposure to their list, they get exposure to yours. The content serves both audiences.

**Execution:**
1. Agree on topic that serves both audiences
2. Each partner promotes to their list (email + social)
3. Run 45-min webinar: 20 min Partner A content, 20 min Partner B, 5 min Q&A
4. Share leads: each partner gets the full attendee list (with consent)
5. Follow up: each partner nurtures their half

**Lead split options:**
- **Full share:** Both partners get all registrant data (most common, requires consent)
- **Sourced split:** Each partner gets leads their promotion generated (tracked by UTM)
- **Follow-up split:** Partner A follows up first 48 hours, Partner B follows up after

### Content Co-Marketing

**Joint blog post:** Each partner writes a section, published on both blogs. Double the SEO value (two backlinks), double the distribution.

**Newsletter swap:** You mention them to your list, they mention you to theirs. Zero-cost audience access. Works best when lists are similar size.

**Co-authored research:** Combine data from both companies for an industry report. Both brands get cited as the source. Higher authority than either brand alone.

## Partnership Outreach

### The Partner Pitch Template

```
Subject: Partnership idea: {their company} + {your company}

Hi {name},

I'm {your name} from {your company}. We help {your audience} do {your thing}.

I noticed your customers also need {the complementary thing you do}, and
our customers often use {their product} alongside ours. There's a natural fit.

Here's what I had in mind:

  Option A: Co-host a webinar on "{topic both audiences care about}"
  Option B: Integration feature where {their product} connects to {your product}
  Option C: Cross-promotion to each other's audiences (newsletter mention swap)

We have {your audience size} {subscribers/customers/followers} in the {industry} space.

Would you be open to a 15-minute call to explore this?

{Your name}
{Title, Company}
```

### What Makes a Good Partner Pitch

- **Leads with their benefit** (not yours)
- **Specific proposal** (not "let's partner somehow")
- **Proof of audience** (your distribution is the value you bring)
- **Low initial ask** (newsletter swap, not "build an integration")
- **Mutual win is obvious** (same customer, different product)

## Integration Partnerships

### Why Integrations Matter

- Products with 5+ integrations retain 2-3x better than products with 0
- Integration pages rank for "[Your product] + [Their product]" keywords
- Each integration is a co-marketing opportunity
- Integrations increase switching costs (stickiness)

### Integration Partnership Levels

```
LEVEL 1: LISTING (Low effort)
  You're listed in their marketplace/directory
  They're listed on your integrations page
  Mutual backlinks, minimal maintenance

LEVEL 2: BASIC INTEGRATION (Medium effort)
  API connection (Zapier, native, or webhook)
  Shared documentation
  Co-announcement blog post

LEVEL 3: DEEP INTEGRATION (High effort)
  Embedded experience within each other's products
  Joint development, shared roadmap
  Co-selling, revenue share possible

LEVEL 4: STRATEGIC ALLIANCE (Highest effort)
  Joint product offering
  Shared go-to-market
  Executive relationship
  May involve revenue share or equity
```

### Integration Launch Checklist

- [ ] Integration tested and working
- [ ] Documentation written for both user bases
- [ ] Landing page created on both sites
- [ ] Blog post co-authored and published on both blogs
- [ ] Email announcement to both customer bases
- [ ] Social media announcement from both accounts
- [ ] Listed in relevant marketplaces/directories
- [ ] Sales teams at both companies briefed
- [ ] Support teams trained on the integration
- [ ] Co-branded assets available (logos, screenshots)

## Measuring Partnership ROI

### Per-Partnership Metrics

| Metric | How to Track | Target |
|--------|-------------|--------|
| **Leads generated** | UTM-tagged links per partner | Growing QoQ |
| **Pipeline influenced** | CRM: deals where partner was a touchpoint | $X per partner |
| **Revenue from partner leads** | Closed-won from partner sources | Track monthly |
| **Integration adoption** | Users who activate the integration | 10-20% of mutual users |
| **Co-marketing reach** | Impressions/attendees from joint campaigns | 2x your solo reach |
| **Partner satisfaction** | Quarterly survey/check-in | NPS 8+ |

## Common Partnership Mistakes

| Mistake | Why It Hurts | Fix |
|---------|-------------|-----|
| Partnering with competitors | Confusion, channel conflict | Complementary only |
| No clear owner internally | Partnership dies from neglect | Assign a partnership manager |
| One-sided value | Partner disengages | Ensure mutual benefit every campaign |
| Too many partners, too shallow | None produce meaningful results | Focus on 5-10 deep partnerships |
| No tracking | Can't prove ROI → budget cut | UTM everything, track in CRM |
| Starting with "build an integration" | Too much commitment upfront | Start with content co-marketing, graduate to integration |
