# PR & Communications Strategy Playbook

> **Use when:** Planning media outreach, press releases, crisis communications, or thought leadership PR campaigns.

---

## PR Strategy Framework

### The PESO Model

```
┌──────────────────┬──────────────────┐
│  PAID             │  EARNED          │
│  Sponsored content│  Press coverage  │
│  Paid media       │  Reviews         │
│  Native ads       │  Organic mentions│
│  Advertorials     │  Word of mouth   │
├──────────────────┼──────────────────┤
│  SHARED           │  OWNED           │
│  Social media     │  Blog/website    │
│  Community        │  Newsletter      │
│  Co-created       │  Podcast         │
│  User-generated   │  Case studies    │
└──────────────────┴──────────────────┘
```

All four quadrants should work together. A press release (Earned) should link to a blog post (Owned), be amplified on social media (Shared), and optionally boosted with paid distribution (Paid).

## Pitch Framework

### The 3-Sentence Pitch

Every media pitch should answer three questions in three sentences:

1. **What's new?** (The news — something that didn't exist yesterday)
2. **Why should readers care?** (The relevance — connected to a trend, pain, or moment)
3. **What's the proof?** (The evidence — data, customer story, or expert quote)

### Email Pitch Template

```
Subject: {Specific, newsworthy hook — not a press release title}

Hi {first name},

{Sentence 1: The news — what happened or what you're launching.}

{Sentence 2: Why their audience cares — connect to their beat or a current trend.}

{Sentence 3: The proof — a number, result, or named customer.}

{Optional: One sentence about why you reached out to THEM specifically — not a generic blast.}

Happy to share {more data / a demo / the full report / an interview with the founder}.

{Name}
{Title, Company}
{Phone}
```

**Rules:**
- Subject line < 60 chars (mobile truncation)
- Total pitch < 150 words (journalists get 100+ pitches/day)
- No attachments on first email (link to assets instead)
- Personalize: reference their recent article or beat
- Send Tuesday-Thursday, 8-10 AM (journalist's timezone)

## Media List Building

### Where to Find Journalists

1. **Muck Rack** — journalist database, search by beat/publication
2. **X/Twitter** — most journalists are active, search "[beat] journalist"
3. **Publication mastheads** — read the bylines of relevant articles
4. **HARO/Connectively** — respond to journalist queries (inbound PR)
5. **Podcast guest databases** — Podcast Guests, MatchMaker.fm

### Media List Fields

| Field | Example |
|-------|---------|
| Name | Sarah Chen |
| Publication | TechCrunch |
| Beat | AI, Enterprise SaaS |
| Email | sarah.chen@techcrunch.com |
| Twitter/X | @sarahchen |
| Recent article | "AI phone agents are replacing call centers" |
| Notes | Covered our competitor last month |

### Tiering Your Targets

**Tier 1 (dream coverage):** 5-10 publications that would transform your brand
**Tier 2 (solid coverage):** 20-30 publications your customers read
**Tier 3 (volume coverage):** 50+ niche blogs, podcasts, newsletters

Start with Tier 3 to build a press portfolio, then use those clips to pitch Tier 1.

## Press Release Structure

See `knowledge/channels/press-releases.md` for the full guide. Quick summary:

```
FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE

{HEADLINE — newsworthy, specific, under 70 chars}
{SUBHEADLINE — additional context}

{CITY, STATE — Date} — {First paragraph: who, what, when, where, why in 2-3 sentences}

{Second paragraph: expand on the news — how it works, who benefits}

{Quote from founder/CEO — not corporate-speak, make it sound human}

{Third paragraph: supporting evidence — data, customer example, industry context}

{Quote from customer or industry expert (if available)}

{Boilerplate: about the company in 2-3 sentences}

Media contact:
{Name, email, phone}
```

## Thought Leadership PR

### Building Authority Without News

Not everything is a launch. Thought leadership PR builds credibility over time:

1. **Byline articles** — Write for industry publications (Forbes Council, Inc., industry blogs)
2. **Expert commentary** — Be the go-to source for journalists covering your space
3. **Data reports** — Publish original research/data (journalists love citing exclusive data)
4. **Podcast guesting** — 1-2 podcast appearances/month in your niche
5. **Speaking** — Conference talks, webinar panels, fireside chats
6. **LinkedIn content** — 3-5x/week builds thought leadership faster than anything else

### Thought Leadership Calendar

| Month | Activity | Output |
|-------|----------|--------|
| Jan | Publish annual industry report | Data PR, 5+ article pitches |
| Feb | Podcast guesting sprint (4 shows) | 4 podcast episodes |
| Mar | Byline article for industry pub | 1 published byline |
| Apr | Conference speaking | 1-2 speaking engagements |
| May | Customer success story | 1 case study, PR pitch |
| Jun | Half-year data update | Updated report, media outreach |
| Ongoing | LinkedIn 3-5x/week | Consistent thought leadership |
| Ongoing | HARO responses 2-3x/week | Inbound media mentions |

## Crisis Communications

### The 4-Step Framework

1. **Acknowledge** — Within 1 hour. "We're aware of [issue] and are investigating."
2. **Investigate** — Gather facts. Don't speculate. Assign one spokesperson.
3. **Respond** — Within 4 hours. State what happened, what you're doing, what changes.
4. **Follow up** — Within 24-48 hours. Report on resolution, preventive measures.

### Crisis Response Template

```
We became aware of {issue} at {time} today.

What happened: {Factual description — no speculation, no blame-shifting}

What we've done: {Immediate actions taken}

What's next: {Preventive measures, timeline for resolution}

We take this seriously because {reason connected to your values}.

For questions: {dedicated contact}
```

### Rules During a Crisis
- One spokesperson only (consistency)
- No "no comment" (say "we're investigating" instead)
- Don't delete social media posts about the crisis (makes it worse)
- Respond in the channel where the crisis is happening (Twitter crisis = Twitter response)
- Over-communicate internally (team should know before they read about it)
- Document everything (for post-mortem and legal)

## Measuring PR Impact

### Direct Metrics
- Media mentions (count + quality of publications)
- Domain authority of linking publications
- Referral traffic from press coverage
- Backlinks earned (SEO value)
- Share of voice vs competitors

### Indirect Metrics
- Brand search volume (Google Trends)
- Direct traffic growth
- Social mention sentiment
- Sales team reporting "I saw you in [publication]"
- Inbound partnership/investor inquiries

### Tools
- **Google Alerts** — Free, basic brand monitoring
- **Mention** — Social + web monitoring
- **Meltwater/Cision** — Enterprise media monitoring
- **Ahrefs/Moz** — Track backlinks from press coverage
- **Google Search Console** — Track branded search impressions
