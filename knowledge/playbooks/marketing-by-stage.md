# Marketing by Company Stage — What to Do When

> **Use when:** Deciding which marketing activities to prioritize based on company maturity. The wrong strategy at the wrong stage wastes money and time.

---

## Stage Map

```
PRE-LAUNCH ($0)  →  EARLY (0-$10K MRR)  →  GROWTH ($10K-$100K MRR)  →  SCALE ($100K+ MRR)
   ↓                    ↓                       ↓                          ↓
 Validate            Find PMF               Optimize                   Systematize
 the idea            + first channel         + expand channels          + build team
```

---

## Stage 1: Pre-Launch ($0 revenue)

**Goal:** Validate demand before building. Get 100 people who want this badly enough to give you their email, money, or time.

### Do This (priority order)

| Activity | Why | Effort |
|----------|-----|--------|
| **Customer interviews (20-30)** | Validate pain is real, understand language they use | 2 weeks |
| **Landing page + email capture** | Test if people will take action on the promise | 1 day |
| **Waitlist with referral mechanic** | Each signup incentivized to recruit the next | 1 day |
| **Build in public (X/Twitter, LinkedIn)** | Free distribution, attracts early adopters | Ongoing |
| **Community participation** | Be in communities where your ICP hangs out | Ongoing |
| **5-10 cold DMs per day** | Direct outreach to potential customers | 30 min/day |

### Don't Do This
- Paid ads (you don't know what converts yet)
- SEO content (takes 3-6 months to compound)
- Brand guidelines / logo design (premature optimization)
- Influencer partnerships (no product to demo)
- Marketing automation (no audience to automate for)

### Key Metrics
- Email signups (target: 100-500 before launch)
- Conversion rate on landing page (>10% = strong signal)
- Interview insights (are people actively trying to solve this problem?)

### Budget: $0-500/month
Spend on: domain, landing page hosting, maybe a small waitlist tool.

## Stage 2: Early ($0-$10K MRR)

**Goal:** Find product-market fit + discover your first scalable channel. Not "do all marketing" — find the ONE channel that works and double down.

### Do This (priority order)

| Activity | Why | Effort |
|----------|-----|--------|
| **One content channel (pick ONE)** | SEO blog, LinkedIn, YouTube — commit to one for 90 days | 3-5 hrs/week |
| **Cold outreach (email/DM/LinkedIn)** | Direct line to customers, fast feedback | 1-2 hrs/day |
| **Customer case studies** | Your first 5-10 customers are your best marketing | Per customer win |
| **Small paid ad experiments** | $20-50/day to test messaging and audiences | 30 min/day |
| **Email newsletter to early users** | Keep relationship warm, share updates, gather feedback | 1x/week |
| **Product Hunt / HN launch** | One-time spike that creates initial awareness | 1 week prep |

### Don't Do This
- Multiple content channels simultaneously (spread too thin)
- Hiring a marketing person (founder should do marketing — you learn your customer)
- Brand campaigns (awareness doesn't matter yet — conversions do)
- Complex marketing automation (premature)
- Conferences / sponsorships (too expensive per lead at this stage)

### Channel Selection Framework

| If Your Product Is... | Start With | Why |
|----------------------|-----------|-----|
| **B2B SaaS, complex** | LinkedIn + cold email | Decision-makers are there, direct outreach works |
| **B2B SaaS, simple** | Content SEO + Product Hunt | Self-serve products scale through search |
| **DTC / consumer** | TikTok or Instagram Reels | Visual, impulse-driven discovery |
| **Local service** | Google Ads + Google Business Profile | Intent-driven local search |
| **Developer tool** | Dev community + GitHub + content | Developers find tools through peers |
| **Marketplace** | Supply-side acquisition first | No demand without supply |

### Key Metrics
- MRR growth rate (target: 15-20% MoM)
- CAC from primary channel
- Conversion rate from primary channel
- Customer retention (month 1, month 3)

### Budget: $500-3,000/month
Spend on: tools (email, CRM, analytics), small ad budget, maybe a freelance writer.

## Stage 3: Growth ($10K-$100K MRR)

**Goal:** Optimize the channel that works, add 1-2 new channels, build the marketing machine.

### Do This (priority order)

| Activity | Why | Effort |
|----------|-----|--------|
| **Double down on winning channel** | 60% of budget to what's already working | Ongoing |
| **Add second channel** | Diversify, but only after first channel is optimized | 3-month commitment |
| **SEO content program (if not primary)** | Starts compounding now, pays off at scale | 2-4 posts/month |
| **Retargeting campaigns** | Cheapest conversions — warm up existing visitors | $500-2K/month |
| **Email marketing automation** | Welcome sequences, nurture flows, re-engagement | One-time setup + ongoing |
| **Customer referral program** | Your happiest customers recruit the next batch | Setup + ongoing |
| **Systematic A/B testing** | Optimize what exists before adding more | Continuous |
| **Hire first marketing person** | You need someone running the machine daily | Full-time |

### Don't Do This
- Spread budget across 5+ channels (focus on 2-3 max)
- Rebrand (unless the current brand is actively hurting conversion)
- Build a marketing team before the system is proven (hire one generalist first)
- Stop founder involvement in marketing (stay close to the customer)

### Channel Expansion Strategy

```
QUARTER 1: Optimize primary channel (the one that got you to $10K)
QUARTER 2: Add retargeting + email automation (cheap, high-ROI)
QUARTER 3: Start second acquisition channel (SEO, ads, or partnerships)
QUARTER 4: Evaluate what's working, cut what's not, double down
```

### Key Metrics
- Blended CAC (across all channels)
- LTV:CAC ratio (target: >3:1)
- Channel-specific CAC and ROAS
- Net revenue retention (target: >100%)
- Pipeline coverage (for sales-assisted)

### Budget: $5,000-30,000/month
Spend on: ad budget (40%), tools (15%), people (35%), content (10%).

## Stage 4: Scale ($100K+ MRR)

**Goal:** Systematize, diversify, build brand, and create compounding assets. Marketing becomes a machine with multiple engines.

### Do This (priority order)

| Activity | Why | Effort |
|----------|-----|--------|
| **3-5 channel portfolio** | No single-channel dependency | Ongoing |
| **Brand marketing** | Create top-of-funnel awareness that feeds all channels | 10-20% of budget |
| **Content engine (2-4x/week)** | SEO compound returns accelerate at this volume | Full-time |
| **Paid ads at scale ($50K+/month)** | Systematic testing, creative refresh, audience expansion | Dedicated person |
| **PR and thought leadership** | Builds trust moat, drives branded search | Ongoing |
| **Community building** | Owned audience, reduces acquisition cost over time | Dedicated person |
| **Analytics and attribution** | Need clear view of what drives revenue | Dashboard + quarterly deep dives |
| **Partner/affiliate channel** | New distribution through others' audiences | Program setup + management |
| **Build marketing team** | Specialists for each channel + ops | 3-8 people |

### Don't Do This
- Lose the founder's voice (brand comes from the top)
- Over-index on paid acquisition (build organic moats too)
- Ignore community/earned channels in favor of "scalable" paid
- Stop experimenting (allocate 10-20% of budget to testing)

### Team Structure

```
VP/Head of Marketing
├── Content Marketing Manager
│   └── Writer(s) / freelancers
├── Demand Gen Manager
│   ├── Paid ads specialist
│   └── Email/lifecycle specialist
├── Brand/Community Manager
│   └── Social media
└── Marketing Ops
    └── Analytics, tools, attribution
```

### Key Metrics
- Revenue attributed to marketing
- Blended CAC trend (should decrease or stabilize)
- Organic vs paid ratio (target: 40-60% organic)
- Brand search volume trend
- Pipeline contribution by channel
- Marketing-influenced revenue (beyond just first-touch)

### Budget: $30,000-200,000+/month
Rule of thumb: 10-20% of revenue for SaaS, 15-30% for DTC.

## Stage Transition Checklist

### Pre-Launch → Early
- [ ] 100+ email signups validated demand
- [ ] 5+ customer interviews confirmed the pain
- [ ] Landing page converts at >5%
- [ ] Product exists (even MVP)

### Early → Growth
- [ ] Reached $10K MRR with clear growth trend
- [ ] One acquisition channel consistently produces customers
- [ ] CAC is known and sustainable (LTV:CAC > 3:1)
- [ ] Retention is healthy (>70% at month 3)
- [ ] Customer can articulate why they chose you

### Growth → Scale
- [ ] Reached $100K MRR
- [ ] 2+ acquisition channels producing reliably
- [ ] Marketing systems documented and repeatable
- [ ] First marketing hire onboarded and productive
- [ ] Brand starting to generate inbound demand (branded search growing)

## Common Mistakes by Stage

| Stage | Mistake | Why It Hurts |
|-------|---------|-------------|
| Pre-Launch | Building product before validating demand | Wastes 3-12 months on something nobody wants |
| Pre-Launch | Spending on brand/design before PMF | Polishing something that might pivot completely |
| Early | Doing 5 marketing channels at once | No channel gets enough attention to work |
| Early | Hiring a marketer too early | Founder needs to learn the customer first |
| Growth | Not systematizing what works | Growth stalls because it depends on heroics |
| Growth | Adding channels before optimizing current ones | New channels perform worse than optimized existing |
| Scale | Over-reliance on paid ads | No organic moat; CAC rises as competition increases |
| Scale | Losing founder involvement | Brand becomes generic; loses the edge that got there |
