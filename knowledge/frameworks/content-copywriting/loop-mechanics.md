# Loop Mechanics — Self-Reinforcing Systems Framework

> **Use when:** Designing growth loops, viral mechanics, retention systems, product-led growth, or any self-reinforcing system. This is the meta-framework that sits above individual marketing tactics.

---

## The 12 Loop Types

A real loop has four parts: **Input → Transformation → Persistent Gain → Re-entry.** If there's no persistent gain, it's repetition. If there's no re-entry, it's a pipeline. If there's no transformation, it's polling. If there's no input pressure or reward, it dies.

### 1. Attention Loops — Amplify Visibility

**Question:** What causes more attention to create even more attention?

**Mechanisms:** controversy, novelty, ranking, public outputs, visible momentum, social proof, ongoing discovery

**Hunt for:**
- What makes observers become amplifiers?
- What makes visibility itself produce more visibility?
- What makes the thing hard to ignore once seen?

### 2. Learning Loops — Amplify Intelligence

**Question:** What causes each cycle to make the next cycle smarter?

**Mechanisms:** error logging, failure clustering, critique memory, replay analysis, better prompts from past runs, retrieval built from prior mistakes

**Hunt for:**
- What gets learned from each run?
- Where is the memory stored?
- What patterns recur often enough to operationalize?
- What turns one failure into a permanent upgrade?

### 3. Quality Loops — Amplify Output Quality

**Question:** What makes each pass improve the artifact instead of just mutate it?

**Mechanisms:** draft → critique → rewrite, implementation → adversary review → fix, claim → verification → correction, output → score → retry under constraints

**Hunt for:**
- What is the scoring function?
- What feedback is actually useful versus noisy?
- What causes refinement instead of drift?
- What stops the loop from polishing the wrong thing?

A lot of "improvement loops" are fake because they lack a hard quality signal.

### 4. Distribution Loops — Amplify Reach Through Structure

**Question:** What makes usage create more entry points?

**Mechanisms:** user-generated artifacts, embedded referrals, shared workspaces, public leaderboards, collaborative surfaces, network-dependent value

**Hunt for:**
- What output leaves the product and carries the product with it?
- What action inside the system recruits the next node?
- What makes one user increase the chance of more users?

Broader than social posting. Includes product architecture.

### 5. Revenue Loops — Amplify Monetization

**Question:** What causes money generated to improve future money generation?

**Mechanisms:** paid usage funds better acquisition, customer revenue funds better tooling, retained users expand usage, higher-value customers generate data that improves the product

**Hunt for:**
- What customer success signal turns into better sales?
- What part of the system becomes easier to sell after each win?
- Does each dollar improve distribution, retention, or product quality?

### 6. Trust Loops — Amplify Credibility

**Question:** What makes each successful interaction make future adoption easier?

**Mechanisms:** proofs, case studies, consistent outputs, audit trails, public receipts, reputation compounding

**Hunt for:**
- What creates proof instead of claims?
- What visible evidence reduces future resistance?
- What compounds belief over time?

Critical in AI, infra, finance, law, healthcare — anything high-stakes.

### 7. Data Loops — Amplify System Advantage

**Question:** What user behavior produces data that improves the product, which then attracts more useful behavior?

**Mechanisms:** better recommendations from usage, better scoring from corrections, better routing from observed outcomes, better personalization from repeated interaction

**Hunt for:**
- What data is uniquely valuable?
- What makes the data improve the system?
- What makes the improved system attract better data?
- Is the loop real, or are you just collecting junk?

Most people say "data flywheel" with no actual mechanism. Usually fake.

### 8. Operational Loops — Amplify Execution Speed

**Question:** What makes each cycle of work reduce future friction?

**Mechanisms:** templates, playbooks, reusable decisions, automation from repeated human actions, better defaults, fallback systems

**Hunt for:**
- What is repeated often enough to standardize?
- What decision can become a rule?
- What handoff keeps failing and needs structure?
- What friction recurs across sessions, teams, or customers?

This is where boring companies become killers.

### 9. Adversarial Loops — Amplify Resilience

**Question:** What makes every attack, contradiction, or failure make the system harder to break next time?

**Mechanisms:** red-team critique, edge-case generation, contradiction hunts, drift detection, replay of failure scenarios, attack libraries

**Hunt for:**
- What failure modes recur?
- What assumptions break under pressure?
- What attacks reveal structural weakness?
- What criticism should become permanent policy?

One of the most underused loop types.

### 10. Network Loops — Amplify Value With Participants

**Question:** What makes each additional node increase the value for existing nodes?

**Mechanisms:** marketplaces, multi-agent systems, collaboration platforms, discovery graphs, matching systems, reputation systems

**Hunt for:**
- Why does the next participant matter?
- Who benefits from whom joining?
- Does density create value, or just noise?
- At what point does the network become self-reinforcing?

### 11. Identity Loops — Amplify Commitment and Retention

**Question:** What makes participation become part of how someone sees themselves?

**Mechanisms:** contributor status, rankings, governance, public identity, belonging, rituals, visible progression

**Hunt for:**
- What identity forms around repeated use?
- What makes leaving feel like losing something about oneself?
- What signals membership or advancement?

Not just social media. This is community gravity.

### 12. Discovery Loops — Amplify Finding Useful Things

**Question:** What makes exploration generate better exploration?

**Mechanisms:** recommendation refinement, anomaly detection, topic clustering, signal extraction, recursive research agents, better search from prior findings

**Hunt for:**
- What does each discovery unlock?
- What new search path opens after each useful result?
- How does the system avoid drowning in its own output?

## The 7 Thinking Personas

### The Ecologist
- Thinks in: environments, dependencies, self-reinforcing conditions
- Asks: "What conditions allow this thing to keep regenerating itself?"
- Good at: system health, sustainability, feedback structure, interdependence

### The Physicist
- Thinks in: force, resistance, acceleration, constraint
- Asks: "What force pushes the loop forward, and what friction bleeds it?"
- Good at: bottlenecks, leverage points, throughput, systemic drag

### The Evolutionary Biologist
- Thinks in: selection pressure, mutation, adaptation, survival
- Asks: "What pressures cause this system to improve instead of collapse?"
- Good at: adversarial loops, competitive loops, adaptation, fitness functions

### The Economist
- Thinks in: incentives, compounding, capital flows
- Asks: "What gets rewarded, and how does that reward change future behavior?"
- Good at: revenue loops, incentive design, market dynamics

### The Game Designer
- Thinks in: progression, replayability, engagement mechanics
- Asks: "Why would an actor complete another cycle?"
- Good at: retention loops, motivation design, visible state change

### The Auditor
- Thinks in: verification, trust, reality checks
- Asks: "What proof closes the loop in reality rather than narrative?"
- Good at: trust loops, quality loops, claim verification, false-loop detection

### The Strategist
- Thinks in: positioning, asymmetry, compounding advantage
- Asks: "If this loop works, what defensible advantage does it create over time?"
- Good at: moats, flywheels, platform design, long-term compounding

## Viral Loop Diagnostic — The 15 Hunt Questions

### 1. What makes one user naturally pull in another?
If the answer is "we'll ask them to share," that's weak. You want: the product gets better with another person, the result becomes more valuable when seen by others, the user benefits when others join.

### 2. What outcome is so legible that people instantly get it?
What is the screenshot? What is the artifact? What is the one sentence someone says when they share it? Can a stranger understand the payoff in 2 seconds?

### 3. What makes sharing selfish?
Does posting make the user look smart, early, rich, creative, funny, or high-status? Does sharing improve their result? Does inviting others unlock access?

### 4. What identity does the product signal?
What does using this say about the user? What tribe does it place them in? What kind of person would proudly post this?

### 5. What emotional trigger causes the first transmission?
Flex? Outrage? Curiosity? Humor? Validation? Competition? Belonging? No emotional trigger, no real loop.

### 6. At what exact moment does the urge to share happen?
Not "after they use the product." What exact second? After a result? After a comparison? After a surprising insight? After a public win?

### 7. What frictions kill the handoff?
Extra clicks, login walls, ugly formatting, forced explanation, confusing artifacts. Most loops die from friction, not lack of brilliance.

### 8. What part of the loop compounds?
What happens after the first recipient sees it? What makes them act instead of consume? What makes them create their own version?

### 9. What variable reward keeps people coming back?
What changes each time? What improves? What gets ranked? What remains uncertain?

### 10. What makes people compare themselves to others?
Can users rank themselves? See who did better? Public proof? Reason to beat someone? Comparison is gasoline.

### 11. What subculture would abuse this first?
Who would over-post? What niche gets status from being early? What community already shares similar artifacts?

### 12. What is the repeatable format?
Series? Template? Challenge? Before/after? Recurring flex? If every share is custom, growth is harder.

### 13. What belief or narrative is riding underneath?
What bigger idea does this reinforce? What old way does it make obsolete? What worldview does using this support?

### 14. What would make the loop survive without us pushing it?
If we stopped posting, would distribution still happen? The real test of a loop vs a promo engine.

### 15. What false loop are we pretending is real?
Confusing paid traffic with virality? Mistaking founder personality for product transmission? Getting vanity shares with no activation?

## The 5 Things to Hunt

1. **The transmission trigger** — the exact moment a user wants to show or send it
2. **The portable artifact** — the screenshot, output, score, clip, template that travels
3. **The selfish incentive** — why sharing benefits the user directly
4. **The social conversion path** — how another person enters and why they act
5. **The compounding mechanic** — why one exposed person becomes a participant, not a dead-end viewer

## Loop Diagnostic Questions

### Mechanism
- What exactly repeats?
- What changes after each cycle?
- Why does the next cycle get stronger?

### Energy
- What powers the loop?
- What reward, pressure, or resource keeps it moving?
- What friction slows or kills it?

### Memory
- What persists from one cycle to the next?
- Where does learning accumulate?
- What prevents starting from zero every time?

### Selection
- What gets reinforced?
- What gets filtered out?
- What determines whether the loop improves or degrades?

### Compounding
What increases with each pass? Speed? Quality? Trust? Data? Density? Money? Reach? Resilience?

### Closure
- How do we know the loop actually completed?
- What real-world signal closes it?
- What stops fake internal "success" from counting?

### Fragility
- What breaks the loop?
- What causes drift?
- What creates runaway behavior?
- What makes the system optimize the wrong variable?

### Transfer
- What does one successful cycle make easier next time?
- Can the benefit move across tasks, users, or domains?
