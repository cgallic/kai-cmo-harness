# Algorithmic Authorship Framework

> **Use when:** Writing any content intended for SEO, Featured Snippets, AI Overviews, or Google AI Mode visibility.

## Quick Reference

- Structure sentences with main clause FIRST, conditions ("if", "because") SECOND
- Use numeric lists for steps/methods, bulleted lists for types/categories
- Keep sentences short, use anchor words between sequential sentences
- Name entities twice before switching to their attributes
- Bold the answer, not the query-matching terms

---

## The 31 Rules of Algorithmic Authorship

These rules optimize content for passage ranking and AI-generated answers. They come from analysis of Google's passage scoring methodology and Featured Snippet selection patterns.

### Sentence Structure Rules

**Rule 1: Put "if" statements in the second part of the sentence**
```
Wrong: "If a becomes B, do X."
Right: "Do X if a becomes B."

Wrong: "If a student studies, the student passes."
Right: "The student passes if the student studies."
```

**Rule 2: Put "because" clauses in the second part of the sentence**
```
Wrong: "Because of Y, the X doesn't behave..."
Right: "X doesn't behave... because of Y."
```

**Rule 3: Use certain/declarative sentences over conditional ones**
```
Wrong: "He will come to the school." (conditional future)
Right: "He comes to the school." (certain present)
```

**Rule 4: Start instruction sentences with a verb**
```
Wrong: "Lightly whip the cream."
Right: "Whip the cream lightly."
```

**Rule 7: Use shorter sentences - break complex ones apart**
```
Wrong: "The origin of Irish coffee relies on New York state, and in 1899 when a cab driver hits the woman who is the daughter of the governor of New York state, which was bigger than today."

Right: "The origin of Irish coffee relies on New York state. A cab driver hit a woman in 1899. The woman was the daughter of the New York State governor. New York state was bigger than today."
```

### List & Formatting Rules

**Rule 5: Use numeric lists for steps, methods, and comparisons**
```
Use NUMBERED lists for:
- "What are the best..."
- "What are the steps..."
- "How to do X..."

Use BULLETED lists for:
- "What are the types of X..."
- "What are the prominent sides of P..."
```

**Rule 6: Use preferential ordinal terms for instructions**
```
Good: "There are four steps for X. First, do X. Secondly, do Z. Third..."
```

**Rule 17: Use the same Part of Speech for first words in list items**
```
Verb Lists:
1. Ensures...
2. Clears...
3. Spends...
4. Absorbs...

Noun Lists:
1. Protein...
2. Carbohydrate...
3. Vitamin B...

Adjective Lists:
1. Small
2. Minimal
3. Medium Sized
4. Large
```

**Rule 18: Use the same phrase patterns in list elements**
```
Verb + Adjective + Noun pattern:
1. Ensures (V) a high level of (Adj) protein levels (N)
2. Clears (V) most of the toxins from (Adj) the blood (N)
3. Spends (V) intense (Adj) energy (N) for...

Noun + Verb pattern:
1. Vitamin B (N) provides (V) proper functioning...
2. Vitamin C (N) forms (V) collagen structures...
3. Vitamin D (N) helps (V) the body absorb...
```

**Rule 19: Always complete sentences - no trailing punctuation**
```
Wrong: "The following explains the reasons for amnesia:"
Right: "The reasons for amnesia are below."
```

### Entity & Anchor Rules

**Rule 8: Use anchor segments between sequential sentences**

Connect sentences with common/repeated words:
```
Wrong: "There are three main types of gold. The main types of gold are yellow, white, and X. The yellow gold type is brighter than the other two main gold types. White gold has the advantage of Z."

Right: "There are three main types of gold. The main types of gold are yellow, white, and X. There are different advantages of every main gold type. For example, the unique advantage of yellow gold is its brightness. The brightness of yellow gold is measured by C as P-value. The advantage of white gold is its durability. White gold is more durable than other main gold types because it has C-type atoms more than others."
```

**Rule 9: Use different lemmatized versions of predicates in sequential sentences**
```
"Lucid car produces electric cars since 2012. Lucid Cars are the 4th US Company that started producing Electric Cars..."
(produces → producing)

"Bitcoin selling requires understanding bitcoin price factors... To sell bitcoin, one should have..."
(selling → to sell)
```

**Rule 10: Use named entities at least twice before switching to attributes**
```
"The United States of America is located in North America. The United States of America has a coastline across the Pacific and Atlantic oceans. The coastline of the United States is longer than 129,000 km."

"Tesla Motors refers to an electric car manufacturer. Tesla Motors manufactures different types and models. The electric car models of Tesla Motors involve Model S, 3, and X. The fastest electric car model is Model S. Model S is the fastest electric car since 2019..."
```

**Rule 11: Use the attribute in questions only after mentioning the entity twice**
```
Pattern: Entity → Entity → Entity's Attribute → Attribute detail

"Wikipedia is the main source of information on the internet. Wikipedia is created by free editors. Authors of Wikipedia are not paid. Wikipedia's editors follow strict guidelines..."
```

**Rule 12: Use synonyms of attributes in sequential sentences**
```
"The love of God is above everything in the teachings of the Bible. The Bible states that dedication to God and God's commands is the only purpose of human life."
("love of God" ↔ "dedication to God" = half synonyms)
```

### Numeric & Evidence Rules

**Rule 13: Use numeric values, not vague quantifiers**
```
Wrong: "There are many results for doing X."
Right: "There are three main results for doing X. These are Z, Y, D."
```

**Rule 14: State main types/factors/effects before others**
```
Wrong: "There are 46 different reasons to do X."
Right: "There are 6 main reasons and 40 other reasons to do X. Total count of reasons for doing X is 46."
```

**Rule 15: Always use examples after declarations**
```
Wrong: "There are 40 different cryptocurrencies in Coinbase."
Right: "There are 40 different cryptocurrencies to trade in Coinbase including Bitcoin, Ethereum, and Litecoin."
```

**Rule 16: Use abbreviations in parentheses on first mention**
```
Wrong: "Bitcoin is the main cryptocurrency asset."
Right: "Bitcoin (BTC) is the main cryptocurrency asset for crypto trading platforms."
```

### Emphasis & Formatting Rules

**Rule 20: Bold the ANSWER, not the query-matching terms**
```
Wrong: "**Vitamin B** provides proper functioning for the body cells by converting food into energy."
Right: "Vitamin B provides **proper functioning for the body cells** by converting food into energy."
```

**Rule 21: Use "converging" for missing information**

Replace missing data with connected contextual information:
```
Wrong: "There are many EB5 Visa Consultants in the USA, some sources state it is more than 200,000."

Right: "There are 46,322 EB5 Visa Applications according to the American Attorney Association in January-June 2021. The EB5 Visa Application count increases on average by 23% every year. Thus, the demand for EB5 Visa Attorneys increases every year."
```

**Rule 22: Remove unnecessary words**
```
Wrong: "George W. Bush was also a member of the Foreign Affairs in the US."
Right: "George W. Bush was a member of the Foreign Affairs in the US."
(Remove words that don't add context or break grammar when removed)
```

**Rule 23: Don't reference earlier sections**
```
Wrong: "As it is shown in the article, the most dangerous poisons have been determined by the P method."
Right: "The most dangerous poisons have been determined by the P method."
```

**Rule 24: State the direction of factors (positive/negative)**
```
Wrong:
"Price factors for EB5 Visa consultancy:
1. Investor Conditions
2. Length of Experience"

Right:
"Price factors for EB5 Visa consultancy:
1. Length of Experience increases the pricing since it increases the chance of success.
2. The popularity of the location increases the cost. Populated areas have higher prices."
```

### Internal Linking Rules

**Rule 25: Don't add internal links to the first word of a sentence**
```
Wrong: "[Choosing the best food for your pet](link) requires too much research."

Right: "To choose convenient pet food for cats, one should understand cat biology. Understanding cat health and behavioral changes helps cat owners compare cat foods. [Choosing the right cat food](link) is processed by cat groomers like Anita Kelsey."
```

**Rule 26: Don't add internal links to the first sentence of a paragraph**

The first sentence needs to establish context before linking.

**Rule 27: Don't add internal links to the first line of a paragraph**

Context consolidation happens when related concepts are explained together. The first line doesn't provide enough opportunity for consolidation.

**Rule 28: Explain the connection before linking**
```
Wrong: "Similarities between Descartes and Kant involve epistemological approaches."

Right: "Descartes and Kant are two important philosophers from the Age of Enlightenment. René Descartes was born in 1596 and died in 1650. Immanuel Kant was the founder of German philosophy. Kant was influenced by Descartes. The scientific improvements between the 16th and 17th centuries are observed via [similarities between Descartes and Kant](link)."
```

**Rule 29: Match source page and target page nature**
- Link from informational content → comparison articles
- Link from e-commerce category → product pages
- Link from product pages → informational dimension pages
- Link from hub content → both comparison and product pages
- Link from subtype → superior types
- DON'T link from "vs." articles to definitional articles

**Rule 30: Match anchor text with the main entity in the heading**
- Add 1 internal link max per heading area
- Anchor text should contain the most important phrase in that heading
- Multiple internal links are acceptable if placed in an HTML list (counted as one block)

### External Resources Rule

**Rule 31: Integrate external resources contextually, not as footnotes**
```
Wrong: "The biotin is found effective for protecting the cell membrane in 2021 (1).
1. Wisconsin University, Dr. Najork Center, 2021."

Right: "Biotin is found effective for protecting the cell membrane in 2021, according to Dr. Nephew and Dr. Kotlin from Wisconsin University during the research of Effects of Kotlin on Cell Membrane."
```

## AI-Sounding Patterns to Avoid

These patterns are dead giveaways that content was AI-generated. Remove them during editing.

### The "Not X, It's Y" Reframe Pattern

This structure appears constantly in AI output. Rewrite as direct statements.

```
AI-sounding:
- "It's not X, it's Y"
- "The leverage is in X, not Y"
- "Not because X — because Y"
- "This isn't X. It's Y."
- "No X — just Y."
- "Context comes from X, not Y"

Human-sounding:
- State the point directly
- "The scripts do the heavy lifting" not "The leverage is in the scripts, not the model"
- "The channel sets the context" not "Context comes from where you ask, not what you ask"
```

### Unnatural Lists of Three

AI loves perfect triads. Real writing has varied rhythm.

```
AI-sounding: "The scripts are tight. The routing is clear. The guardrails are in place."
Human-sounding: "I spent time on scripts and routing and guardrails so the model doesn't have to be smart."
```

### Fake Secrecy Language

```
Avoid:
- "Here's the part nobody tells you"
- "The secret that experts don't want you to know"
- "Here's what most people miss"
```

### Permission Slip Language

```
Avoid:
- "This is your sign to..."
- "You're allowed to..."
- "It's okay to..."
```

### Conditional Statement Openers

```
Avoid:
- "If you've been struggling with..."
- "If you've been doing everything right but..."
```

## AI Mode Answer Components

Google AI Mode answers have 6 conditional components:
1. **Definition of Entity** with the Main Attribute
2. **Anchors** for every answer segment
3. **Listicles with Headwords** (term: explanation format)
4. **Listicles without Headwords** (simple lists)
5. **Section Names**
6. **Headword Explanations**

**Word/Character Limits:**
- 250-350 words typical
- 2000-3500 characters

**Answer Structure Pattern:**
1. Entity definition connecting to query attribute
2. Hypernym framing (broader category)
3. Main answer via generalization
4. Evidence expansion via query augmentation
5. Direction of augmentation is context-driven

## Checklist

Before publishing SEO content:

- [ ] Conditions ("if", "because") come AFTER main clauses
- [ ] Instructions start with verbs
- [ ] Sentences are short and declarative
- [ ] Numeric lists used for steps/methods; bulleted for types
- [ ] Named entities appear twice before switching to attributes
- [ ] Anchor words connect sequential sentences
- [ ] Examples follow every declaration
- [ ] Answers are bolded, not query terms
- [ ] Internal links placed after context, not in first line/sentence
- [ ] External sources integrated in-text, not as footnotes
- [ ] No "Not X, it's Y" reframe patterns
- [ ] No unnatural lists of three
- [ ] No fake secrecy or permission slip language
