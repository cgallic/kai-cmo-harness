# 50 Copywriting Formulas — Quick Reference

> **Use when:** Writing any marketing copy — ads, emails, landing pages, social posts, headlines. Find the right formula for the job.

---

## Headline Formulas (15)

### 1. [Number] Ways to [Desired Outcome]
"7 Ways to Double Your Email Open Rates"

### 2. How to [Desired Outcome] Without [Undesired Effort]
"How to Get More Clients Without Cold Calling"

### 3. [Do This] Like [Admired Entity]
"Write Landing Pages Like Apple's Marketing Team"

### 4. The Secret to [Desired Outcome] That [Authority] Won't Tell You
"The Secret to Ranking #1 That Your SEO Agency Won't Tell You"

### 5. Why [Common Belief] Is Wrong (And What to Do Instead)
"Why Posting More Often Is Wrong (And What to Do Instead)"

### 6. [Number] [Topic] Mistakes That Cost You [Negative Outcome]
"5 Ad Copy Mistakes That Cost You 40% of Your Budget"

### 7. What [Specific Number] of [Group] Taught Me About [Topic]
"What 500 A/B Tests Taught Me About Headlines"

### 8. [Desired Outcome] in [Timeframe] (Even If [Objection])
"10 New Clients in 30 Days (Even If You Have Zero Budget)"

### 9. The [Adjective] Guide to [Topic]
"The No-BS Guide to Facebook Ads for Law Firms"

### 10. I [Did Something Impressive]. Here's How.
"I Grew from 0 to 10K Subscribers in 90 Days. Here's How."

### 11. [Counterintuitive Claim]: Here's the Data
"Posting Less Gets You More Engagement: Here's the Data"

### 12. Stop [Common Mistake]. Start [Better Alternative].
"Stop Chasing Followers. Start Building a Newsletter."

### 13. [Specific Outcome] + [Specific Timeframe] + [Specific Method]
"30% More Leads in 60 Days Using This Email Framework"

### 14. Warning: [Something They're Doing] Is [Negative Consequence]
"Warning: Your About Page Is Costing You Clients"

### 15. The [Year] Guide to [Topic] (What's Changed)
"The 2026 Guide to SEO (What's Changed Since AI Overviews)"

## Body Copy Formulas (20)

### 16. PAS — Pain, Agitate, Solution
```
PAIN:     Name the specific problem they face
AGITATE:  Make it worse — show what happens if they don't solve it
SOLUTION: Present your product as the fix
```
Best for: Ads, email subject lines, landing page hero sections

### 17. AIDA — Attention, Interest, Desire, Action
```
ATTENTION: Pattern interrupt (bold claim, surprising stat)
INTEREST:  Expand — why should they care?
DESIRE:    Show the transformation or outcome
ACTION:    Clear, specific CTA
```
Best for: Landing pages, long-form ads, sales emails

### 18. BAB — Before, After, Bridge
```
BEFORE: Their current painful reality
AFTER:  The desired state they want to reach
BRIDGE: Your product is how they get from Before to After
```
Best for: Case studies, testimonials, email sequences

### 19. 4 Ps — Picture, Promise, Prove, Push
```
PICTURE: Paint a vivid scene of their desired outcome
PROMISE: Make a specific, bold promise
PROVE:   Back it with evidence (data, testimonials, case study)
PUSH:    Create urgency and drive action
```
Best for: Sales pages, webinar invites

### 20. FAB — Features, Advantages, Benefits
```
FEATURE:   What the product does (technical)
ADVANTAGE: Why that matters vs alternatives
BENEFIT:   What it means for their life/business
```
Best for: Product descriptions, comparison pages

### 21. The 1-2-3-4 Formula
```
1. What I've got for you
2. What it's going to do for you
3. Who am I? (credibility)
4. What you need to do next (CTA)
```
Best for: Cold outreach, short landing pages

### 22. Star-Chain-Hook
```
STAR:  The big idea or attention-grabbing opening
CHAIN: A chain of facts, benefits, reasons — each building on the last
HOOK:  The CTA that captures them
```
Best for: Persuasive articles, long-form ads

### 23. The So What? Test
After every claim, ask "So what?" and answer it.
```
"We use AI." → So what? → "It answers calls in 0.4 seconds."
→ So what? → "You never miss a client call again."
→ So what? → "That's an extra $4,000/month in revenue."
```
Keep going until you hit the real benefit.

### 24. Problem-Promise-Proof-Proposal (4P)
```
PROBLEM:  The pain they're experiencing right now
PROMISE:  The specific outcome you'll deliver
PROOF:    Evidence that you can deliver it
PROPOSAL: What to do next and what it costs
```
Best for: Proposals, high-ticket offers

### 25. APP — Agree, Promise, Preview
```
AGREE:   Start by agreeing with something they believe
PROMISE: Show them a better way
PREVIEW: Give them a taste of what they'll get
```
Best for: Blog post intros, email opens, webinar opens

### 26. SLAP — Stop, Look, Act, Purchase
```
STOP:     Pattern interrupt — make them pause scrolling
LOOK:     Earn their attention with a relevant visual/message
ACT:      Drive them to take a specific action
PURCHASE: Convert that action into a sale
```
Best for: Social media ads, display ads

### 27. The Bucket Brigade
Use transitional phrases to keep readers moving through copy:
- "Here's the thing..."
- "But wait, there's more."
- "Now it gets interesting."
- "Think about it:"
- "The bottom line?"
Best for: Blog posts, long-form content (SEO)

### 28. Open Loop / Curiosity Gap
Introduce information but don't complete it immediately.
```
"The #1 reason law firms lose clients has nothing to do with their legal skills. (I'll reveal it in a second, but first...)"
```
Best for: Threads, video hooks, email subject lines

### 29. The Objection Preempt
Address the objection before they think it.
```
"You're probably thinking: 'AI can't handle complex legal questions.'
You're right — and that's not what this does.
It answers, routes, and schedules. The lawyer handles the law."
```
Best for: Sales pages, FAQ sections, objection-heavy products

### 30. Future Pacing
Describe their life after using your product — in present tense.
```
"It's Monday morning. You walk into the office. Every call from the weekend is logged, transcribed, and sorted by urgency. The three hot leads are already in your CRM. You didn't miss a single one."
```
Best for: Sales pages, email closes, video CTAs

### 31. The Risk Reversal Stack
Stack guarantees until saying "no" feels riskier than saying "yes."
```
✓ 14-day free trial
✓ No credit card required
✓ Cancel with one click
✓ Full data export
✓ 30-day money-back guarantee
✓ Keep everything you created
```
Best for: Pricing pages, checkout pages, CTA sections

### 32. The Specificity Principle
Replace every vague word with a specific one.
```
VAGUE: "We help companies grow"
SPECIFIC: "We helped 127 law firms increase inbound calls by 340% in 90 days"
```
Specific claims are 2-3x more believable than vague ones.

### 33. One Reader Rule
Write to ONE specific person, not "users" or "businesses."
```
BAD:  "Companies can improve their customer service..."
GOOD: "You're losing 3 clients a week to voicemail. Here's how to fix it."
```

### 34. The Power of "Because"
Adding "because" + reason increases compliance 34% (Langer, 1978).
```
"Try it free because your first week of missed calls costs more than our annual plan."
```

### 35. The Contrast Principle
Show the alternative to make your offer look better.
```
"You could hire a receptionist for $3,500/month.
Or an answering service for $800/month.
Or KaiCalls AI for $99/month — and it never sleeps."
```

## CTA Formulas (10)

### 36. First-Person Action
"Start **My** Free Trial" > "Start **Your** Free Trial"

### 37. Value-Based CTA
"Get My Free SEO Audit" > "Submit"

### 38. Urgency + CTA
"Claim Your Spot — Only 7 Left"

### 39. Risk-Removal CTA
"Try It Free — Cancel Anytime"

### 40. Outcome CTA
"Start Getting More Calls" > "Sign Up"

### 41. Two-Option Close
```
A) Yes, I want more clients [Button]
B) No, I'll keep losing calls to voicemail [Plain text link]
```

### 42. Specificity CTA
"See How [Company Like Yours] Got 340% More Calls"

### 43. Social Proof CTA
"Join 500+ Law Firms Already Using KaiCalls"

### 44. Micro-Commitment CTA
"See Pricing" (lower commitment) → "Start Trial" (on pricing page)

### 45. Command CTA
"Stop Missing Calls. Start Now."

"{First name}, you're leaving ${amount} on the table"

"Why 500+ law firms switched to AI (and what happened next)"

## Formula Selection Guide

| Situation | Best Formulas |
|-----------|--------------|
| Cold audience (don't know you) | PAS, AIDA, Open Loop |
| Warm audience (know you) | BAB, 4P, Future Pacing |
| High-ticket / complex sale | Problem-Promise-Proof-Proposal, Objection Preempt |
| Social media ad (3 seconds) | SLAP, PAS (compressed), Curiosity Gap |
| Email subject line | Curiosity Gap, Direct Benefit, Question |
| Landing page hero | BAB, AIDA, PAS |
| CTA button | First-Person Action, Value-Based, Outcome |
| Blog post hook | APP, Bucket Brigade, Open Loop |
| Testimonial framing | Star-Chain-Hook, BAB |
| Pricing page | Contrast Principle, Risk Reversal Stack |
