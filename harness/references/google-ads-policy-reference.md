## 1. Prohibited Content

Content that is **never allowed** under any circumstances. Ads promoting these will be immediately disapproved and may trigger account suspension.

### 1.1 Counterfeit Goods

- Fake items that imitate brand features (trademark, logo, name) to pass off as genuine
- Knockoff designer goods, replica watches, fake electronics
- No exceptions — even if the ad discloses the item is a replica

### 1.2 Dangerous Products or Services

**Recreational drugs:**
- Chemical or herbal recreational drugs
- Psychoactive substances (synthetic cannabinoids, salvia, kratom in certain contexts)
- Equipment to facilitate drug use (bongs, rolling papers marketed for drug use)
- Instructional content on producing or obtaining drugs

**Weapons and explosives:**
- Firearms (guns, gun parts, gun kits)
- Ammunition and magazines
- Explosives and explosive-making instructions
- Combat knives, throwing stars, brass knuckles
- Tasers and stun guns (in most regions)
- **Exception:** Gun safety devices (trigger locks, gun safes) ARE allowed

**Tobacco:**
- Cigarettes, cigars, pipe tobacco, chewing tobacco
- E-cigarettes, vapes, and vaping liquids
- Component parts of tobacco products
- Products that directly facilitate tobacco consumption
- Hookah lounges and tobacco paraphernalia

**Other dangerous products:**
- Recreational drug-like substances (e.g., bath salts)
- Products causing injury when used as instructed
- Fireworks

### 1.3 Enabling Dishonest Behavior

**Deception and fraud tools:**
- Fake IDs, passports, diplomas, professional credentials
- Fake references or work history services
- Academic cheating services (essay mills, exam-taking services)
- Review manipulation services (fake reviews, social media engagement)
- Products to deceive financial institutions
- Ad click generators, traffic manipulation software

**Unauthorized access tools:**
- Hacking services and software
- Game-hacking or exploit software
- Radar/speed camera jammers
- Phone tapping or interception equipment
- Cable/satellite signal descramblers
- Lock-picking tools marketed for break-ins

**Unauthorized surveillance:**
- Spyware and stalkerware
- GPS trackers marketed for covert tracking of individuals
- Hidden cameras marketed for spying on people
- Intimate partner surveillance tools

**Enforcement:** 7-day warning before suspension. Part of the three-strikes system.

### 1.4 Inappropriate Content

- **Hate speech:** Content promoting hatred against protected groups (race, ethnicity, religion, disability, gender, age, veteran status, sexual orientation, gender identity)
- **Violence:** Graphic crime scenes, accident imagery, gratuitous violence
- **Bullying and intimidation:** Content targeting individuals for harassment
- **Animal cruelty:** Content depicting or promoting harm to animals
- **Self-harm:** Content promoting or instructing self-harm or suicide
- **Exploitation:** Content promoting sexual exploitation of minors — triggers immediate permanent suspension
- **Sensitive events:** Content exploiting natural disasters, mass shootings, public health crises
- **Profanity:** Excessive profane language in ad copy
- **Endangered species:** Sale or trade of products from endangered animals
- **Extortion or blackmail:** Content promoting these activities

## 2. Prohibited Practices

Behaviors that are **never allowed** regardless of the product or service being advertised.

### 2.1 Abusing the Ad Network

**Malware distribution:**
- Linking to sites that distribute viruses, trojans, worms
- Ads containing malicious code or forced downloads
- Compromised sites injecting scripts, PHP backdoors, hidden iframes

**Cloaking:**
- Showing different content to Google's review system vs. actual users
- Hiding the true destination users are directed to
- Using redirects to send users to a page different from what was reviewed

**Arbitrage:**
- Promoting destinations whose sole or primary purpose is showing more ads
- Made-for-advertising (MFA) pages with minimal content

**System circumvention:**
- Creating multiple accounts after suspension
- Misspelling prohibited words to evade detection
- Using hidden visuals or text to bypass policy review
- Manipulating ad settings to circumvent review systems
- "Gaming" auction mechanics

**Double serving / unfair advantage:**
- Running multiple ads across different accounts to dominate the same auction
- Creating duplicate accounts to gain unfair impression share

### 2.2 Data Collection and Use

**Required practices:**
- Clear disclosure of what data is collected and why
- User consent before collecting personal data
- Secure handling of sensitive information (SSL/TLS for forms)
- Compliance with applicable privacy laws (GDPR, CCPA, etc.)

**Prohibited practices:**
- Collecting data without disclosure or consent
- Collecting financial information (credit cards, bank accounts) on non-secure pages
- Misrepresenting data usage
- Collecting data from minors without parental consent
- Using remarketing lists built from medical records or health site visits

### 2.3 Misrepresentation

**False or misleading claims:**
- Omitting material information about the product, service, or business
- Hiding or obscuring billing details (how, what, when users will be charged)
- Making offers that aren't actually available (bait-and-switch)
- Claiming affiliations, endorsements, or qualifications that don't exist
- Making misleading claims about weight loss, financial gain, or health outcomes
- Collecting donations under false pretenses

**Dishonest pricing practices (enforced October 2025):**
- Advertising products at unrealistically low prices then pressuring upsells (bait-and-switch)
- Exploiting vulnerable individuals by demanding prices far above market rates
- Promoting apps as free when users must pay to install
- Offering free trials without clearly stating duration or auto-billing terms
- Hiding mandatory fees, surcharges, or add-on costs

**Impersonation:**
- Impersonating other brands, organizations, or public figures
- Claiming to be a government entity when you are not
- Misleading users about brand affiliation

**Enforcement:** Circumventing Systems and Unacceptable Business Practices are considered "egregious" violations — account suspended immediately without warning.

## 3. Restricted Content & Features

Content that CAN be advertised but only with specific certifications, geo-targeting, audience restrictions, or compliance requirements.

### 3.1 Sexual Content

**Completely prohibited:**
- Sexually explicit content (pornography)
- Escort services
- Adult live streaming services
- Deepfake pornography and AI-generated sexual images (banned May 2024 — immediate suspension)
- Sexual exploitation of any kind

**Allowed with restrictions:**
- Sexually suggestive but non-explicit content — limited formats and geographies
- Sexual health products — requires strict content guidelines and compliant landing pages
- Relationship counseling services — clean content only
- Contraceptive products — in approved regions

**Regional bans:** Vietnam, Thailand, and Middle Eastern nations prohibit all sexual content ads.

### 3.2 Alcohol

**Requirements when allowed:**
- Must comply with local laws and industry standards
- Cannot target minors (strict age-gating required)
- No glorification of excessive or irresponsible consumption
- Responsible drinking messaging required in some regions
- Must include alcohol content disclaimers where required
- Geotargeting limited to jurisdictions where alcohol advertising is permitted

**Completely prohibited in:** Many Middle Eastern countries, some South Asian countries.

### 3.3 Copyrights

- Cannot use copyrighted material without authorization
- Resellers and authorized distributors may need documentation proving legitimacy
- Copyright holder complaints trigger immediate ad review and potential takedown
- Content must be original or properly licensed

### 3.4 Gambling and Games

**Requirements:**
- Valid gambling license for the operating jurisdiction
- Google Ads certification (application required)
- Cannot target minors
- Must promote responsible gambling (include problem gambling resources)
- Country-specific: Google maintains an approved countries list
- Stricter certifications introduced for 2026

**Covers:** Online casinos, sports betting, poker sites, lottery services, daily fantasy sports, skill games with prizes.

### 3.5 Healthcare and Medicines

(See detailed section [9. Healthcare & Pharma Rules](#9-healthcare--pharma-rules))

**Key points:**
- Prescription drugs: restricted, certification required
- Online pharmacies: must be Google-certified (LegitScript or equivalent)
- Health claims: must be evidence-based, cannot promise cures
- Classified as "egregious" — violations trigger immediate suspension

### 3.6 Political Content

- Advertiser identity verification required
- Varies significantly by country — changes frequently
- Voter targeting and election-related messaging heavily regulated
- Must comply with campaign silence periods where applicable
- Must identify who paid for the ad
- **EU:** Google plans to stop serving political ads in the EU starting late 2026 due to regulatory complexity

### 3.7 Financial Services

(See detailed section [10. Financial Services Rules](#10-financial-services-rules))

**Key points:**
- Loans, credit, banking, insurance — disclosure requirements
- Cryptocurrency — certification required for exchanges/wallets
- Binary options — completely banned globally
- High-APR loans (>36% in US) — prohibited

### 3.8 Trademarks

- Cannot use trademarks in ways that confuse users about brand affiliation
- Trademark owner complaints trigger ad reviews
- Resellers CAN use trademarks when providing legitimate product information
- Authorized dealers can use brand names with proper documentation
- Google's trademark detection now covers international trademarks (expanded 2026)
- Must obtain written authorization from trademark holder to use their terms in headlines/descriptions

### 3.9 Legal Requirements

- Must comply with all applicable laws in targeted jurisdictions
- Age-gating required for age-restricted products
- Geographic restrictions based on local advertising laws
- Industry-specific licensing must be current and verifiable
- Tax and import regulations must be respected

### 3.10 Other Restricted Businesses

**Weight loss products:**
- No before/after comparison photos
- No unrealistic promises ("Lose 20 lbs in a week")
- No body-shaming language
- Must include "Results May Vary" disclaimers
- Must target 18+ only
- Products containing hCG (chorionic gonadotropin) are prohibited
- Cannot claim products are as effective as prescription drugs
- Claims require peer-reviewed evidence or FDA approval (as of 2025)

**Legal services:**
- Cannot guarantee legal outcomes
- Must include jurisdiction disclaimers per state bar rules
- Referral services must disclose they are not law firms
- Specialization claims must align with bar-recognized certifications

**Addiction treatment services:**
- LegitScript certification required (US, Canada, UK)
- Clinical trial ads must link to verified registries (ClinicalTrials.gov)
- Cannot exploit vulnerable populations

**Online dating:**
- Must comply with platform-specific requirements
- Cannot use deceptive imagery or misleading profiles

### 3.11 Ad Protections for Children and Teens

- Ad personalization disabled for users under 18
- Certain product/service categories cannot target minors
- Advertisers must respect audience age boundaries
- Health-based targeting of minors is strictly prohibited

### 3.12 Restricted Ad Formats and Features

- Dynamic ads availability varies by industry
- Some ad extensions unavailable for certain advertiser categories
- HTML5 ads have specific technical requirements
- Advanced features subject to advertiser/industry restrictions

## 4. Editorial & Technical Requirements

All ads must meet professional and editorial standards. Google requires ads that are clear, professional, and lead users to relevant, useful content.

### 4.1 Capitalization

**Prohibited:**
- ALL CAPS words (e.g., "FLOWERS", "BUY NOW")
- Random capitalization for emphasis (e.g., "FrEe ShIpPiNg", "FlOwErS")
- Gimmicky letter patterns (e.g., "F.L.O.W.E.R.S.")

**Allowed:**
- Standard title case or sentence case
- Legitimate acronyms (HIPAA, CRM, ASAP, NASA)
- Registered trademarks that use non-standard capitalization
- Coupon codes (e.g., "SAVE20")
- Common abbreviations

### 4.2 Punctuation and Symbols

**Prohibited:**
- Repeated punctuation ("Buy now!!", "Really???", "Sale!!!")
- Decorative or misleading symbols (heart, star, arrow: ♥, ★, ☞)
- Non-standard characters used to replace letters ("f1owers", "fl@wers")
- Non-standard superscripts
- Bullet points or asterisks for decoration ("*flowers*")
- Excessive or gimmicky use of periods ("F.L.O.W.E.R.S.")

**Allowed:**
- Single exclamation mark (one per ad, and only in descriptions — NOT in headlines)
- Asterisk for star ratings ("5* hotel") or legal conditions
- Standard currency symbols ($, EUR, GBP)
- Standard mathematical symbols when relevant
- Trademark symbols when legitimate (TM, (R))
- Trademarks/brand names that consistently use non-standard punctuation

### 4.3 Spelling and Grammar

- Proper spelling required in all ad components
- No intentional misspellings or "leet speak" (e.g., "Fr33", "OfF3r")
- Must comply with the grammar rules of the target language
- No excessive abbreviations unless part of a recognized brand name

### 4.4 Repetition

- Cannot repeat words, phrases, or symbols unnecessarily
- Using a keyword once is acceptable; repeating it multiple times violates standards
- **Not allowed:** "Cheap shoes, cheap shoes, cheap shoes online"
- **Allowed:** "Shop affordable shoes online"

### 4.5 Spacing

- No extra spaces between letters or words ("F R E E D E L I V E R Y")
- Irregular line breaks are prohibited
- Formatting tricks to draw attention are forbidden

### 4.6 Business Name

- Must use actual registered business or brand name
- Cannot add promotional phrases, taglines, or keywords to business name
- **Not allowed:** "Best Cheap Shoes Online - Free Delivery"
- **Allowed:** "ABC Shoes"

### 4.7 Phone Numbers

- Phone numbers belong in call extensions, not in ad text
- Do not place phone numbers in headlines or descriptions

### 4.8 Relevance

- Ad copy must be relevant to the product or service
- Must not use click-bait tactics that misrepresent the landing page content
- Sitelinks, callouts, and structured snippets must contain appropriate content
- Cannot use ad extensions for keyword stuffing

### 4.9 Image Quality (Display/PMax)

- Must be clear, high-resolution, distortion-free
- No blurry pictures, watermarks, or illegible text overlays
- No excessive filters or misleading edits
- Product images must represent actual items accurately

### 4.10 Video Quality (YouTube/Video ads)

- Clear visuals without pixelation
- Proper audio quality (no sudden loud noises or jarring sounds)
- No excessive flashing effects or distracting overlays
- Must be relevant to the advertised product or service

## 5. Destination Requirements

Rules for landing pages and websites that ads link to.

### 5.1 Functionality

- Landing page must be fully functional (no 404 errors, no server errors)
- Website must be navigable (working links, menus, back button)
- No "under construction" pages
- Mobile-responsive design required
- Sub-3-second load time recommended (Core Web Vitals)
- HTTPS mandatory — HTTP pages are disapproved

### 5.2 Content Match

- Landing page content must match ad copy promises
- The product or service advertised must be accessible on the landing page
- No bait-and-switch between ad and landing page

### 5.3 Malware and Security

- No malware, viruses, or harmful code on the destination
- No forced downloads or deceptive installers
- No phishing pages or credential harvesting
- No hacked or compromised sites (injected scripts, PHP backdoors, hidden iframes)
- No sneaky redirects to different domains

### 5.4 Redirects

- Auto-redirects from the final URL to a different domain are not allowed
- Destination URL must match the display URL domain
- No redirect chains that obscure the final destination

### 5.5 User Experience

- No excessive interstitial ads blocking main content
- Pop-ups must not prevent users from accessing content
- Must provide clear business contact information
- Must display privacy policy and terms of service
- No thin, duplicated, or auto-generated content
- Content must be useful and relevant

### 5.6 Destination Mismatch

- Final URL must match the actual landing page URL
- Display URL domain must match the final URL domain
- Mismatched URLs result in immediate disapproval

## 6. Character Limits by Ad Format

### 6.1 Responsive Search Ads (RSA)

| Component | Limit | Count |
|-----------|-------|-------|
| Headlines | 30 characters each | 3-15 headlines |
| Descriptions | 90 characters each | 2-4 descriptions |
| Path fields | 15 characters each | 2 paths max |
| Final URL | 2,048 characters | 1 |

### 6.2 Performance Max (PMax)

| Component | Limit | Count |
|-----------|-------|-------|
| Short headlines | 30 characters each | Up to 5 |
| Long headlines | 90 characters each | Up to 5 |
| Descriptions | 90 characters each | Up to 5 |
| Business name | 25 characters | 1 |

### 6.3 Responsive Display Ads

| Component | Limit | Count |
|-----------|-------|-------|
| Short headlines | 30 characters each | Up to 5 |
| Long headline | 90 characters | 1 |
| Descriptions | 90 characters each | Up to 5 |
| Business name | 25 characters | 1 |
| CTA text | Pre-defined options | 1 |

### 6.4 Video Ads (In-Stream)

| Component | Limit |
|-----------|-------|
| Headline | 15-90 characters |
| Description | 90 characters |
| Display URL | 35 characters |
| CTA button | 10 characters |

### 6.5 Video Discovery Ads

| Component | Limit |
|-----------|-------|
| Headline | 100 characters |
| Description line 1 | 35 characters |
| Description line 2 | 35 characters |

### 6.6 Shopping Ads

| Component | Limit |
|-----------|-------|
| Title | 150 characters |
| Description | 5,000 characters |
| Brand | 70 characters |
| GTIN | 50 characters |
| MPN | 70 characters |

### 6.7 App Promotion Ads

| Component | Limit | Count |
|-----------|-------|-------|
| Headlines | 30 characters each | Up to 5 |
| Descriptions | 90 characters each | Up to 5 |

### 6.8 Ad Extensions

| Extension Type | Component | Limit |
|----------------|-----------|-------|
| Sitelink | Link text | 25 characters |
| Sitelink | Description lines | 35 characters each (2 lines) |
| Callout | Text | 25 characters each (up to 10) |
| Structured snippet | Values | 25 characters each (3-10 values) |
| Price | Header | 25 characters |
| Price | Description | 25 characters |
| Promotion | Item | 20 characters |

### 6.9 Double-Byte Character Note

Chinese, Japanese, Korean, Arabic, and Cyrillic characters count as **2 characters each**. A 30-character headline limit becomes approximately 15 characters in these scripts.

### 6.10 General Rules

- Spaces count toward character limits
- Expanded Text Ads (ETAs) were discontinued June 30, 2022 — RSAs are now the primary format
- Call-only ads are being deprecated (no new creation from February 2026, stops serving February 2027)

## 7. Common Disapproval Reasons

Based on enforcement data from 2025-2026, these are the most frequent reasons ads get rejected.

### 7.1 Editorial Violations (Most Common)

1. **Capitalization:** ALL CAPS in headlines or descriptions
2. **Punctuation:** Multiple exclamation marks, repeated symbols
3. **Spelling/grammar:** Typos, intentional misspellings
4. **Prohibited language:** "Click here now", "Secret", "Miracle", "Unbeatable"
5. **Phone numbers in ad text** instead of call extensions

### 7.2 Destination Issues

6. **Broken landing page:** 404 errors, server down, slow loading
7. **Destination mismatch:** Final URL doesn't match landing page
8. **Non-crawlable URLs:** Google's crawler blocked by robots.txt or server config
9. **HTTP pages:** Not using HTTPS
10. **Excessive pop-ups:** Interstitials blocking content

### 7.3 Policy Violations

11. **Trademark infringement:** Using competitor trademarks without authorization
12. **Misleading claims:** Exaggerated results, fake urgency (fake countdown timers)
13. **Missing disclosures:** Hidden fees, unclear billing terms
14. **Restricted content without certification:** Healthcare, gambling, crypto
15. **Copyright infringement:** Using copyrighted content without permission

### 7.4 Image/Media Issues

16. **Low quality images:** Blurry, distorted, watermarked
17. **Misleading imagery:** Photos that don't match the product
18. **Inappropriate content:** Images that violate content policies

### 7.5 Account-Level Issues

19. **Identity verification incomplete:** Required in expanding list of regions
20. **Payment verification failure:** Suspicious payment activity
21. **Limited Ad Serving:** New or flagged accounts with reduced impressions (30-90% reduction)

### 7.6 Top Suspension Reasons (2025 data, 1000-case analysis)

| Violation | Share |
|-----------|-------|
| Circumventing Systems | 38% |
| Unacceptable Business Practices | 38% |
| Suspicious Payment Activity | 8% |
| Counterfeit Goods | 3% |
| Malware/Compromised Sites | 2% |
| Verification failures | ~5% |
| Terms & Conditions violations | ~3% |
| Multiple account policy | ~3% |

### 7.7 High-Risk Industries for Suspensions

Industries facing elevated suspension rates: affiliates and dropshippers, healthcare/pharma and supplements, cryptocurrency and finance, adult entertainment, flight resellers, online gaming, life coaches using funnel strategies, service trades (locksmiths, garage doors).

## 8. Claim Substantiation

### 8.1 General Rules

- Every claim in ad copy must be substantiated on the landing page
- Use specific, verifiable data points — not vague superlatives
- Superlatives without proof ("Best in class", "#1 solution") may be disapproved
- Claims of "guaranteed" results require evidence or must be backed by a money-back guarantee

### 8.2 Health Claims

- Must be backed by peer-reviewed evidence
- Cannot promise cures for serious medical conditions
- Skin care, weight loss, and anti-aging claims require peer-reviewed evidence or FDA approval (enforced 2025)
- Must cite primary sources: link to FDA, WHO, or national health authority guidance
- Include measurable endpoints in claims
- Cannot exploit vulnerable audiences

### 8.3 Financial Claims

- Cannot guarantee specific returns on investments
- Must include risk warnings for investment products
- Loan ads must display APR, repayment terms, total cost
- Performance claims must include disclaimers ("past performance does not guarantee future results")

### 8.4 Comparative Claims

- "Better than [competitor]" claims require substantiation
- Must be current, relevant, and backed by data
- Cannot use misleading comparisons

### 8.5 Testimonials and Endorsements

- Must be genuine and verifiable
- Cannot fabricate endorsements
- Celebrity or influencer endorsements must comply with FTC disclosure rules
- Reviews used in ads must be authentic

### 8.6 AI-Related Claims

- Claims about AI capabilities must be backed by competent and reliable evidence
- FTC's "Operation AI Comply" (launched 2024) actively enforces this
- Cannot overstate what an AI product or service actually does

### 8.7 Prohibited Phrasing

These phrases/words in ad copy often trigger disapproval:

**High risk of rejection:**
- "Guaranteed" (without substantiation)
- "Miracle"
- "Secret"
- "Free gift"
- "Unbeatable"
- "Click here now"
- "Missing link"

**Safer alternatives:**
- "Official", "Authorized", "Certified"
- "Transparent", "Reliable", "Secure"
- Specific data points and verifiable claims

## 9. Healthcare & Pharma Rules

Healthcare is one of the most heavily regulated categories. Violations are considered **egregious** — accounts are suspended upon detection without prior warning.

### 9.1 Prescription Drugs

**General rule:** Restricted. Certification required.

**Online pharmacies:**
- Must be Google-certified
- LegitScript certification required in US, Canada, UK
- NABP (National Association of Boards of Pharmacy) accreditation may also be required
- Can only operate in approved countries

**Pharmaceutical manufacturers:**
- Must be Google-certified
- Can advertise prescription and OTC drugs in select countries only
- Allowed countries include: US, Canada, UK, Australia, Austria, Belgium, Czechia, Denmark, Estonia, Germany, Indonesia, Israel, Japan, Kenya, Lithuania, New Zealand, Netherlands, Norway, Philippines, Portugal, Singapore, Slovakia, South Africa

**Prescription drug terms in ads:**
- Restricted — bidding on prescription drug terms requires certification
- Non-promotional use is permitted (regulatory warnings, legal notices, public health campaigns, academic publications)
- Google loosened restrictions in 2025 for non-promotional use of prescription drug terms

### 9.2 Over-the-Counter Medicines

- Generally allowed with restrictions
- Must comply with local advertising laws
- Claims must be supported by evidence
- Cannot make claims that exceed approved labeling

### 9.3 Health Supplements

- Subject to stricter review
- Cannot claim to cure, treat, or prevent diseases
- Certain ingredients may be prohibited in specific countries
- Must include appropriate disclaimers
- Products containing hCG (chorionic gonadotropin) are banned

### 9.4 Medical Devices

- Must comply with regulatory requirements in target markets
- Prescription medical devices require certification
- OTC devices have fewer restrictions but still need compliance

### 9.5 Addiction Treatment Services

- LegitScript certification required (US, Canada, UK)
- Cannot exploit vulnerable populations
- Must provide accurate information about treatment options
- Cannot guarantee treatment outcomes

### 9.6 Clinical Trials

- Ads must link to verified registries (e.g., ClinicalTrials.gov)
- Must provide accurate information about the trial
- Cannot make misleading claims about potential benefits

### 9.7 Telemedicine

- Must comply with telehealth regulations in target jurisdictions
- Online prescribing services require certification
- 2026 update: HCP (healthcare professional) targeting reintroduced for eligible B2B health advertisers

### 9.8 Weight Loss (Healthcare Context)

- No before/after comparison photos
- No unrealistic promises or guaranteed outcomes
- No body-shaming language
- Must target 18+ only
- Claims require peer-reviewed evidence or FDA approval
- "Results May Vary" disclaimers required
- Cannot claim products are as effective as prescription drugs
- Classified as a "sensitive interest category" — stricter scrutiny

### 9.9 Cosmetic Procedures

- Must comply with local medical advertising laws
- Cannot make unrealistic claims about results
- Before/after photos subject to restrictions

### 9.10 Certification Requirements Summary

| Service Type | Certification Needed |
|-------------|---------------------|
| Online pharmacy | LegitScript + Google certification |
| Pharmaceutical manufacturer | Google certification |
| Addiction treatment | LegitScript |
| Telehealth/online prescribing | Google certification |
| Health insurance | G2 verification (some regions) |
| Clinical trials | Verified registry listing |

**Timeline:** Initial certification typically takes 4-8 weeks.

### 9.11 Google Shopping for Healthcare

Late 2025: Google ramped up enforcement around Shopping ad eligibility for healthcare advertisers. Product listings for healthcare items face stricter review.

### 9.12 AdMob Pharmaceutical Changes (January 2026)

Authorized Buyers can now promote prescription drugs and prescription drug services in select markets without requiring Google certification. Regulatory responsibility shifts to the advertiser.

## 10. Financial Services Rules

Financial advertising is tightly regulated. Transparency, accuracy, and consumer protection are mandatory.

### 10.1 Completely Prohibited

- **Binary options:** Banned globally, no exceptions
- **High-APR personal loans:** APR exceeding 36% prohibited in the US
- **Short-term loans:** Personal loans requiring repayment in fewer than 61 days
- **Credit repair services:** Companies claiming to improve credit scores for payment
- **Fraudulent schemes:** Guaranteed returns, Ponzi schemes, get-rich-quick
- **Unlicensed financial services:** Products requiring regulatory approval without proper licensing

### 10.2 Allowed with Verification

| Product | Requirements |
|---------|-------------|
| Banking services | Standard compliance |
| Personal loans (61+ day, <36% APR) | APR disclosure, lender info |
| Credit cards | Fee disclosure, terms |
| Mortgage lending | Full terms disclosure |
| Investment services | Risk warnings, regulatory status |
| Wealth management | Fee structure, disclaimers |
| Insurance products | Licensed entity, policy limits |
| Pension services | Regulatory compliance |
| Financial planning | Qualification verification |

### 10.3 Cryptocurrency

**Certification NOT required for:**
- Merchants accepting cryptocurrency as payment
- Cryptocurrency mining hardware sellers
- NFT-based games with in-game item purchases (non-investment)
- Blockchain technology educational content

**Certification REQUIRED for:**
- Cryptocurrency exchanges (buying/selling/trading)
- Digital wallets for storing/transferring crypto
- Cryptocurrency trusts or investment funds
- Crypto CFDs (contracts for difference)
- DeFi borrowing and lending platforms
- DeFi insurance
- dApps and decentralized exchanges

**Prohibited:**
- Initial Coin Offerings (ICOs)
- DeFi trading protocols
- Promotion of the direct purchase, sale, or trade of cryptocurrencies (without certification)

**2026 update:** In-account certification capabilities for crypto exchanges, wallet providers, and complex speculative financial products (February 2026).

**Geographic requirements:**
- UK: Must register with the FCA (enforced January 15, 2025)
- EU, Australia, Singapore: Evidence of regulatory approval
- India, Singapore, Indonesia: Third-party G2 verification

### 10.4 Required Disclosures

**Credit and loan products:**
- Representative APR
- All fees and charges
- Repayment terms and schedule
- Total cost of the loan (example calculation)
- Consequences of non-payment
- Lender identification

**Investment services:**
- Risk warnings ("investments may lose value", "capital at risk")
- Fee structure
- Performance disclaimers ("past performance does not guarantee future results")
- Regulatory status and registration

**Insurance products:**
- Insurance type identification
- Underwriter information
- Key exclusions and limitations
- Premium factors
- Claims process details

**Cryptocurrency:**
- Volatility warnings
- Regulatory status
- Security information
- Fee transparency
- Jurisdictional limitations

### 10.5 Targeting Restrictions

- Cannot target users under 18
- Credit products often require 21+ targeting
- Consumer finance ads in US/Canada cannot target by gender, age, parental status, marital status, or ZIP code (fair lending compliance)

- [ ] Landing page content matches ad copy promises
- [ ] All claims are substantiated on the landing page
- [ ] Required disclosures included (financial, healthcare, legal)

- [ ] No misleading or exaggerated claims
- [ ] No trademark violations

- [ ] Privacy policy and terms of service on landing page
- [ ] Business contact information visible on landing page

## Appeal Process

If an ad is disapproved:

1. **Review** the specific policy cited in Google Ads Policy Manager
2. **Fix** the issue and resubmit (faster than formal appeal)
3. **Appeal** formally via Google Ads Help Center if you believe the disapproval was incorrect
4. **Secondary review** — request with supporting documentation if appeal is denied
5. **Timeline:** 3-7 business days for appeal review (often faster with AI processing in 2025-2026)

**Median suspension resolution:** 32 days (expert-managed cases resolve faster).
