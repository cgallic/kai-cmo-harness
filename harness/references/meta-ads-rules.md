# Meta (Facebook/Instagram) Advertising Policies — Harness Reference

*Last updated: March 2026. Source: Meta Transparency Center, Meta Business Help Center, and official policy documentation.*

This reference covers the complete Meta advertising standards. All ads running on Facebook, Instagram, Messenger, and Audience Network must comply with both Community Standards and Advertising Standards. Violations result in ad rejection, account warnings, restricted delivery, or permanent account disablement.

---

## 1. Prohibited Content (Hard Bans — Zero Tolerance)

These categories are completely banned. No exceptions, no workarounds, no approvals available.

### 1.1 Illegal Products and Services

- Products or services illegal in the target jurisdiction
- Content that facilitates or promotes illegal activity
- Jurisdictional bans applied based on reports from local governments, regulators, court orders, and NGOs

### 1.2 Discriminatory Practices

- Ads that discriminate or encourage discrimination based on race, ethnicity, color, national origin, religion, age, sex, sexual orientation, gender identity, family status, disability, or medical/genetic condition
- Applies to ad content, targeting, and landing pages

### 1.3 Tobacco and Related Products

- Cigarettes, cigars, chewing tobacco, pipe tobacco
- Electronic cigarettes, vaporizers, vape pens
- Nicotine-containing products
- Hookah and shisha products
- Tobacco accessories (rolling papers, vape cartridges)
- Exception: Smoking cessation programs (restricted, not prohibited)

### 1.4 Drugs and Drug-Related Products

- Illegal drugs, recreational drugs, controlled substances
- Drug paraphernalia
- Products facilitating illegal drug use
- Images depicting drug use or abuse

### 1.5 Unsafe Supplements

- Unapproved weight loss products, steroids, anabolic supplements
- Products containing unsafe substances or ingredients
- HCG, DHEA, ephedra, human growth hormone in supplement form
- Products making unverified physiological claims

### 1.6 Weapons, Ammunition, and Explosives

- Firearms, firearm parts, firearm accessories
- Ammunition and reloading supplies
- Explosives and explosive materials
- Knives designed for violence (switchblades, throwing stars)
- Pepper spray, mace, stun guns, tasers
- Fireworks (regulated by region)

### 1.7 Adult Content

- Nudity and sexually explicit content
- Sexual services and adult entertainment
- Sexually suggestive positioning or imagery
- Adult products and sex toys (some exceptions for health-framed products with age gating)
- Pornography or links to pornographic content
- Sexual solicitation and sexually explicit language

### 1.8 Surveillance Equipment

- Spy cams, hidden cameras, and recording devices marketed for covert surveillance
- GPS trackers marketed for tracking people without consent
- Phone-tapping or wiretapping equipment
- Spyware and keystroke loggers

### 1.9 Misleading, False, and Deceptive Content

- Content debunked by third-party fact checkers
- Deceptive claims about products, services, or businesses
- Get-rich-quick schemes and unrealistic income promises
- Clickbait headlines that misrepresent the destination content
- Fake news and fabricated stories
- Manipulated media intended to deceive
- Fraudulent business practices and data theft schemes
- Phishing attempts

### 1.10 Dangerous Organizations and Individuals

- Content supporting or praising terrorist organizations
- Content supporting organized hate groups
- Content promoting mass violence or criminal organizations
- Content created by or representing dangerous organizations

### 1.11 Misinformation and Vaccine Discouragement

- Content discouraging vaccination
- Advocating against vaccines
- False health claims tied to vaccines
- Health misinformation about public health emergencies

### 1.12 Payday Loans and Predatory Financial Products

- Payday loans
- Paycheck advances and salary advances
- Bail bonds
- Short-term loans of 90 days or less
- High-interest short-term lending (APR above certain thresholds)
- Penny auctions
- Binary options trading
- Initial Coin Offerings (ICOs)

### 1.13 Counterfeit and Infringing Products

- Counterfeit goods and knockoffs
- Products that infringe third-party intellectual property
- Unauthorized use of Meta brand assets (logos, trademarks)
- Fake documentation or identification

### 1.14 MLM and Deceptive Business Models

- Ads promoting MLM recruitment with income promises
- Pyramid schemes
- Deceptive business opportunity claims
- Misleading student loan services with exaggerated benefits

### 1.15 Non-Functional Landing Pages

- Landing pages that do not load or return errors
- Pages that disrupt browser navigation (auto-downloads, pop-up traps)
- Pages with content that does not match the ad
- Pages requiring app downloads before viewing content

### 1.16 Personal Health — Before/After Images

- Before/after transformation images for weight loss
- Before/after images for cosmetic procedures
- Before/after images for health treatments
- "Implied transformations" — showing a product next to a fit/healthy person (treated the same as before/after)
- Image sequencing that implies transformation (dated progress photos)
- Numerical testimonials implying health transformations (e.g., "A1C dropped from 8.2 to 6.4")

### 1.17 Spyware, Malware, and Exploitative Software

- Malware, viruses, trojan software
- Spyware and adware
- Software designed to exploit system vulnerabilities
- Cloaking tools or systems designed to circumvent ad review

### 1.18 Coordinating Harm

- Content coordinating real-world harm
- Inciting threats or public disruption
- Promoting or facilitating crime

### 1.19 Human Exploitation

- Human trafficking
- Exploitation of people in any form
- Forced labor promotion

### 1.20 Child Safety Violations

- Child sexual exploitation, abuse, or nudity
- Content sexualizing minors in any way
- Zero tolerance — triggers immediate account termination

### 1.21 Violent and Graphic Content

- Firearms, bladed weapons, or explosives pointed at the viewer
- Exposed organs, bones, or muscle tissue imagery
- Graphic depictions of violence, injury, or death
- Fictional images resembling real people in violent scenarios
- Cruelty to animals

### 1.22 Hateful Conduct

- Hate speech based on protected characteristics
- Dehumanizing speech or imagery
- Slurs and epithets targeting protected groups

## 2. Restricted Content (Allowed with Conditions)

These categories can be advertised but require additional approval, age-gating, targeting restrictions, disclaimers, certifications, or geographic limitations.

### 2.1 Alcohol

- Must comply with all applicable local laws, regulations, and industry codes
- Must include age-gating: 21+ in the US, legal drinking age in other jurisdictions
- Cannot target underage users
- Cannot associate alcohol with driving, operating machinery, or dangerous activities
- Cannot depict excessive or irresponsible consumption
- Cannot imply that drinking improves social, sexual, or athletic performance
- Geographic restrictions: banned in many Muslim-majority countries and other regions
- Must not appeal to minors through imagery, language, or characters

### 2.2 Dating Services

- Requires prior written permission from Meta
- Must target users 18+
- Cannot request information about sexual orientation or sexual preferences without explicit authorization
- Cannot use overtly sexual imagery
- Must accurately represent the service
- Must follow local regulations regarding dating services

### 2.3 Online Gambling and Gaming

- Requires prior written permission from Meta
- Must target users 18+ (or local legal age)
- Authorized advertisers must follow all applicable local laws
- Cannot make exaggerated promises of winnings
- Must include responsible gambling messaging where required by law
- Geographic restrictions apply (banned in many jurisdictions)
- Definition: any product/service where monetary value is part of entry method and prize

### 2.4 Online Pharmacies

- Two-step approval process required:
  1. Obtain LegitScript certification (reviews background, qualifications, state licensing, compliance, privacy practices)
  2. Apply to Meta for permission to advertise with LegitScript certification
- Cannot promote controlled substances
- Must be a legally operating pharmacy
- Must comply with all pharmaceutical advertising regulations

### 2.5 Subscription Services

- Services with negative options, automatic renewal, or free-to-pay conversion billing are restricted
- Must clearly disclose subscription terms, pricing, and cancellation process
- Must not obscure recurring charges
- Mobile marketing services restricted
- Clear opt-out mechanism must be available and disclosed

### 2.6 Financial Products and Services

- Advertisers must prove legal authorization if required by local regulations
- Meta may request documentation before allowing ads to run
- Must clearly disclose all terms, fees, risks, and conditions
- Cannot make false promises (guaranteed returns, risk-free investments)
- Interest rate/return claims must use Meta's standardized risk warning templates (as of 2026)
- Advertiser verification now mandatory in 38 countries
- Cannot collect sensitive financial information without user consent

### 2.7 Branded Content

- Must use Meta's branded content tool (Partnership Ads) for third-party partnerships
- All influencer/creator-promoted content (paid partnerships, gifted products, affiliate) requires Partnership Ads format (2026 rule)
- UGC-style ads without proper designation classified as "Deceptive Practice"
- Brands are equally liable for creator claims in boosted Partnership Ads
- Creator consent requires 90-day re-verification; previously granted permissions no longer indefinite

### 2.8 Cryptocurrency

**Three-Tier System (2026):**

| Tier | Category | Requirements |
|------|----------|-------------|
| Tier 1 | Regulated Exchanges (FCA/SEC licensed) | Awareness/download/conversion campaigns permitted with disclaimers |
| Tier 2 | Licensed Non-Custodial Products (wallets, analytics) | Quarterly compliance declarations required; promotional offers and yield messaging restricted |
| Tier 3 | Educational Content (informational, hardware wallets) | ROI claims and testimonials prohibited |

**Categorically Prohibited:** DeFi protocols, memecoins, ICOs, copy-trading platforms, yield farming

**Additional Requirements:**
- Targeting restricted to users 25+ (raised from 18)
- Risk disclaimers must appear in-creative (minimum 12pt font, 3 seconds on-screen in video)
- Requires pre-approval with regulatory licensing, custody insurance proof, and compliance certification
- Approved advertisers receive "Verified Crypto Advertiser" badge (required)
- EU campaigns require MiCA Article 29 compliance

### 2.9 Drug and Alcohol Addiction Treatment

- Requires prior authorization from Meta (US-targeted ads)
- Must be LegitScript-certified (reviews background, qualifications, state licensing, compliance, privacy)
- Two-step process: LegitScript certification first, then Meta approval application
- Cannot use personal attributes language ("Are you struggling with addiction?")
- Must comply with all applicable healthcare advertising regulations
- Cannot make misleading recovery claims

### 2.10 Cosmetic Procedures and Surgery

- Must target users 18+
- Cannot use before/after imagery (including implied transformations)
- Cannot generate negative self-perception to promote procedures
- Cannot declare or imply a "perfect" body type or appearance
- Cannot imply viewers need cosmetic procedures
- Must accurately represent outcomes and risks

### 2.11 Social Issues, Elections, and Politics

- Requires identity verification and "Paid for by" disclaimers
- Must be authorized through Meta's authorization process
- Includes: advocacy for/against social causes, ads by/about political figures and parties, election-related content, issue-based campaigns (climate change, healthcare reform, immigration, education)
- Geographic restrictions during election periods
- Falls under Special Ad Categories with targeting restrictions
- Requires verified Business Manager account status
- Cannot use misleading practices or emotion manipulation

### 2.12 Health and Wellness (General)

- Cannot directly reference a person's medical condition or mental state
- Cannot use second-person language: "you have," "are you suffering from," "do you struggle with"
- Cannot generate negative self-perception to promote products
- Cannot promise specific results in specific timeframes
- Must target 18+ for dietary, weight loss, health, and supplement products
- Weight loss ads cannot reference specific amounts or time-bound claims ("Lose 10 lbs in 30 days")
- Supplement disclaimer required in ad copy: "This product is not intended to diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent any disease"
- EU-targeted supplement claims verified against EFSA authorized health claims database
- Cannot claim to cure "incurable or terminal illnesses" (cancer, HIV, diabetes, autism, Alzheimer's, Parkinson's, ALS)
- Landing pages face equal scrutiny as ad copy

### 2.13 Hazardous Goods

- Products posing safety risks require appropriate warnings
- Must comply with local safety regulations
- Includes certain chemicals, flammable materials, and regulated household products

### 2.14 Historical Artifacts

- Sale of historical artifacts restricted
- Must comply with local and international antiquities laws

### 2.15 Human Body Parts and Bodily Fluids

- Restricted commerce in organs, blood, and other human materials
- Exceptions for legitimate medical/scientific contexts with proper authorization

### 2.16 Animals (Endangered and Non-Endangered)

- Cannot promote sale of endangered species
- Non-endangered animal sales restricted to proper channels
- Must comply with local wildlife protection laws

### 2.17 Commercial Exploitation of Crises

- Cannot exploit natural disasters, conflicts, or public health emergencies for commercial gain
- Cannot use crisis imagery to sell unrelated products

## 3. Special Ad Categories — Detailed Rules

Special Ad Categories exist to prevent discrimination in areas that significantly impact people's lives. Failure to declare the correct category results in ad rejection, reduced delivery, or account flagging. Meta's AI now auto-detects ads that should be classified but have not been.

### 3.1 Categories

| Category | Scope |
|----------|-------|
| **Housing** | Sale or rental of housing, real estate listings, homeowner's insurance, mortgage loans, housing repairs |
| **Employment** | Job listings, job fairs, employment services, employment opportunities |
| **Credit** | Credit card offers, auto loans, personal loans, business loans, mortgage lending, insurance, BNPL (as of 2026) |
| **Financial Products & Services** (as of Oct 2024) | Banking, savings, investment services, insurance — broader than just credit |
| **Social Issues, Elections, or Politics** | Political candidates, election-related, social cause advocacy, issue campaigns |

### 3.2 Targeting Restrictions (Housing, Employment, Credit, Financial)

These restrictions are non-negotiable and automatically enforced:

| Parameter | Restriction |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Age** | Fixed to 18–65+. Cannot narrow. Cannot select specific ages. |
| **Gender** | Must include all genders. Cannot be changed or narrowed. |
| **ZIP/Postcode** | Cannot use ZIP code or postcode targeting. |
| **Location Radius** | Minimum 15-mile (25 km) radius in US; 10-mile (17 km) in select European countries. |
| **Location Exclusions** | Cannot exclude specific locations. |
| **Detailed Targeting** | Many demographic, interest, and behavior filters unavailable. |
| **Detailed Targeting Exclusions** | Cannot exclude detailed targeting selections. |
| **Lookalike Audiences** | Disabled entirely. Cannot create or use. |
| **Special Ad Audiences** | Removed (as of Oct 2024). No longer available. |
| **Custom Audiences (Customer Lists)** | Restricted for housing, employment, and financial products (March 2025). Consumer reporting companies cannot use any customer lists. |
| **Advantage+ / Audience Expansion** | Unavailable for these categories. |

### 3.3 Geographic Scope

- Applies to all US-based advertisers
- Applies to advertisers targeting the US, Canada, and select European countries
- Expanding globally — now covers countries in Asia and Africa as well

### 3.4 HEC Auto-Detection (2026)

Meta's multimodal system now detects HEC content through:
- Image analysis: floor plans, "For Sale" signs, office settings, loan calculators
- Text analysis: keywords related to housing, employment, credit
- Landing page analysis: content that implies HEC categories

Attempting to circumvent HEC classification triggers account health damage and potential disablement.

### 3.5 Declaration Requirement

Advertisers must self-identify the correct Special Ad Category during campaign setup. Meta automatically updates saved audiences to comply with restrictions. Failure to declare is treated as evasion.

### 3.6 Timeline of Changes

| Date | Change |
|------|--------|
| 2019 | HUD Settlement launched Special Ad Categories |
| June 2022 | Expanded enforcement with certification requirements |
| Oct 2024 | "Financial Products & Services" became standalone category |
| Oct 2024 | Special Ad Audiences removed |
| Jan 2025 | Pixel and Conversions API integrations blocked for domains associated with Special Ad Category data |
| March 2025 | Customer list custom audience restrictions for housing, employment, financial |
| 2026 | BNPL and crypto lending classified under Credit category |

## 4. Targeting Restrictions (General)

### 4.1 Detailed Targeting Exclusions — Removed

As of March 31, 2025 (Ads Manager) and June 10, 2025 (boosted posts), detailed targeting exclusions are no longer available. Custom audience exclusions remain as an alternative.

### 4.2 Interest Category Consolidation

As of June 23, 2025, Meta consolidated many detailed interest categories into broader groupings. Sports, food, music genres, car models, and similar granular interests merged into higher-level categories. Starting January 15, 2026, ad sets using unavailable targeting options stop delivering.

### 4.3 Health and Finance Audience Restrictions

- Lookalike audiences and source custom audiences using data related to health and finances are restricted (September 2025)
- Custom and lookalike audiences whose names, rules, or metadata include or imply sensitive traits are flagged and disabled
- Custom conversions implying sensitive categories are flagged

### 4.4 Healthcare-Specific Tracking Restrictions

- Health and wellness brands cannot optimize for lower-funnel conversion events (sales, appointment bookings)
- Partial restrictions: most health/wellness brands
- Full restrictions: entities linking to sensitive web properties (patient portals)
- Cannot build audiences based on conversion events for health/wellness
- Cannot target users based on health conditions or behaviors tied to medical data
- Expected 2026 expansion to healthcare lead generation (Schedule, Lead, CompleteRegistration events)

### 4.5 Remaining Targeting Controls

Four main audience controls remain available:
- Age (within allowed ranges)
- Location (within allowed constraints)
- Brand protection exclusions
- Employment-related filters

### 4.6 Advantage+ Targeting

Meta's AI-powered targeting suggests audiences while machine learning identifies additional potential customers. Advertisers suggest, but Meta's algorithm has significant latitude. This is Meta's preferred approach as of 2026.

## 5. Ad Format Requirements

### 5.1 Text Limits

| Element | Limit | Notes |
|---------|-------|-------|
| Primary Text | 125 characters visible | More allowed but truncated with "See more" |
| Headline | 40 characters | Truncation varies by placement |
| Link Description | 25 characters | Not shown in all placements |

### 5.2 Image Specifications

| Placement | Recommended Size | Aspect Ratio |
|-----------|-----------------|--------------|
| Feed | 1080 x 1080 px | 1:1 (square) |
| Feed (portrait) | 1080 x 1350 px | 4:5 |
| Stories/Reels | 1080 x 1920 px | 9:16 (vertical) |
| Right Column | 1080 x 1080 px | 1:1 (square only — as of 2026) |
| Carousel | 1080 x 1080 px | 1:1 |
| Collection cover | 1080 x 1080 or 1080 x 1350 px | 1:1 or 4:5 (2026 update) |

- Maximum file size: 30 MB
- Accepted formats: JPG, PNG
- Minimum resolution: 1080 x 1080 px

### 5.3 Video Specifications

| Parameter | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| Format | MP4 (H.264 codec), MOV, or GIF |
| Maximum file size | 4 GB |
| Facebook maximum length | 240 minutes |
| Instagram Feed maximum | 2 minutes |
| Instagram Stories maximum | 15 seconds per card |
| Reels maximum | 30 seconds (expanded in 2026) |
| Optimal length | 15 seconds or less for best performance |
| Desktop/Mobile aspect ratio | 1:1 |
| Mobile-only aspect ratio | 4:5 or 9:16 |

### 5.4 Text-in-Image Rules

- The strict 20% text-in-image rule has been officially removed
- However, ads with less image text consistently perform better
- Excessive text may reduce reach, increase CPC, and lower engagement
- Best practice: use ad copy fields for text, keep images visual
- Headline fonts: ~42 pixels maximum recommended
- Body text fonts: ~24 pixels recommended

### 5.5 Safe Zones (Stories/Reels)

- Keep logos and important text 250 px from the top
- Keep important content 250–340 px from the bottom
- UI elements (profile info, CTA buttons) overlay these areas

### 5.6 Carousel Ads

- 2–10 cards per carousel
- Each card: 1080 x 1080 px (1:1 ratio)
- Each card can have its own headline and link
- Video cards supported

### 5.7 New Formats (2025–2026)

**Threads Video Ads:**
- Native video placement autoplaying in feeds
- Supports 16:9 and vertical aspect ratios
- Full-screen expansion on tap

**Creator Collabs / Partnership Ads:**
- Brand-influencer co-branded content with shared attribution
- Pre-built templates and performance insights
- Mandatory format for all paid influencer content

## 6. Community Standards that Affect Ads

All ads must comply with Meta's Community Standards in addition to Advertising Standards. Key areas:

### 6.1 Hate Speech

- No content that attacks people based on race, ethnicity, national origin, religious affiliation, sexual orientation, caste, sex, gender identity, serious disease, or disability
- Applies to text, images, video, and audio in ads

### 6.2 Violence and Incitement

- No threats of violence against individuals or groups
- No content inciting or facilitating real-world violence
- No glorification of violent acts

### 6.3 Bullying and Harassment

- No content targeting private individuals with harassment
- No degrading or shaming content
- No unwanted sexual attention

### 6.4 Suicide and Self-Harm

- No content promoting or glorifying suicide or self-harm
- No content depicting eating disorders positively
- Resources and awareness campaigns may be allowed with care

### 6.5 Misinformation

- Content rated false by third-party fact checkers is restricted
- Repeated misinformation violations reduce advertising ability
- Note: Meta transitioned to "Community Notes" model in Jan 2025 (US), replacing some third-party fact-checking with crowdsourced context

### 6.6 Privacy

- Cannot share or solicit others' personal information
- Cannot display personal data without consent
- Cannot use personal information obtained from Meta in advertising without following platform terms

### 6.7 Authenticity

- No fake accounts or misrepresentation
- No coordinated inauthentic behavior
- Ads must accurately represent the advertiser

## 7. Common Rejection Reasons (2026 Rankings)

| Rank | Reason | Share of Rejections |
|------|--------|:-------------------:|
| 1 | Personal Attributes violations | 24% |
| 2 | Misleading Claims | 19% |
| 3 | Undisclosed AI Content | 14% |
| 4 | HEC Category Evasion | 12% |
| 5 | Landing Page Mismatch | 11% |
| — | Other | 20% |

### 7.1 Personal Attributes (Most Common)

**What triggers it:** Any ad that asserts or implies knowledge of the viewer's personal characteristics.

**Prohibited phrasing patterns:**
- "Struggling with [condition]?" — REJECTED
- "Are you [attribute]?" — REJECTED
- "If you have [condition]..." — REJECTED
- "For people managing [condition]" — REJECTED (indirect framing now flagged in 2026)
- "Do you struggle with..." — REJECTED
- "Are you drowning in debt?" — REJECTED

**Safe alternatives:**
- "Discover new ways to manage stress" (instead of "Struggling with anxiety?")
- "Learn smart strategies for managing finances" (instead of "Are you drowning in debt?")
- "Our medication helps manage diabetes" (instead of "If you have diabetes, try our medication")
- "Support is available for those experiencing depression" (instead of "If you're feeling depressed, we can help")

**Protected attributes (cannot assert or imply):**
- Race, ethnicity, color
- Religion, beliefs
- Age (except broad references)
- Sexual orientation or practices
- Gender identity
- Disability
- Physical or mental health (including medical conditions)
- Vulnerable financial status
- Voting status
- Trade union membership
- Criminal record
- Name or personally identifiable information

**What IS allowed:**
- Broad geographic references ("American," "New Yorker")
- Passing references to age groups or ranges
- General lifestyle references not implying specific personal knowledge

### 7.2 Misleading Claims

- Exaggerated product benefits
- Unsubstantiated health or financial claims
- Clickbait that does not match the landing page
- Fake urgency or false scarcity
- Deceptive pricing or hidden fees

### 7.3 Undisclosed AI Content (New in 2026)

- Mandatory disclosure for any AI-generated, substantially modified, or composited visual/audio content
- Meta's automated detection scans for C2PA metadata and synthetic artifacts
- Non-disclosure triggers ad rejection and account policy strikes
- Advantage+ Creative variations are exempt
- Manually uploaded AI variations require individual disclosure tags

### 7.4 Landing Page Mismatch

- Ad promise differs from destination content (bait-and-switch)
- Landing page contains prohibited content even if ad does not
- Non-functional forms, broken links, or slow-loading pages
- Dark patterns or misleading checkout flows detected
- Mobile responsiveness required

### 7.5 Other Common Triggers

- Restricted language without disclaimers ("guaranteed," "instant," "best rates")
- Sensational creative (zoomed-in body parts, aggressive imagery)
- Rapid edits to ads after launch (triggers suspicious activity flags)
- Account history of violations (increases scrutiny on all future ads)
- Targeting violations on Special Ad Category ads

## 8. Claim Requirements and Substantiation

### 8.1 Health Claims

**Prohibited claims (always rejected):**
- Claims of curing incurable or terminal illnesses
- "Boosts metabolism," "cleanses toxins," "balances hormones," "improves circulation" — require medical substantiation most advertisers cannot provide
- Superlative claims: "most effective," "strongest formula," "ultimate solution" — require substantial evidence
- Specific outcome promises with timeframes: "Lose 10 lbs in 30 days"
- Guaranteed health outcomes of any kind
- Diagnosis or treatment implications without FDA-authorized language

**Required disclaimers:**
- Supplement ads MUST include: "This product is not intended to diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent any disease"
- EU supplement claims must be verified against EFSA authorized health claims database
- Diabetes messaging requires pre-approval in EU/APAC

**Allowed with care:**
- General wellness messaging without outcome promises
- Product feature descriptions (ingredients, dosage, form factor)
- Educational health content using third-person or passive voice
- Neutral framing: what the product does, not what it will do for "you"

### 8.2 Financial Claims

**Prohibited claims:**
- Guaranteed returns on investments
- Risk-free investment opportunities
- "Past performance" claims without standardized periods, fees, and risk language
- Misleading income opportunity representations

**Required disclosures:**
- All terms, fees, risks, and conditions must be clearly disclosed
- Interest rate and return claims must use Meta's standardized risk warning templates
- Financial authorization/licensing documentation must be available for review
- Proper regulatory disclaimers per jurisdiction

### 8.3 Testimonials and Results Claims

- Must be representative of typical results
- Cannot use outlier results as if they are typical
- Numerical health testimonials categorically rejected (e.g., specific lab values)
- Before/after results (including implied) prohibited for health/wellness/cosmetic

### 8.4 General Claim Rules

- All claims must be truthful and substantiatable
- Comparative claims ("better than," "faster than") require evidence
- Superlatives ("best," "#1," "leading") require third-party verification
- Price claims must be accurate and current
- Free trial claims must clearly disclose what happens after the trial

## 9. Healthcare and Wellness — Specific Rules

### 9.1 Sensitive Category Classification

As of 2025, health and wellness is a "sensitive category" with enhanced restrictions:
- Brands associated with medical conditions, health statuses, or provider/patient relationships are categorized and restricted
- Classification affects targeting, tracking, and optimization capabilities

### 9.2 Tracking and Conversion Restrictions

| Restriction Level | Applies To | Impact |
|-------------------|------------|--------|
| Partial | Most health/wellness brands | Cannot optimize for lower-funnel events (sales, appointments) |
| Full | Entities with sensitive web properties (patient portals) | All conversion tracking restricted |

- Cannot build audiences from health-related conversion events
- Cannot track bottom-of-funnel events if account is labeled health/wellness
- Server-side first-party tracking recommended to maintain some signal
- Expected 2026 expansion to restrict Schedule, Lead, CompleteRegistration events

### 9.3 Content Rules

- Cannot directly reference viewer's medical condition or mental state
- Cannot use second-person health language ("you have," "you suffer from")
- Cannot generate negative self-perception to sell health products
- Cannot imply diagnosis or treatment capability
- Cannot show before/after imagery or implied transformations
- Blood sugar claims require disclaimers
- Weight loss is the most restrictive sub-category

### 9.4 Weight Management (2026 Sub-Category)

- Subject to HEC-like targeting restrictions
- Targeting under 18 prohibited
- No specific weight loss amounts in claims
- No time-bound weight loss promises
- No before/after imagery of any kind
- No implied transformations

### 9.5 Online Pharmacy Requirements

1. LegitScript certification mandatory
2. Meta application and approval required
3. Cannot promote controlled substances
4. Must be legally operating pharmacy
5. Must comply with all pharmaceutical advertising regulations in target jurisdiction

### 9.6 Addiction Treatment Requirements

1. LegitScript certification mandatory (US)
2. Meta authorization required
3. Cannot use personal attributes language about addiction
4. Must comply with healthcare advertising regulations
5. Cannot make misleading recovery claims or guarantees

## 10. Financial Services — Specific Rules

### 10.1 General Requirements

- Advertisers must demonstrate legal authorization to operate in their jurisdiction
- Meta may require documentation review before ads are approved
- All terms, fees, risks, and conditions must be clearly and prominently disclosed
- Advertiser verification mandatory in 38 countries (as of 2026)

### 10.2 Prohibited Financial Products

- Payday loans, paycheck advances
- Bail bonds
- Loans of 90 days or less
- Binary options trading
- ICOs (Initial Coin Offerings)
- DeFi protocols, memecoins, copy-trading platforms, yield farming

### 10.3 Credit Advertising (Special Ad Category)

- Must declare Special Ad Category
- All HEC targeting restrictions apply (see Section 3.2)
- BNPL (Buy Now Pay Later) products now classified as Credit (2026)
- Crypto lending platforms now classified as Credit (2026)
- Financial disclaimers required
- Cannot make guaranteed return claims

### 10.4 Insurance and Investment Advertising

- Part of the expanded "Financial Products & Services" Special Ad Category (Oct 2024)
- Includes banking services, savings accounts, insurance, investment services
- Must follow all Special Ad Category targeting restrictions
- Risk disclosures required for investment products

### 10.5 Cryptocurrency (See Section 2.8 for full details)

- Three-tier system with escalating requirements
- Pre-approval mandatory
- Verified Crypto Advertiser badge required
- Age restriction: 25+
- In-creative risk disclaimers required

## 12. Enforcement and Review System

### 12.1 Multimodal Ad Review System (MARS) — 2026

Five simultaneous analysis layers:

| Layer | What It Checks |
|-------|---------------|
| **Text** | Semantic NLP detecting synonyms and euphemisms, not just keywords |
| **Visual** | Computer vision identifying before/after imagery, medical/financial visuals, HEC content |
| **Audio** | Speech-to-text transcription — verbal claims held to same standards as written copy |
| **Landing Page** | Real-time URL crawling for semantic consistency, dark patterns, misleading checkout flows |
| **Historical** | Cross-references advertiser violation history; prior violations trigger stricter thresholds |

### 12.2 Review Timelines

| Type | Timeline |
|------|----------|
| Standard ads | ~60 seconds automated review |
| Complex video | Up to 24 hours |
| Human review route | 48–72 hours |
| Edits to existing ads | Trigger full re-review |
| Budget/schedule adjustments | Do NOT trigger re-review |

### 12.3 Account Health Score (2026)

| Score | Impact |
|-------|--------|
| 50–100 | Normal operation |
| 25–49 | Increased review times, higher rejection rates |
| Below 25 | "Restricted Delivery" mode — severely limited impressions |
| Recovery | Gradual improvement over 90 days of clean operation |

### 12.4 Appeal System (Three Tiers)

| Tier | Process | Timeline |
|------|---------|----------|
| Tier 1 | Automated re-review | 24 hours, 35% false positive resolution rate |
| Tier 2 | Human policy team review | 3–5 business days |
| Tier 3 | Policy Advisory Board escalation | 10–15 business days |

### 12.5 Account Recovery

Disabled accounts may:
1. Complete compliance training
2. Submit corrective action plan
3. Pass re-certification quiz
4. If reinstated: 30-day probation with mandatory manual review before impressions serve

## 13. Quick Compliance Checklist for Ad Creation

Use this checklist before submitting any Meta ad:

- [ ] Ad does not contain prohibited content (Section 1)
- [ ] Restricted content has required approvals and disclaimers (Section 2)
- [ ] Correct Special Ad Category declared if applicable (Section 3)
- [ ] No personal attribute assertions or implications (Section 7.1)
- [ ] No before/after imagery or implied transformations
- [ ] No second-person health/financial language ("you have," "you suffer from")
- [ ] All claims are truthful and substantiatable (Section 8)
- [ ] Required disclaimers included in ad copy (supplements, financial, etc.)
- [ ] AI-generated content properly disclosed
- [ ] Landing page matches ad content and is fully functional
- [ ] Landing page is mobile-responsive and loads quickly
- [ ] Age-gating set correctly (18+ for health/wellness/cosmetic, 21+ for alcohol in US, 25+ for crypto)
- [ ] Image specs met (correct dimensions, under 30 MB, JPG/PNG)
- [ ] Video specs met (MP4/H.264, under 4 GB, correct aspect ratio)
- [ ] Text within recommended limits (125 primary, 40 headline, 25 description)
- [ ] Minimal text overlay on images
- [ ] Partnership Ads format used for any influencer/creator content
- [ ] Geographic targeting complies with product restrictions

## 14. Account Ban Triggers — Patterns to Avoid

These behavioral patterns increase risk of account restriction or disablement:

- Repeated ad rejections across campaigns
- Frequent edits and budget changes immediately after launch
- Multiple accounts promoting the same offer
- Circumventing review systems (cloaking, domain switching)
- Promoting restricted services without proper approval
- Sudden spending spikes on new accounts
- Constant audience/creative changes (triggers suspicious activity flags)
- Prior violation history (creates escalating scrutiny)
- Ignoring Special Ad Category requirements
