# Amazon Advertising Policies — Comprehensive Reference

> Last updated: March 2026
> Sources: Amazon Ads Policy Center, Amazon Creative Acceptance Policies, Amazon Stores Guidelines, industry compliance guides

This document covers every major policy area enforced by Amazon Ads (Sponsored Products, Sponsored Brands, Sponsored Display, DSP). Use it as a pre-flight checklist before creating any Amazon ad content. Violations can result in ad disapproval, campaign suspension, or account restriction.

## 1. Prohibited Content

Content that is **never allowed** under any circumstances. Ads containing these elements will be immediately disapproved.

### 1.1 Deceptive & Misleading Content

- Deceptive or misleading content or ad experiences
- False claims about products, services, or pricing
- Inaccurate statements about prices, promotions, or discounts
- Fake endorsements or testimonials
- Pressure tactics or urgency-based manipulation without genuine scarcity

### 1.2 Hate Speech & Discrimination

- Hate speech or offensive, intolerant, discriminatory, or harassing content
- Content targeting individuals or groups based on protected characteristics

### 1.3 Sexual & Explicit Content

- Nudity, sexually explicit, and sexually suggestive content
- **Exception:** Appropriate entertainment, art, healthcare, or science contexts
- Realistic depictions of bodily fluids

### 1.4 Controversial & Sensitive Topics

- Content revolving around highly debated social topics
- Content exploiting critical events (natural disasters, incidents of mass trauma or casualty)
- Content exploiting sensitive life events (pregnancy, job changes, marital status, medical events, death)

### 1.5 Child Exploitation

- Content with minors that exploits or depicts minors in a dangerous, sexual, or otherwise exploitative manner
- Child product ads that directly address children using persuasive language or interactive elements

### 1.6 Substance Abuse

- Content depicting persons under the influence of drugs or alcohol
- Promotion of illicit drugs or excessive alcohol/substance consumption

### 1.7 Violence & Gore

- Gratuitous violence, blood, and gore
- Sexual violence
- Animal cruelty
- Violence toward minors
- Self-harm content

### 1.8 Profanity & Offensive Language

- Uncensored profanity
- Uncensored sexually explicit language
- Gratuitously offensive language

### 1.9 Brand Impersonation

- Unauthorized content or elements that mimic Amazon branding, color schemes, fonts, or user interface elements
- False affiliation claims with Amazon

### 1.10 Surveillance

- Content promoting unauthorized surveillance or monitoring of a person without their knowledge or consent

### 1.11 Cultural Violations

- Products that violate cultural norms in the locale in which they are served

### 1.12 Other Prohibited Content

- ASCII art
- Children in dangerous situations (near firearms, explosions, alcohol, drugs)
- Content glorifying smoking or promoting physical/mental harm
- External contact details, phone numbers, emails, QR codes
- Links outside Amazon (except social media buttons; brand website URLs in videos permitted without CTAs)
- Cruelty toward people or animals
- Shipping/delivery claims
- Time-sensitive promotions without actual basis
- Disease or addiction diagnosis or treatment claims

## 2. Prohibited Products & Services

Products and services that **cannot be advertised** on Amazon under any circumstances.

### 2.1 Political & Religious Content

- Political campaign ads supporting or opposing candidates or parties
- Content related to elections, legislation, or contested public issues
- Religious services, advocacy, fundraising, proselytizing, or discriminatory religious content
- **Exception:** Religious books and merchandise are allowed
- Astrology and mysticism services

### 2.2 Adult Products

- Pornography and sexually explicit products
- Sexual enhancement products and sexualized costumes
- Fetish/bondage wear and sex toys
- Sexual services including escorts and prostitution

### 2.3 Drugs & Controlled Substances

- Illegal drugs and drug paraphernalia
- Products claiming to cure addiction
- All tobacco and nicotine products (cigarettes, e-cigarettes, pouches, heat-not-burn, hookah)
- Items containing or implying THC, CBD, or hemp content
- Imagery associated with illicit drugs

### 2.4 Weapons

- Guns, knives, and associated accessories
- Products concealing or resembling realistic weapons
- Instructional guides for building or using weapons

### 2.5 Deceptive & Harmful Products

- Products or services that facilitate deceptive or dishonest behavior
- Products or services that cause damage, harm, or injury
- Malware, spyware, and scams
- Counterfeit or intellectual property-infringing items

### 2.6 Other Globally Prohibited Products

- Human trafficking or sexual exploitation facilitators
- Military/law enforcement/medical professional-exclusive devices
- Permanent tattoo devices that puncture skin
- Premium pay-per-call services
- Products harming animals or containing endangered species materials
- News and journalism content
- Specific foods prohibited by local law
- Infant formula (where legally prohibited)

### 2.7 Country-Specific Prohibitions

| Country/Region | Prohibition |
|----------------|------------|
| Australia & New Zealand | Ticket resellers |
| Denmark, Finland, France, Germany, Netherlands, Norway, Poland, Sweden, Switzerland | Scientology/Dianetics content (e.g., L. Ron Hubbard publications) |
| Singapore | Chewing gum |
| United Kingdom | HFSS food product ads (high fat/sugar/salt), except Ofcom-regulated channels with 9 PM-5 AM dayparting |

## 3. Restricted Content & Products

These categories **may be allowed** with placement, supply, and scheduling restrictions. Requirements vary by marketplace.

### 3.1 Alcohol

- Advertising allowed with placement, supply, and scheduling restrictions
- Stores cannot mix alcohol and non-alcohol related products
- Alcohol beverage stores are prohibited in Canada, Japan, India, and the USA
- Legal age verification requirements apply
- No health benefit claims for alcohol
- Responsible consumption messaging may be required

### 3.2 Dating & Related Services

- Subject to placement, supply, and scheduling limitations
- Must comply with age-appropriate content standards

### 3.3 Financial Products & Services

- Encompasses cryptocurrencies and related offerings
- Must include appropriate disclaimers and risk disclosures
- Credit and employment-related advertising limitations apply

### 3.4 Healthcare Products & Services

- Medical and health-related items with compliance requirements
- No disease treatment claims without proper substantiation
- Evidence-based claims only
- Regulatory compliance required in all target jurisdictions

### 3.5 Real Money Gaming / Gambling

- Subject to advertising restrictions
- Geographic and licensing requirements apply

### 3.6 Entertainment & Books

- Movies, books, and video games with content restrictions
- Content ratings must be disclosed where applicable

### 3.7 Infant & Baby Products

- Age-sensitive category with specific restrictions
- Must meet safety standards
- Age-appropriate warnings required

### 3.8 Charities

- Restricted category requiring compliance with specific placement rules

### 3.9 Public Service Announcements

- Subject to placement and scheduling constraints

### 3.10 Employment & Housing

- Credit and employment-related advertising limitations apply
- Must not discriminate based on protected characteristics

### 3.11 Outdoor Recreational Weapons

- Sports equipment and knife sales restricted
- Age restriction compliance required
- Cannot glorify violence

### 3.12 Event Days

- Day-parting and scheduling restrictions during specific periods

- Safety information required
- No misleading performance claims
- Proper usage instructions

### 4.7 Political Products (Merchandise)

- Balanced, non-inflammatory content
- No partisan messaging beyond product itself
- Accuracy in political claims

## 5. Claim Substantiation

### 5.1 General Rule

All advertising claims must be truthful and supported by evidence. If you make a claim about your brand, that claim must be substantiated within the Store/ad, with the date and evidence to support it.

### 5.2 Evidence Requirements

- Source and date of evidence must be identified
- Source can be advertiser's data or third-party data, depending on claim type
- Data must not be more than **18 months old** (exception: efficacy claims, such as "Up to 100% flake free," can be older)

### 5.3 Claims Requiring Substantiation

**Survey-based claims:**
- Claims about consumer survey results must be substantiated even if the survey collected subjective opinions
- Example: "33% of people like XXX" must include a disclosure citing source and date

**Quantified performance claims:**
- Claims implying objective measurement (e.g., "Reduces 99% of wrinkles," "Teeth appear three shades whiter")
- Must be supported with citation of an independent source, dated within previous 12-18 months

**Comparative claims:**
- Must cite independent source from previous 12-18 months
- Direct competitor comparison is prohibited (cannot name competing products)

**Accolades and awards:**
- Require date and awarding body details
- Ideally less than 18 months old

**Warranty and guarantees:**
- Must clearly describe limitations and responsible party

### 5.4 Substantiation Placement

Evidence must be discoverable on the product detail page or the product packaging of associated products.

### 5.5 Exceptions

Superlative claims referencing only a brand's own products do NOT require substantiation:
- "Our most powerful vacuum cleaner"
- "[Brand]'s most advanced formulation yet"

## 6. Creative Requirements

### 6.1 Editorial Standards

**Text requirements:**
- Must be in the primary language of the Amazon site
- Correct grammar and punctuation required
- No incomplete sentences, typos, or misspellings
- No excessive punctuation (no "!!!" or "???")
- Sentence case recommended
- No random mixed capitalization (e.g., "THIS headline is GREAT")
- Proper capitalization for abbreviations (DIY) and brand names is acceptable
- No profanity or obfuscated language

**Ad copy accuracy:**
- Ads must be clear and accurate so customers receive correct information
- Ad copy is always required when the ad contains special offers, sweepstakes, or promotions
- Text must generally display horizontally (vertical text only acceptable when brief and easily readable, such as logos)

### 6.2 Headline Rules

**Sponsored Brands headlines:**
- Must be relevant to at least one product in the ad
- CTA cannot be button-shaped
- Video ad headlines: up to 50 characters

**Call-to-Action (CTA) requirements:**
- Must be clear and direct
- "Click here" and non-specific CTAs are prohibited
- Must link to product pages, grids, or other Store pages
- Acceptable CTAs: "Shop now," "Learn more," "See details," "Pre-order now," "Buy tickets," "Rent now," "Rent or buy now," "Get a quote"
- Prohibited language: "Last Chance," "Don't Miss Out," or emotionally pressuring phrasing

### 6.3 Image Specifications

**eCommerce Display Creatives (recommended uploads):**
- Square image: 1200 x 1200 px (1:1 ratio)
- Tall image: 900 x 1600 px (9:16 ratio)
- Wide image: 1200 x 628 px (1.91:1 ratio)

**Brand Store:**
- Logo: 1:1 aspect ratio, minimum 400 x 400 px
- All text legible: minimum 32px in Hero/gallery/full-width tiles, 16px for other tiles
- Content outside safe zones may be cropped on some ad sizes

**Video ad logos:**
- Minimum width: 600 px
- Minimum height: 100 px
- Maximum file size: 100 KB

**Image quality standards:**
- Must be high-resolution and professional
- No low-quality, blurry, or pixelated images
- No misleading visual representations
- No heavily edited or unrealistic product depictions
- Must accurately represent products
- Demonstrate inclusivity and diverse representations

### 6.4 Video Specifications

- Brand logo, text headline, and CTA link appear alongside the video player (templated layout for mobile and desktop)
- Videos start without audio by default; customers can enable audio by tapping the mute toggle
- Must support both sound-on and sound-off viewing
- Professional production standards
- Readable text overlays (minimum size standards)
- Mobile-optimized playback
- Begin with product focus, end with clear logo/branding
- Local language matching target market

### 6.5 Brand Store Requirements

- Must be suitable for general audiences
- Cannot denigrate another company or product
- Cannot leverage another company name or product to promote other products
- Brand logo required so customers can identify the advertiser
- Brand logos must not be on a complex graphical background and must be visually appealing
- All products listed must be active, in-stock Amazon listings

### 6.6 Holiday & Promotional Messaging

- Must align promotions with actual sale dates
- Use verified discount percentages
- Avoid misleading "limited time" language without genuine scarcity
- Holiday-specific claims must match actual campaign timing

## 7. Amazon-Specific Rules

### 7.1 Amazon Branding References

- Cannot use Amazon trademarks without permission
- No false affiliation claims with Amazon
- "Sold on Amazon" or similar permitted only if accurate
- Cannot mimic Amazon branding, color schemes, fonts, or UI elements
- Prime-related claims only when applicable to offerings

### 7.2 Competitor References

- Cannot denigrate another company or product
- Cannot use competitor names to promote other products
- Direct competitive comparison is prohibited in claims
- No competitive disparagement

### 7.3 Pricing Claims

- No false savings claims or price comparisons
- Pricing must be accurate and verifiable
- Promotions must match actual availability and timing
- Discount percentages must be verified

### 7.4 Customer Reviews

- Cannot fabricate or incentivize fake reviews
- Must represent genuine customer experiences
- Ratings cannot be manipulated

### 7.5 Subscribe & Save References

- Only mention if applicable to advertised products

### 7.6 Product Listings

- All promoted products must be active, in-stock Amazon listings (ASINs)
- Do not promote unavailable or out-of-stock items
- Keywords and product targeting must align with actual product offerings

## 8. DSP Specific Policies

### 8.1 Audience Policies

- Advertisers may not use Amazon or third-party audiences as proxies for reaching customers based on prohibited categories or any protected/sensitive personal characteristics
- All content must be appropriate for a general audience and for the type of placement
- Must be honest about promoted products/services and avoid misleading or offensive content

### 8.2 Non-Endemic Advertising

- Businesses that do not sell in the Amazon store can use DSP to reach audiences
- Policies and eligibility vary by placement
- For major events, certain non-endemic categories face restrictions on homepage and event pages

### 8.3 Programmatic Display Rules

- Managed-service option typically starts at ~$50,000 USD (varies by country)
- Self-service option available through Amazon or partners
- Both managed and self-service content policies are the same as core Amazon ad policies
- Must use clear, compliant imagery and concise copy
- Retargeting permitted for users who viewed products or abandoned carts

### 8.4 DSP Creative Standards

- Follow all standard Amazon creative requirements
- Display ad creative sizes must match specified dimensions
- Include clear, visible CTAs
- Maintain brand consistency
- Ensure mobile responsiveness
- Fast-loading optimization required

## 9. Common Rejection Reasons

### 9.1 Editorial Issues

- Grammar or spelling errors
- Excessive punctuation or capitalization
- Sensationalized language
- Incomplete sentences

### 9.2 Misleading Claims

- Unsubstantiated product claims
- False savings or pricing claims
- Claims lacking required substantiation evidence and dates
- Misleading before-and-after comparisons

### 9.3 Product Issues

- Unavailable or out-of-stock product listings
- Inaccurate product information
- Products in prohibited categories

### 9.4 Creative Quality

- Low-quality or pixelated imagery
- Illegible text (below minimum pixel sizes)
- Missing brand logo in Stores
- Non-professional creative execution

### 9.5 Policy Violations

- Competitive disparagement or naming competitors
- Misleading Amazon brand element usage
- Time-sensitive promotions without genuine basis
- Missing required disclaimers

### 9.6 Content Issues

- Distracting or attention-hijacking elements
- Misleading visual representations
- Inappropriate or offensive content

### 9.7 Submission Best Practice

Amazon recommends submitting ads **at least one week in advance** of launch date to allow time for approvals and possible resubmissions. Rejected advertisers receive an email explaining the reason.

## 10. Recent Policy Changes (2025-2026)

### AI Agent Policy (Effective March 4, 2026)

Amazon's Business Solutions Agreement update introduces formal requirements on AI agents and automated software systems:
- AI agents must identify themselves
- Must comply with new agent-specific rules
- Continued use of Amazon selling services after March 4, 2026 constitutes acceptance

### Amazon Marketing Cloud Access (September 2025)

Starting September 16, 2025, AMC became directly accessible through the Amazon Ads Console without requiring a partner or rep:
- Pre-built, no-code templates available
- AI assistance included
- Advanced analytics more accessible to all advertisers

### 2025 Ad Quality Overhaul

Amazon revamped ad policies in 2025 to:
- Improve overall ad quality standards
- Refine performance metrics
- Require sellers to adjust advertising strategies to align with updated guidelines
- Enhanced transparency and better performance tracking
- Stricter enforcement of misleading claims

### 2026 AI Advertising Tools (Q1 2026)

- Full Funnel Campaigns launching
- Amazon Ads Agent (AI-powered campaign management)
- Growth in programmatic buying through DSP for video, CTV, and display inventory

### DSP Pricing Changes

- 0% charge for programmatic guaranteed deals on Amazon's owned media
- 1% charge on open-web buys (significantly lower than competitors)

### HFSS Restrictions (UK)

High fat/sugar/salt food product ads restricted in the UK, except on Ofcom-regulated channels with 9 PM-5 AM dayparting restrictions.
