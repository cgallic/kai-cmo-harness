# TikTok Advertising Policies — Harness Reference

*Last updated: March 2026. Sources: TikTok Ads Manager Help Center, TikTok Community Guidelines, TikTok Shop Seller Center, TikTok for Business Blog, and official policy documentation.*

This reference covers TikTok's complete advertising standards for ads running on TikTok (In-Feed, TopView, Spark Ads, Branded Effects, Search Ads) and TikTok Shop. Every ad is reviewed before going live (typically within 24 hours). Violations result in ad rejection, account warnings, frozen ad accounts, mandatory compliance reviews, or permanent account termination.

---

## 1. Prohibited Content (Hard Bans — Zero Tolerance)

These categories are completely banned across all TikTok ad formats. No exceptions, no approvals available.

### 1.1 Illegal Products and Services

- Products or services illegal in the target jurisdiction
- Content that facilitates or promotes illegal activity
- Products subject to economic or trade sanctions
- Recalled products
- Products that present significant safety risks to consumers

### 1.2 Tobacco, Vaping, and Nicotine

- Cigarettes, cigars, pipe tobacco, chewing tobacco
- Electronic cigarettes, vaporizers, vape pens
- E-liquids and vape cartridges (with or without nicotine)
- Nicotine pouches, patches, and gums marketed as tobacco alternatives (not cessation)
- Hookah and shisha products
- Parts or components used in e-cigarettes or vapes
- Heat-not-burn tobacco products (IQOS, etc.)
- Smokeless, dissolvable, or combustible tobacco products
- Branded content promoting tobacco or vaping is also prohibited

### 1.3 Drugs and Drug Paraphernalia

- All illegal drugs, recreational drugs, and controlled substances
- Chemicals, ingredients, or raw materials used to manufacture drugs or addictive substances (marijuana, opium, etc.)
- Drug paraphernalia: bongs, glass pipes, rolling papers, grinders
- Pill presses and die molds
- Products listed on FDA warning letters as unsafe or misbranded
- THC-infused edibles, CBD oils, hemp-derived supplements (regardless of state legality)
- Any ingestible or topical products containing cannabinoids
- Content depicting or promoting recreational drug use
- Products enabling substance misuse (e.g., whipped cream chargers marketed for inhalation)

### 1.4 Weapons, Ammunition, and Explosives

- Firearms: guns, pistols, rifles, shotguns, automatic weapons
- Replica and imitation firearms (including airsoft guns)
- Ammunition and reloading supplies
- Military ordnance
- Explosive materials and fireworks
- Knives, swords, and bladed weapons marketed for combat
- Stun guns, tasers, and pepper spray
- Brass knuckles and other martial arts weapons
- Manufacturing instructions for any weapons
- Sport and hunting guns

### 1.5 Adult and Sexual Content

- Nudity and sexually explicit content
- Sexually suggestive or provocative scenes and behavior
- Sexual services: escort services, prostitution, erotic massages
- Adult entertainment: strip clubs, host/hostess clubs
- Products designed for explicit sexual activity (dildos, vibrators, BDSM gear, etc.)
- Erotic clothing and sexually suggestive materials
- Pornography or links to pornographic content
- Sexual solicitation and sexually explicit language
- Sugar dating or compensated companionship services

### 1.6 Gambling (General Prohibition)

- Online gambling and real-money betting platforms
- Offline gambling promotion
- Gambling-like activities and mystery value products
- Lottery ticket sales
- Casino games with real-money outcomes
- Products with unknown or variable worth that function like gambling
- Exception: Limited paid advertising may be allowed with TikTok's explicit permission, strict age-gating, and compliance with all local laws (see Section 2.6)

### 1.7 Counterfeit and Infringing Products

- Counterfeit goods and knockoffs
- Products that infringe third-party intellectual property (copyright, trademark)
- Gray market products
- Unauthorized use of TikTok brand assets
- Fake documentation or identification
- Unauthorized branded electronics

### 1.8 Endangered Species and Wildlife

- Living animals (sale or trade)
- Products made from endangered species
- Protected plants and plant products
- Items derived from wildlife protected under CITES or local laws
- Historical artifacts made from protected animal materials

### 1.9 Political Advertising

- TikTok does not allow ANY paid political advertising
- This includes ads for/against political candidates, parties, or political figures
- Ads about elections, referendums, or ballot measures
- Issue-based political advocacy ads
- Creators being compensated for making branded political content
- Use of TikTok Promote tool for political content
- Political deepfakes of candidates or officials (permanent ban, year-round in most jurisdictions, 90-day pre-election ban in remaining)
- Non-consensual synthetic media depicting government figures making false statements

### 1.10 Misleading and False Content

- Exaggerated, overstated, or misleading claims about product performance
- Claims that cannot be proven or substantiated
- Malicious claims or comparisons accusing competitors of serious violations
- Clickbait that does not match the landing page (bait-and-switch)
- Fake urgency or false scarcity tactics
- Deceptive pricing or hidden fees
- Discrepancies between ad and landing page on promotion, price, or discounts
- False expressions or exaggerated information
- Unsubstantiated health, financial, or product claims

### 1.11 Frauds and Scams

- Financial scams: fake investment offers, get-rich-quick schemes
- Phishing and identity theft attempts
- Job and transaction scams
- Content teaching scam or fraud methods
- Money muling
- Multi-level marketing (MLM) and pyramid schemes
- Trading fake currency, forged documents, or stolen information/data

### 1.12 Discriminatory Content

- Ads that discriminate or encourage discrimination based on race, ethnicity, national origin, religion, age, sex, sexual orientation, gender identity, disability, or any other protected status
- Content that incites hatred against any group
- Hate speech-related merchandise

### 1.13 Harmful and Dangerous Content

- Content depicting or promoting violence, self-harm, or behaviors leading to injury
- Gruesome or graphic images likely to shock or scare users
- Content promoting eating disorders
- Products mentioning specific forms of self-injury or suicide
- Dangerous acts and challenges

### 1.14 Engagement and Fake Services

- Buying/selling likes, followers, comments, or views
- Fake engagement services
- Bot services for social media manipulation

### 1.15 Non-Functional Landing Pages

- Landing pages that do not load or return errors
- Pages not optimized for mobile devices
- E-commerce sites without clear refund/return policies
- Pages requiring sensitive personal information without a privacy policy
- Pages with language inconsistent with ad language and targeted region
- Landing pages with auto-downloads, pop-up traps, or browser manipulation

## 2. Restricted Content (Allowed with Conditions)

These categories can be advertised but require approval, age-gating, targeting restrictions, disclaimers, certifications, or geographic limitations.

### 2.1 Alcohol

**General Requirements:**
- Must promote products permissible in approved countries
- Must clearly state the alcoholic content (ABV) and strength of the beverage
- Must carry a responsible drinking disclaimer on ad content AND landing pages
- Must obtain and submit all necessary licenses from authorized regulators
- Must work with a TikTok sales representative to determine eligibility
- No retail or e-commerce advertisements promoting the sale of alcohol or alcohol delivery services

**Age-Gating:**
- US and Canada: Must target users aged 25+ (not 21+)
- Other markets: Must target legal drinking age minimum
- Cannot feature people below the age of 25 or pregnant people

**Prohibited in Alcohol Ads:**
- Targeting or appealing to people below legal drinking age
- Offering alcohol as a prize or reward
- Promoting offers or incentives encouraging alcohol consumption (e.g., discounts)
- Portraying excessive drinking, intoxication, or reckless behavior under the influence
- Associating alcohol with driving or dangerous activities

**Country-Specific Restrictions:**
- **UK, Ireland, Australia**: Alcohol brand advertising is prohibited (including wine, beer, spirits, low-alcohol, and 0% ABV beverages). Bars, pubs, and drinking establishments may be promoted if targeting 18+ only.
- **Muslim-majority countries**: Generally prohibited
- **Singapore, South Africa, Malaysia, Thailand**: 0% alcohol beverages may be allowed with specific disclaimers and age restrictions

### 2.2 Financial Services

**Allowed (with conditions):**
- Banking services, personal loans, credit cards, debit cards, bank accounts
- Tax preparation and budgeting tools
- Insurance products
- All require TikTok's explicit permission
- All must target users aged 18+

**Required Disclosures:**
- Interest rates, annual percentage rates (APR), transaction fees
- Repayment periods and representative examples
- All disclaimers and terms on landing pages
- Risk warnings where applicable

**Restricted (requires enhanced approval):**
- Credit building or repair services
- Buy Now Pay Later (BNPL) services
- Investment and trading platforms
- Forex trading

**Prohibited:**
- Pyramid schemes and MLM opportunities
- Get-rich-quick schemes
- Too-good-to-be-true financial offers
- Payday loans and paycheck advances
- Bail bonds
- Penny auctions
- Binary options
- Ads promoting single securities or other tradable financial assets
- Unaccredited digital banks performing traditional bank-like functions
- Debt assistance programs making unrealistic claims

### 2.3 Healthcare and Pharmaceuticals

**General Rules:**
- All healthcare and pharmaceutical products must comply with local laws and regulations in each market
- Must target users aged 18+

**Allowed (with market-specific requirements):**
- Over-the-counter (OTC) medicines
- Medical institutions and pharmacies
- Medical devices, tools, and equipment for diagnosing, treating, or monitoring conditions

**Prohibited:**
- Branded content for pharmaceuticals, healthcare, and medicine products
- Medical institution branded content
- Cosmetic clinic and cosmetic surgery branded content
- Contraceptive product and service branded content
- Claims of curing diseases
- Guaranteed health outcome claims
- Before-and-after visuals for health or body transformation
- Language like "cures acne," "guarantees weight loss," or "clinically proven to heal"
- Unapproved pharmaceuticals or supplements claiming unrealistic health benefits

### 2.4 Weight Management and Body Image

TikTok maintains a body-positive and inclusive policy:

**Prohibited:**
- Weight loss supplements: fat burning pills, appetite suppressants, detox teas, weight loss lollipops
- Weight loss services including weight loss surgery advertising
- Meal replacement products marketed primarily for weight loss (shakes, bars, drinks)
- Carbohydrate control supplements marketed for weight loss
- Products designed for weight loss or muscle gain
- Products where weight loss/muscle gain claims are the primary marketing focus
- Before-and-after body transformation imagery
- Dramatic weight loss claims or time-bound promises

**Allowed (with care):**
- General fitness and wellness products not focused on weight loss
- Nutrition products marketed for general health (not weight loss)
- Exercise equipment marketed for fitness (not weight loss)

### 2.5 Dating Services

**Allowed in most markets (with conditions):**
- Must target users aged 18+
- Must not feature discriminatory, exploitative, dishonest, or sexually inappropriate content
- Must not promote apps designed purely for casual sex

**Prohibited in specific markets:**
Algeria, Bahrain, Bangladesh, Egypt, Iraq, Jordan, Kuwait, Lebanon, Morocco, Oman, Pakistan, Qatar, Saudi Arabia, Turkey, United Arab Emirates

**Prohibited dating-related content:**
- Sugar dating or compensated companionship
- Transactional relationship services

### 2.6 Gaming and Gambling

**Non-Casino Games (allowed with conditions):**
- Must work with TikTok sales representative for eligibility
- Must restrict ads to age requirement based on game rating
- Must not involve real money or financial rewards
- Must be clear that players cannot win real money

**Social Casino Games (allowed with conditions):**
- Must work with TikTok sales representative
- Must be free-to-play
- In-app purchases allowed if no money-out options
- Must target appropriate age group

**Online Gambling (allowed in limited cases):**
- Requires TikTok's explicit permission
- Must comply with all local laws and regulations
- Must include responsible gambling messaging
- Must apply strict age-targeting restrictions
- Geographic restrictions apply heavily

### 2.7 Cryptocurrency and NFTs

**Generally Prohibited:**
- Cryptocurrency wallets
- Cryptocurrency credit or debit cards
- Initial coin offerings (ICOs)
- Token sales
- Promotion of individual digital currencies or tokens
- DeFi protocols, memecoins, copy-trading platforms

**May Be Allowed (with strict conditions):**
- Ads for regulated cryptocurrency exchanges (must hold trade license permitting financial virtual business)
- Must target users aged 18+
- Must include compliance disclaimers
- Must provide evidence of regulatory accreditation or certification
- Beta program in US and Canada allows crypto companies registered with approved regulatory bodies to run ads to users 18+

**Completely Banned by Region:**
- Vietnam: All crypto content and advertising
- Iran: All cryptocurrency transactions and promotions
- Egypt: All forms of crypto promotion

**NFT Restrictions:**
- Indonesia: NFT advertising permitted only with BAPPEBTI and Ministry of Communication approvals

### 2.8 Food and Dietary Supplements

**Food Products:**
- Generally allowed with standard advertising rules
- Must not make unauthorized health claims
- Must comply with local food advertising regulations

**Dietary Supplements:**
- TikTok Shop: Invite-only category — must be specifically invited to sell
- Must submit vitamin/supplement category certification
- Cannot claim to diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent disease
- Cannot use medical or health claims as primary marketing angle
- Must comply with FDA/local regulatory requirements

**Energy Drinks:**
- Allowed, but restricted to users aged 18+

**Vitamins and Health Supplements:**
- Restricted in some regions
- Claims must be substantiated and compliant with local regulations

### 2.9 Contests, Sweepstakes, and Promotions

**Requirements:**
- Must include official rules with: campaign start/end date, eligibility criteria, entry method, prizing and value
- Must include acknowledgment that the promotion is in no way sponsored, endorsed, or administered by TikTok
- Entry must NOT require payment or purchase
- Entrants must have a TikTok account
- Must comply with all applicable federal, state, and local laws

**Prohibited:**
- Requiring contestants to use copyrighted music, photos, brand logos, or other copyrighted material for entries
- Contests that function as gambling (require monetary consideration for chance-based prizes)

### 2.10 Cosmetic Procedures and Surgery

- Branded content for cosmetic clinics and cosmetic surgery is prohibited
- Cannot use before/after imagery
- Cannot generate negative self-perception to promote procedures
- Must target 18+ where allowed through paid ads

## 4. Creative Requirements

### 4.1 Video Specifications

| Parameter | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Aspect Ratio** | 9:16 (recommended), 1:1, 16:9 supported |
| **Resolution** | Minimum: 540x960 (9:16), 640x640 (1:1), 960x540 (16:9) |
| **File Size** | Maximum 500 MB |
| **Duration** | 5 seconds to 10 minutes (ads under 5s or over 10min rejected) |
| **Optimal Duration** | 9-15 seconds for best engagement |
| **File Types** | .mp4, .mov, .mpeg, .3gp, .avi |
| **Codec** | H.264 recommended |

### 4.2 TopView Ad Specifications

- Premium full-screen placement on app open
- Vertical (9:16) format required
- Higher resolution requirements than standard In-Feed
- Limited inventory — requires reservation through TikTok sales team

### 4.3 Text and Caption Rules

- Captions do not support clickable links, @mentions, or hashtags
- Ad captions displayed in white with uniform font (not customizable)
- Must be free of spelling or grammatical mistakes
- Must not use symbols incorrectly among letters
- Avoid excessive or informal use of capitalization, spacing, numbers, symbols, or punctuation
- Language must be consistent with targeted region

### 4.4 Safe Zones

- **Side margins**: 80px from edges
- **Top**: 160px clear of creative or branding elements
- **Bottom**: 440px clear (TikTok UI overlays — likes, comments, share buttons)
- Critical content and CTAs must not be placed in areas where TikTok's UI will obscure them

### 4.5 Audio and Music Rules

- Sound is fundamental: 93% of TikTok users watch with sound on
- High-quality, clear audio required — no muffled or unclear sounds
- **TikTok Commercial Music Library**: Pre-cleared tracks safe for advertising use — use these
- Cannot use copyrighted music without proper licensing
- Cannot use another creator's audio without authorization
- AI-cloned voices require disclosure and documented consent

### 4.6 Image Quality Requirements

- Must not feature blurry, unclear, or unrecognizable visuals
- Must not use columns or pixels to partially cover images
- Must not have black bars or pixelation covering content
- High-quality, dynamic, and engaging content preferred
- Materials should include real people or high-quality animations with a storyline

### 4.7 Landing Page Requirements

- Must be functional and load properly on mobile devices
- Must work on the network of the targeted market
- E-commerce ads must display complete and accurate information
- Must include valid contact details (telephone, email) as required by local law
- Lead ads must include a privacy policy
- Cannot request sensitive information (personal ID, financial info, health info, biometric data) to access the site
- Product/service shown in ad must appear on landing page
- Consistent language between ad and landing page
- Must have clear refund/return policies for e-commerce

### 4.8 Branded Content Requirements

- All branded content must use TikTok's Content Disclosure Setting
- Must toggle disclosure when marketing your own business, product, or service
- Must toggle for branded content involving incentives (reviews, endorsements, compensation)
- All branded content must follow TikTok's Branded Content Policy, Ads Creative Policy, and Industry Entry Policy
- Undisclosed commercial content becomes ineligible for the For You Page (FYP)
- Repeated non-disclosure leads to posting restrictions or account bans

- Most ad categories: 18+ targeting required
- Alcohol (US/Canada): 25+ targeting required

### 5.2 Restrictions on Targeting Users Under 18

**Markets with limited teen targeting:** US, Canada, European Economic Area (EEA), UK, Switzerland, Latin America

**When targeting under-18 audiences, advertisers can ONLY use:**
- Location

**Unavailable for under-18 targeting:**

**No personalized ads for teens (13-17) in:**
- European Economic Area, UK, Switzerland

If primary objective is reaching under-18 audiences, use awareness-focused campaigns:

## 6. Spark Ads Specific Rules

### 6.1 What Spark Ads Are

Spark Ads allow brands to promote existing organic TikTok posts (their own or authorized creator content) as paid ads. All engagement (likes, shares, comments, follows) is attributed to the original post.

### 6.2 Authorization Process

**For brand-owned posts:**
- No Spark code needed
- Select directly via creative picker in Ads Manager

**For creator content:**
1. Creator navigates to target post
2. Creator accesses "Ad settings" via three-dot menu or share menu
3. Creator toggles "Ad authorization" to ON
4. Creator selects authorization duration: 30, 60, or 365 days
5. Creator generates and copies the Spark code
6. Brand enters code in Ads Manager: Tools > Creative Assets > Spark Ad posts > Apply for Authorization

### 6.3 Key Restrictions

- **Captions cannot be edited** after post authorization — content must be finalized before authorization
- Posts must remain **public** and eligible for promotion
- Authorization durations are **fixed** — cannot extend an existing code
- If the post is edited after authorization, a new code may be required
- Posts inheriting the creator's original aspect ratio (cannot be changed)

### 6.4 Compliance Requirements

- Brands become **co-responsible** for creator AI disclosure compliance when using Spark Ads
- Before boosting creator content, verify the creator's AI disclosure status
- Request confirmation that all AI elements are properly labeled
- Must apply all standard TikTok ad policies to the promoted content
- Commercial content disclosure toggle must be activated

### 6.5 Tracking

- UTM parameters must be added to landing page URLs for external attribution
- TikTok's native measurement handles on-platform engagement

## 7. Common Rejection Reasons

### 7.1 Top Rejection Triggers

| Rank | Reason | Description |
|------|--------|-------------|
| 1 | **Misleading/exaggerated claims** | Overstated product performance, unsubstantiated promises |
| 2 | **Landing page issues** | Non-functional, non-mobile-friendly, missing privacy policy, missing contact info, no refund policy |
| 3 | **Prohibited product/service** | Attempting to advertise banned categories |
| 4 | **Low quality creative** | Blurry video, poor audio, pixelation, black bars, no sound |
| 5 | **Ad/landing page mismatch** | Different product, price, or promotion shown in ad vs. landing page |
| 6 | **Undisclosed AI content** | AI-generated visuals, audio, or text not properly labeled |
| 7 | **Inappropriate content** | Sexually suggestive, graphic, shocking, or violent imagery |
| 8 | **Intellectual property violation** | Using copyrighted material, trademarks, or branded content without authorization |
| 9 | **Video duration violation** | Under 5 seconds or over 10 minutes |
| 10 | **Restricted product without approval** | Advertising alcohol, financial services, dating, etc. without proper authorization |

### 7.2 Landing Page Rejection Details

Landing pages commonly fail review for these reasons:
- Website does not function properly or is down
- Not mobile-friendly (TikTok is mobile-first)
- Missing contact information (phone/email)
- Missing privacy policy
- E-commerce sites without refund/return policies
- Language mismatch between ad and landing page
- Content mismatch — promoted product not visible on page
- Requesting sensitive personal information to access content
- Dark patterns or deceptive checkout flows

### 7.3 Creative Rejection Details

Ads commonly fail creative review for:
- Low-resolution or blurry video/images
- Parts covered with black bars or pixelation
- No audio/sound in video ads
- Poor audio quality (muffled, unclear)
- Spelling or grammatical errors in captions
- Excessive capitalization, symbols, or punctuation
- Claims not supported by evidence
- Inconsistent display name vs. landing page brand

### 7.4 AI Content Rejection Details

- Completely AI-generated images, videos, or audio without disclosure
- AI showing primary subject doing something they didn't do (e.g., dancing)
- AI voice-cloned content without the "AI Disclosure" tag
- Virtual influencer making experience-based claims ("I use this product every day")
- Deepfakes of real people without consent or clear labeling
- AI-generated endorsements (person appearing to endorse product without actual consent)

## 8. Brand Safety and Suitability

### 8.1 TikTok Inventory Filter (Three Tiers)

Built into TikTok Ads Manager. Controls what content appears adjacent to your ads:

| Tier | Description | Use Case |
|------|-------------|----------|
| **Expanded Inventory** | Ads will NOT appear next to explicitly inappropriate content | Maximum reach, moderate safety |
| **Standard Inventory** | Ads appear next to content appropriate for most brands | Balanced reach and safety (recommended) |
| **Limited Inventory** | Ads appear only next to content without mature themes | Maximum safety, reduced reach |

### 8.2 Content Categories Excluded by Default

The Inventory Filter excludes adjacency to:
- Violent extremism, hateful conduct
- Illegal activities and regulated goods
- Dangerous acts and challenges
- Self-harm, suicide, and eating disorders
- Harassment and bullying
- Adult nudity and sexual activities
- Minor safety violations
- Integrity and authenticity violations
- Copyright and trademark infringement

### 8.3 Additional Brand Safety Controls

**Category Exclusion**: Allows excluding specific content categories beyond the inventory tier defaults.

**Vertical Sensitivity**: Fine-tuned controls for industry-specific content adjacency concerns.

**Video Exclusion List**: Specify individual videos your ads should never appear next to.

**Profile Feed Exclusion List**: Exclude specific creator profiles from ad adjacency.

**Brand Safety Hub**: One-stop shop in TikTok Ads Manager centralizing all first-party safety solutions.

### 8.4 Third-Party Verification Partners

**OpenSlate (DoubleVerify) — Pre-Bid Solution:**
- Analyzes content BEFORE ad placement using machine learning
- Provides Brand Safety Verification Rate, measured ad impressions, verified safe percentage
- Reports top 10 video placements
- Supported formats: Brand Premium In-Feed, Brand Auction, Reach & Frequency

**Zefr — Post-Bid Solution:**
- Scrutinizes content AFTER campaign completion
- GARM-aligned standards across 44 categories and 53 languages
- Risk designations: No risk, Low risk, Medium risk, High risk
- Supported formats: Reach & Frequency, Brand Auction

### 8.5 GARM Alignment

All TikTok brand safety solutions align with the **Global Alliance for Responsible Media (GARM) Brand Safety and Suitability Framework**, ensuring industry-standard brand protection.

## 9. Influencer/Creator Ad Rules

### 9.1 Mandatory Disclosure Requirements

**TikTok Platform Requirements:**
- All promotional content must use TikTok's built-in **Content Disclosure Setting** (toggled before publishing)
- Select "Branded Content" when promoting a third-party brand
- Select "Your Brand" when business accounts promote their own products
- Platform applies a visible "Paid partnership" label at the top of the video
- Even minimal incentives (e.g., $5 product sample) require disclosure

**Caption/Description Requirements:**
- Include "Ad" or "Paid partnership with [brand]" in the first two lines of the description (before truncation)
- Use disclosure hashtags: #ad, #sponsored, #paidpartnership
- Disclosure must be prominent and not buried

### 9.2 FTC Compliance (US)

- Clear and conspicuous disclosure of ANY material connection with a brand
- "Material connection" includes: payment, free products, discounts, affiliate relationships, employment, family connections
- Disclosure must be in the same medium as the endorsement (in the video itself, not just description)
- Must be visible and understandable to the average viewer
- Cannot rely solely on platform tools — independent disclosure also recommended

**Penalties:**
- FTC issued formal actions against 15 influencers and brands in H1 2025 (40% increase from 2024)
- Penalties range from $10,000 to $100,000+ depending on severity and repeat violations
- Both brands and creators can be held liable

### 9.3 AI-Generated Creator Content

- AI-generated endorsements are prohibited (making it appear someone endorses a product without actual consent)
- Virtual influencers must clearly identify as AI/virtual in their account bio
- Virtual influencers must apply "AI-generated" labels to every post
- Virtual influencers must use the Branded Content toggle for partnerships
- Virtual influencers CANNOT make experience-based claims ("I use this daily," "This changed my skin")
- Virtual influencers may only share factual product information

### 9.4 International Disclosure Requirements

| Region | Requirement |
|--------|-------------|

| **China** | Mandatory watermarking; creator identity disclosure required |

### 9.5 Consequences of Non-Disclosure

- Undisclosed commercial content becomes **ineligible for the For You Page (FYP)**
- Repeated non-disclosure leads to posting restrictions or account bans
- Brands using Spark Ads share responsibility for creator disclosure compliance

## 10. AI-Generated Content Rules (2026)

### 10.1 What Requires Disclosure

Mandatory AI content labels apply to:
- **Synthetic faces/persons**: AI-generated spokespeople and deepfakes
- **Voice cloning**: Cloned celebrity or creator voices
- **Photorealistic backgrounds**: AI-generated realistic scenes
- **Photorealistic product images**: AI-rendered product mockups
- Content showing a primary subject doing something they didn't actually do
- Content making a primary subject say something they didn't actually say

**Exempt from labeling:**
- TikTok's native text-to-speech feature
- Standard beauty filters
- AI-written captions and text
- Color grading and basic editing effects

### 10.2 Detection Methods

TikTok uses three detection layers:
1. **C2PA metadata scanning**: Detects embedded provenance data from tools like DALL-E, Midjourney
2. **Visual artifact detection**: 94.7% accuracy on synthetic faces — identifies unnatural hand geometry, lighting inconsistencies
3. **Audio fingerprinting**: Compares spectral patterns against known voice synthesis signatures

### 10.3 Penalty Structure

| Level | Trigger | Consequence |
|-------|---------|-------------|
| 1 | First undisclosed AI content | Retroactive labeling applied |
| 2 | 2-3 violations within 90 days | Content removal + 7-day posting ban |
| 3 | 4+ violations or deepfakes | 30-day account suspension |
| 4 | Malicious deepfakes | Permanent account termination |

**Advertisers face stricter consequences:** Frozen ad accounts and mandatory compliance reviews.

### 10.4 Absolute Bans (No Disclosure Exception)

These are prohibited regardless of disclosure:
- Political deepfakes of candidates or officials
- Non-consensual intimate imagery
- Fabricated crisis statements by authorities
- Fraud impersonations designed to extract money or data

### 10.5 Permitted with Proper Disclosure

- Satirical content with unambiguous comedic intent
- Entertainment featuring celebrities in fictional scenarios (with consent)
- Educational historical re-enactments of deceased figures
- Branded AI avatars with documented consent

### 10.6 Advertiser-Specific AI Rules

1. Ad-level disclosure required via TikTok Ads Manager's "AI Disclosure" tag
2. Landing page consistency: AI disclosures on ads must match landing page presentations
3. TikTok Shop: AI-generated product images require labels in both ads and product listings
4. Spark Ads: Brands become co-responsible for verifying creator AI disclosure compliance

### 10.7 Performance Impact

- Proactively labeled AI content performs within **5-8%** of non-labeled organic content on reach
- Unlabeled content retroactively flagged loses **35-45% reach** due to 12-48 hour distribution holds
- Properly disclosed AI avatar ads achieve **23% lower CPM** than retroactively flagged ones
- Q1 2026: TikTok removed 2.3 million videos globally for synthetic media violations (180% increase YoY)

## 13. Quick Compliance Checklist for Ad Creation

Use this checklist before submitting any TikTok ad:

### Content Compliance
- [ ] Ad does not contain prohibited content (Section 1)
- [ ] Restricted content has required approvals and age-gating (Section 2)
- [ ] No misleading, exaggerated, or unsubstantiated claims
- [ ] No before/after body transformation imagery
- [ ] No weight loss claims or weight management product promotion
- [ ] No political content of any kind
- [ ] All claims are truthful and can be substantiated

### Creative Quality
- [ ] Video is between 5 seconds and 10 minutes
- [ ] Resolution meets minimum requirements (540x960 for 9:16)
- [ ] File size under 500 MB
- [ ] Audio is clear and high quality
- [ ] No blurry, pixelated, or low-quality visuals
- [ ] No black bars covering content
- [ ] Critical content within safe zones (80px sides, 160px top, 440px bottom)
- [ ] No spelling or grammatical errors in captions
- [ ] No excessive capitalization or symbol abuse

### Landing Page
- [ ] Landing page is functional and mobile-optimized
- [ ] Product/service matches what's shown in ad
- [ ] Language consistent between ad and landing page
- [ ] Contact information present (phone/email)
- [ ] Privacy policy present (especially for lead ads)
- [ ] Refund/return policy visible (for e-commerce)
- [ ] No sensitive information requested without justification

### Targeting
- [ ] Age targeting meets industry requirements (see Section 5.4)
- [ ] Geographic targeting complies with regional restrictions
- [ ] Teen targeting uses only broad options (location, language, device)

### Disclosure
- [ ] AI-generated content properly disclosed via AI Disclosure tag
- [ ] Branded content uses Content Disclosure Setting
- [ ] FTC disclosure included (#ad, #sponsored, or Paid Partnership label)
- [ ] Required disclaimers present (alcohol, financial, health, etc.)
- [ ] Spark Ads: Creator AI disclosure verified

### Music and IP
- [ ] Music licensed through TikTok Commercial Music Library or independently licensed
- [ ] No unauthorized use of copyrighted material
- [ ] No unauthorized use of trademarks or brand logos
